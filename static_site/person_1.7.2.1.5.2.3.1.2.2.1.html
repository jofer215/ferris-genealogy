ALICE OSBORN

ID: 1.7.2.1.5.2.3.1.2.2.1 | Source: John
(1.7.2.1.5.2.3.1.2.2.1) ALICE OSBORN born and raised in NYC and died age 86y, September 30, * Highstown; attended Chapin and Brearley Schools and graduated from Vassar College, 1941; as senior class president at Vassar, she introduced Eleanor Roosevelt as her commencement speaker; she remained active in supporting her alma mater throughout her life; she married Newell Brown, Princeton ’39; an Army Lieutenant Colonel in WWI and newspaper owner and reporter, and Director of Career and Study Services at Princeton, and Assistant Secretary of Labor during the Eisenhower administration; left public office 1961 and took a job t a machine tool company in Hartford while working to earn his master’s in guidance and counseling from the U of Hartford; 1963 accepted his appointment at Princeton serving there until he retired 19980; and died April 14, 2000 Keene NH; after the war the family lived in New Hampshire, Washington, D.C., and Hartford CT before moving to Princeton in 1964; while raising her 5 kids, she taught English history at Potomac School in Washington, D.C., and later received a Master’s Degree from Rutgers U in library science; after serving as librarian at Princeton High School, she became Executive Director of the Princeton Historical Society in 1977; married 2d, also his 2d marriage, Gerald Breese born June 4, 1912 Breesport NY and died August 25, 1995 age 83y; AB Ohio Wesleyan U 1935; BD Yale Divinity School 1938; PhD from Chicago 1947; WWII Vet 1942-45 serving in England and France; 1947-49 staff member for the Social Science Research Council’s committee on housing research, Washington, D.C.; joined Princeton faculty 1949, promoted to associate professor 1952 and professor 1959; professor of sociology and urban planning at Princeton U, retiring 1977; see ‘Net for more info; born to her and Newell were:
