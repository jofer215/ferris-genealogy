THERON SAMUEL WEED

ID: 4.6.5.9.3.2.13 | Source: Mary
(4.6.5.9.3.2.13) THERON SAMUEL WEED born October 18, 1837 LeRoy, Livingston, NY and died January 16, 909 Mentor, Lake, OH; carpenter and farm hand; when young attended a military school in Michigan and afterwards enlisted in the US regular Army in February 1855 and served until September 1856 when discharged; served with General Fremont in Utah in service against the Indians; wounded in this service from which he suffered his whole life; served as a Sergeant in Company D, 14th Michigan Volunteer Infantry Regiment, December 5, 1862 to August 26, 1862 being honorably discharged Columbus OH after suffering another war wound; reenlisted with the 14th Independent Battery of Ohio Light Artillery and was discharged August 9, 1865 Camp Dennison OH – this unit served under Sherman in the taking of Atlanta; active member of GAR Garfield Post 591; married July 4, 1857 in Litchfield, Hillsdale, MI Annie Eliza Kelley [dau of Ezekiel & Elizabeth (Williams) Kelley] born March 7, 1836 Sandusky OH and died October 10, 1915 Mentor, Lake, OH; both buried there Mentor Cemetery; and born to them were: (4.6.5.9.3.2.13.1) ALICE M WEED (Allace W) born March 17, 1861 MI and died March 1, 1907 West Mentor OH; married December 25, 1878 in Kirtland OH, Matthew Dickey
