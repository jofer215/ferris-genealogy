JOHN HANFORD FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.6.3.4.3 | Source: James
(5.1.3.6.3.4.3) JOHN HANFORD FERRIS born October 22, 1842 Five Mile River [now Rowayton] CT and died April 10, 1904; educated in the public schools and at the age of 14 went to sea with his father. After following the sea for 4 years, John engaged in the wholesale grocery business with his uncle in Norwalk. He then spent six months in the West and on his return established himself in the coal business. The concern grew to large proportions and he became one of the leading men connected with the industry. He was one of the organizers of the City National Bank of South Norwalk, and served as its president until a year prior to his death. He was also the vice-president of the South Norwalk Savings Bank. For a time he was president of the Norwalk Steamboat Company, after remaining a director. He also held directorships in the Norwalk Lock Company, the Norwalk Gas Company, the Norwalk Iron Works Company, and the Manhattan Rubber Company of New York City. He was a recognized leader of the Republican Party and served a number of terms as a member of the City Board of Councilmen, and was the effectual champion of the municipal lighting plant project. For twelve years he held the office of city treasurer. In 1887, he represented South Norwalk in the State Assembly, and as a legislature made a record notable for ability and fidelity to duty; he was made chairman of the house committee on banks. Two years later he was elected to the State Senate to represent the Thirteenth District. He was president pro-tem of the Senate and was frequently called upon to act as chief executive of the Senate during the absence of the governor and lieutenant-governor. He was enrolled in the Norwalk, South Norwalk, Pine Lodge, Knob Outing, and Norwalk Yacht Clubs. He was affiliated with the St. John's Lodge, No. 6, Ancient Free and Accepted Masons of Norwalk. He was a member of the South Norwalk Congregational Church and served as the treasurer of the Sunday school. He was incorporator, first president and lifelong director of a general hospital for all the Norwalks, managed by a hospital association.
