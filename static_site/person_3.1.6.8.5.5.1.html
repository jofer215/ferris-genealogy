THOMAS HARPER MILBURN

ID: 3.1.6.8.5.5.1 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.6.8.5.5.1) THOMAS HARPER MILBURN born March 11, 1836 Easton’s Corners, Wolford Township, Grenville, Ontario and died May 28, 1910 Pittsburgh, Allegheny, PA; moved from Canada to Mishawaka IN Fall of 1854 where he became a salesman, bookkeeper and partner in the general merchandise business; sold out in 1860 and moved to Lincoln IL where he was a partner in Niles & Milburn, a hardware business; vice president and manager of the agencies and sales department of Milburn Wagon Co until March 1872; April 1873 moved to Memphis TN where he was a member of Milburn & Walker Co and became president of the Fourth National Bank of Memphis; in 1879 he purchased controlling interest in the German National Bank, closed Fourth National and did all business from German Bank until May 1887 when he sold all interests in the bank; 1883 became treasurer of Milburn Gin and Machine Works; well-known in the Memphis Cotton Exchange for 30y; married September 27, 1858 in Mishawaka, Mary E Luce born 1838 NY and died November 6, 1890 Memphis; both buried Memphis TN; and born to them were:
