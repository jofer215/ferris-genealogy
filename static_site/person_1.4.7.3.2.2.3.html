ADELE DUANE PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.2.2.3 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.2.2.3) ADELE DUANE PELL (1838-) married December 22, 1863 John Busteed Ireland (Photos) [son of John Lawrence & Mary Gelston (Floyd) Ireland] born September 6, 1823 Watkins Glen, Schulyer, NY and died October 11, 1913 NYC as one of the oldest members of the Union Club; graduated from New York U and after three years study was admitted to the bar in Albany, 1845; in 1851, he made a six years tour of the world and wrote a book titled, “Wall Street to Cashmere”; he inherited wealth from his father and devoted his time to managing his big real estate interests; admitted to the Union League Club in 1859 and was also a member of the Sons of the Revolution; he purchased several parcels of land to keep the Pell Farm intact for his wife, who was the only heir of Robert & Maria Pell. He was active in the development of Greenwich Village to the north of Abington Square; a long time resident of a property he owned on the south side of Washington Square Park which is now a part of NYU.
