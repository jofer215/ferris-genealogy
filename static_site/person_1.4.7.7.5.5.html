LOUIS LIVINGSTON SEAMAN, M.D.,

ID: 1.4.7.7.5.5 | Source: John
(1.4.7.7.5.5) LOUIS LIVINGSTON SEAMAN, M.D., born October 17, 1851 Newburgh NY; graduated with the following degrees: A.B., Cornell College, 1872; M.D., Jefferson Medical College, Philadelphia PA, with gold medal, 1876; New York U., 1877, LL.B., N.Y.U., 1884; LL.D., Lincoln U. Served as Major-Surgeon, 1st Regular U.S. Volunteers, Engineers, Spanish-American War; 17th U.S. Infantry, Phillipines, 18991900; in Boxer Campaign in China, 1900; with 2d Japanese Army at front in Manchuria, 1905-05; authored From Tokio through Manchuria with the Japanese, 1904; was first American volunteer officer in WWI, leaving NY, August 7, 1914 for Belgian front; decorated by Belgium, Japan, France, Romania, China and other countries; married 1st February 12, 1889 Fanny Blackstone [dau of John P. & Fanny (Freeman) Blackstone] born October 31, 1861 and died April 21, 1895; married 2d Mary Stuart Huntington [dau of John M.]. Mary is reported to have been the first American woman to volunteer for service in the WWI, entering at the beginning of the conflict, and doing field hosptial work under the personal direction of the Queen of the Belgians. no issue. Barton Hall serves as the home of Cornell’s ROTC units – in the main stairwell of Barton Hall are 2 plaques which recall Cornellians who served admirably in the US Army. One is dedicated to Frank A. Barton and the other is dedicated to Louis Livingston Seaman, the president of the Class of 1872 and a Major in the US Army during WWI. (Photo)

No children recorded
