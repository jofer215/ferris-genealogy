SAMUEL PECK

ID: 3.10.1.3 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.3) SAMUEL PECK III (GJ59-J3) born April 1720 Greenwich and died there January 29, 1793; a Private in Captain George Peck’s Company of Colonel John Mead’s Regiment from August 13 to September 2, 1776 at New York; again in same Company from November 1, 1776 to January 8, 1777 as a guard at Greenwich. He was the Deacon of the First Congregational Church of Greenwich for many years; a man of great energy and decision of character, a valuable citizen. Both Mary and Samuel were buried in the Tomac Avenue Cemetery (Old Sound Beach) Old Greenwich (tombstone photos); his tombstone is engraved: “Departed this life Jan. 29, 1793, age 72 years and 9 months. He was a faithful man and feared God above many.” He married, November 7, 1745, Mary FERRIS (5.1.1.1) and born to them were:
