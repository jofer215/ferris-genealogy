THOMAS THORNE CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.10.3 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.10.3) THOMAS THORNE CHADEAYNE (Photo) born October 4, 1854 at the “Just a Mere” farm, Yorktown NY and died January 31, 1940, Ossining NY; buried Fair Ridge Cemetery, Chappaqua NY; married April 15, 1891 in Westchester Co, Harriet E. Young born October 17, 1865 and died February 22, 1938 Ossining NY; buried Fair Ridge Cemetery, Chappaqua NY; 1900 Thomas was a merchant residing at 164 Main St, Ossining, NY. Obit: T. T. Chadeayne Dies at Home. Was Former President of Water Board. Thomas Thorne Chadeayne, eighty-six, of 33 Linden Avenue, who served on the Ossining Board of Water Commissioners for 21 years, died early this morning at his residence after a long illness. Mr. Chadeayne retired from the Water Board in 1933 after serving as president since 1928 and as a member since 1912. He also served for many years on the Board of Deacons of the First Presbyterian Church of Ossining and was an exempt member of the Ossining Steamer Company. During Mr. Chadeayne's terms as president of the Water Board, the system was enlarged with a $100,000 project, which included the installation of the filter and ozonation plant at the reservoir. One of the best known of the old residents of Ossining and vicinity, Mr. Chadeayne born Oct, 4, 1854 on the old Chadeayne farm near the Kitchawan Railroad Station in the Town of Yorktown. He was the son of Leonard and Mary Ann Thorne Chadeayne. He received his education in the schools of Yorktown Township and later took courses at the Hiram Post School, Daniel Ryder's School, and Odell's Business College. In February of 1895, he opened a crockery and house-furnishing store on Main Street, Ossining in a store, which was later, sold to Griffin S. Hilliker, who made it part of his building. Later he transferred to the site formerly occupied by Ernest H. Cowles. Soon after the World War, Mr. Chadeayne retired. Born to them was:
