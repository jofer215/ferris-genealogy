EZRA LOCKWOOD

ID: 4.6.1.5.7 | Source: Mary
(4.6.1.5.7) EZRA LOCKWOOD (#694) (Judge) born June 27, 1777 Pound Ridge NY and died May 29, 1853. He resided his entire life at Pound Ridge, occupying a residence directly opposite the old homestead where he born. For some years he taught a private school at Pound Ridge. He was a Supervisor on the Board of Supervisors, 1807-19; represented Westchester County in the State Legislature, 1806; Justice of the Peace; Surrogate of Westchester County NY, 1808-10 and 1811-13; and was appointed by His Excellency Martin Van Buren, then Governor of the State of New York, Judge of the Court of Common Pleas for said County. He was a man of studious habits and well educated, but he lacked the elements of popularity, which his brother, Horatio, possessed. Ezra was an ardent Whig and at times party spirit ran so high that the two brothers would not speak, although they lived opposite each other.
