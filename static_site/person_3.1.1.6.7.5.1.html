LANCELOT CHAPMAN EWBANK

ID: 3.1.1.6.7.5.1 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.1.6.7.5.1) LANCELOT CHAPMAN EWBANK born July 20, 1837 Dearborn Co IN and died July 21, 1920 Kingman, Fountain, IN; September 1861 he enlisted in Company I, 31 st Indiana Regiment and took part in the siege of Cornith and the Battles of Stone River and Chickamauga; in the latter he received a wound to the right shoulder and breast from a shell; this necessitated his going to a hospital and after a while he was placed in control of the same; returning to his regiment, he ther remained until, on account of an abscess on his thigh, he had to return to the hospital, where he remained until discharged at Indianapolis 1864; his common school education was supplemented by a course of study at Georgetown IL where he prepared himself for a teacher’s duties and taught 7 terms in Parke Co; he purchased 80acres in Parke Co IN in 1865 and by 1893 it had grown to 284acres; served as Justice of the Peace for 12y and also was a Notary Public; he also worked at carpentering for a short time, after he which he settled down to the peaceful vocation of a farmer; married March 8, 1865 in Parke Co, Mary Ratcliff [dau of John & Mary (Clark) Ratcliff] born May 17, 1839 Parke Co and died September 30, 1923 Covington, Fountain, IN; both buried Cashatt Cemetery, Parke Co IN; several of their children attended Union Christian College in Sullivan Co IN; and born to them were:
