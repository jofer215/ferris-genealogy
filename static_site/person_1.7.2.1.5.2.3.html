SAMUEL BRADHURST SCHIEFFELIN

ID: 1.7.2.1.5.2.3 | Source: John
(1.7.2.1.5.2.3) SAMUEL BRADHURST SCHIEFFELIN born February 18/24, 1811 NYC and died September 13, 1901; educated in private schools and early turned his attention to business, but contributed largely to the religious press; his works include Message to Ruling Elders, their Office and their Duties, 1859; The Foundations of History: a Series of First Things, 1863; and numerous others; he was a signatory on a letter to the Governor of New York asking that a brigade of Colored Volunteers be raised, 1863 (see ‘Net); interested in political affairs and connected himself with the People’s Party, whose platform was the product of his pen; he believed that no republic can exist where wealth is allowed to accumulate in the hands of a small minority and favored laws limiting inheritance (bet that made his family happy!); 1883 nominated by his party for state senator from the 10th District of NYC, but failed to win; married Lucretia Hazard and born to them were:
