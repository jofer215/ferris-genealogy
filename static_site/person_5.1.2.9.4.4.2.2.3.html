ARTHUR TOMPKINS

ID: 5.1.2.9.4.4.2.2.3 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.4.4.2.2.3) ARTHUR TOMPKINS born 1879 Carmel NY and died December 28, 1946 Peekskill NY; married Florence Mason [daughter of Perry & Rachel (Underwood)] Obituary, Star, Peekskill, NY, Monday, Dec. 30, 1946: Arthur Tompkins, retired steamfitter from Standard Brands, Inc., who served for many years as a conductor for the Peekskill Lighting and Railroad Company, died suddenly of a heart attack Saturday morning at his home-225 Depew Street. Saturday morning the sidewalks and paths were covered with ice and he went out the back door to take a look at the icy condition. Mrs. Tompkins heard a thud and went out to investigate and found that her husband had collapsed. Dr. W. H. Sweet Sr., the family physician, was called and said that Mr. Tompkins had diedstantly. He had suffered a heart attack about five years ago, but made a good recovery and returned to his work with Standard Brands, Inc. He continued until Sept. 1, 1945, when he was retired. Although born in Carmel, N.Y., son of Joshua and Sarah Tompkins, he didn't remain long in that village. The family moved to Tompkins Corners when he was real young. Later he came to Peekskill where he was employed by the Company where he befriended many by his courteous and friendly acts. During World War I when there was a real shortage of skilled workers at the trades, he was induced to leave the trolley position and learn the steamfitters trade. He went to work at Standard \Brands, Inc., and remained there for 38 years. He was a life member of the Red Men and also a member of the Steamfitters Union. Surviving are his wife, the former Florence Mason, of Peekskill; and one daughter, Mrs. Herman (Gertrude) Link, of Roe Park, and several brothers and sisters. Interment will be in Hillside Cemetery. Obituary, Star, Peekskill, NY, Wednesday, Oct. 30, 1957: Mrs. Florence Mason Tompkins, a lifelong resident of this city, died yesterday morning at her home, 850 Constant Avenue. She born in Peekskill the daughter of Perry and Rachel Underwood Mason. Her husband was the late Arthur Tompkins of Depew Street, and at one time a motorman for the Peekskill Lighting and Railroad Company.
