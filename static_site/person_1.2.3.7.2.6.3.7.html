CHARLES LEWIS COLE

ID: 1.2.3.7.2.6.3.7 | Source: John
(1.2.3.7.2.6.3.7) CHARLES LEWIS COLE born March 21, 1842 Catlin NY and died c1912; married December 13, 1865 at Horseheads NY, Sarah Almira Walkin born April 22, 1842 Oak Hill NY [dau of Charles Hyde and Betsey (Hunting) Walkin]; Charles enlisted at Elmira NY July 1862 in the 107th New York Infantry, arriving in Washington, D. C., August 15; he was in the battles of Antietam, Chancellorsville, and Gettysburg. In September, 1863, his regiment was sent West and made a part of the 20th Army Corps, under General Joe Hooker. The regiment was in the battle of Resaca and Gainesville GA, May, 1864. Charles also took part in engagements at New Hope Church, May 25, 1864; Pine Knob GA, June 15; Cuffs Farm, June 22; Kemminson, June 27; Peachtree Creek, July 20; and was at the siege of Atlanta, July 23 to August 24; and was in the March to the Sear, November 15 to December 10, 1864. At Rockingham NC, on March 8, 1865, he was shot through the left breast, taken prisoner and left to die in an old log house at Browning Creek, about 12 miles from Rockingham. His shoes and clothing were taken from him and he was unconscious for over 36 hours, during which time he was nursed and cared for by two women who lived in the hut. During this time he was unattended by a physician. He remained there until the latter part of May when he walked, with the help of a Negro, to Wilmington, then to Newburn, Washington, and Elmira. He was discharged in June, 1865. Born to him and Sarah were:
