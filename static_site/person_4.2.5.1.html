SAMUEL LOCKWOOD,

ID: 4.2.5.1 | Source: Mary
(4.2.5.1) SAMUEL LOCKWOOD, Jr (#180) (Captain) born November 20, 1737 Greenwich CT and died August 26, 1807, drowned near Greenwich Point, while fishing. He was a Captain of a Revolutionary War gun boat and also commanded land forces. The firmness of the indefatigable Captain Samuel Lockwood (by whose vigilance both by night and day, with forty men and a twelve pounder, in a gondola, eleven armed vessels with General Prescott, one hundred and thirty officers and soldiers, and one hundred and thirty seaman were taken at Sorel) has not only crowned them with honor as soldiers, but entitles them to the applause of their bleeding country and among those taken prisoners, December 31, 1775, and not exchanged on May 18, 1776 is Captain Samuel Lockwood. On October 5, 1787, he was one of a committee to see about building a bridge.
