JOHN FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.1.7.2 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.7.2) JOHN FERRIS (Photos) born May 19, 1782, New York City and died January 18, 1870, Newbury, Geauga, OH age 87y 8m 1d; John had no education, but he must have inherited a bright mind and superior intellect; was a great humorist and practical joker; a shoemaker by trade and a successful farmer and an expert at the violin; married December 29, 1804 Hannah Black who taught John to read; born April 15, 1787 Springfield MA and died February 24, 1875; both buried Newbury Center Cemetery, Geauga Co OH. Under the skillful direction of her educated, Puritan parents and experienced teachers, she secured a fine education. After they married, they settled in Phelps, Ontario, NY and then 1816 moved to Eaton, Madison Co where they lived until 1823 when they moved back to Phelps. In 1832, with a family of ten children, he joined the New England colony, which had for many years been moving west to settle the Ohio Western Reserve. He first moved to Richmond, near Panesville, then to Russel, west of Chardon, and finally located in Newbury, where he remained until he died. At Newbury, he purchased a tract of land and erected upon it about 1835, the ‘old Ferris homestead’. Born to them were:
