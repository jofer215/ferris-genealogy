HANNAH FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.10 | Source: James
(5.1.3.10) HANNAH FERRIS born September 6, 1751 Greenwich CT and died there April 3, 1823 [age 72]; buried with her husband; married at Greenwich, Captain Henry Waring [son of Michael & Elizabeth (Scofield) Waring] born October 6, 1744 Stamford/Greenwich CT and died November 6, 1830 Greenwich; buried Tomac Avenue Cemetery, (Old Sound Beach) Old Greenwich. He was a shoemaker and was a Private in Captain Samuel Keeler's Company, Bradley's Battalion, Wadsworth Brigade of Connecticut State Troops, serving from June 16, 1776. He was taken prisoner at Fort Washington. He was commissioned a 1st Lieutenant on January 1, 1777 in Colonel Lamb's 2nd Regiment of Artillery and was made a Captain, September 26, 1779 in Coast Guards. He resigned April 14, 1781.
