LUCIUS MERVIN DARBY

ID: 3.10.1.2.1.9.4.5.1.10 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.1.9.4.5.1.10) LUCIUS MERVIN DARBY born December 2, 1906 Clarksville IA and died November 15, 1999 Waverly IA; married February 1, 1941 in Clarksville, Olive Rebecca Loucks born September 26, 1912 in Anoka MN. WATERLOO COURIER OBITUARY: “Mervin & Olive live in the rebuilt schoolhouse where Mervin's mother taught school & meet Mervin's father on the way home or to school while he was doing his farmwork. It is about two miles north of their home farm. The intersection is just the country roads crossing each other. Going east of their house, is the Wygle farm where Raymond Darby remember living , then farther east is Leland & Marian's farm, (where Allan Whiteside grew up), south goes past Mervin & Olives farm, and then farther south dead ends on the Clarksville road, the Isaac Scott farm driveway is right off that dead end and a little farther south to the buildings. Going west from Mervin & Olive's place is Highway # 14 that runs into Greene to the right and into Allison to the left. Most of the roads are blacktopped now, whereas Raymond Darby remember when on the Wygle farm, none of the "side roads" were hard surfaced, they were all crushed rock & gravel.” “Mervin Darby, 92, of Clarksville, died Monday, Nov. 15, at Waverly Municipal Hospital, Waverly, of natural causes; survivors include his wife, Olive; two sons, Jim of Spooner, Wis., and Steve of Boone; eight grandchildren and six great-grandchildren; and two sisters, Irene Oakley and Lucille Froberg, both of Rockford, Ill.; he was preceded in death by a son, Richard; three brothers; and five sisters; services will be 1:30 p.m. Thursday at Hauser Funeral Home, Charles City, with burial in Lynwood Cemetery, Clarksville; friends may call from 3 to 9 p.m. today at the funeral home, where there will be a 7 p.m. prayer service; visitation also from 9 a.m. until services Thursday; Mr. Darby farmed until his retirement.” FROM LUTHER LOUCKS "HISTORY OF THE KEILLORS, CRANDALLS, HUNTS, & LOUCKS" "Olive was always pretty, gracious and industrious. She was well liked by all that knew her and was a true friend. Her younger sister Mary Lou was a constant companion, as they became a little older. Olive took music lessons at McPhail School of Music and got her teaching degree, which she was able to use to good advantage as she taught many students over a period of many years. After finishing High School at Central High School in Minneapolis and Business College, she got a job at "Butler Brothers", a wholesale furniture house in Minneapolis. In 1941 Olive was married to Mervin Darby who had purchased the Darby Farm from his fathers' estate. Olive did well at the many duties of a good and faithful farm wife. She became the mother of two bright children, 8 grandchildren and 6 great-grandchildren. Olive and Mervin have always been most hospitable and continue to be so in their "retirement" home, which is close to their farm, which they still own and lease out. They both have been such a help in the local meeting there, and helped organize the annual Bible Conference starting in 1949. It continues on as the largest Bible conference in the Fellowship. "

No children recorded
