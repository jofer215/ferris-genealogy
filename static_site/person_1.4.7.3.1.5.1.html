JOHN HOWLAND PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.1.5.1 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.1.5.1) JOHN HOWLAND PELL born December 23, 1830 (1831) NY and died October 6, 1882, Yonkers NY. At the beginning of the Civil War he enlisted in Duryea’s Zouraves and was stationed at Fort Schuyler NY; commissioned ensign in the Fourth Regiment, New York State Volunteers July 6, 1861. When a Lieutenant he was honorably mentioned for gallant conduct at the Battle of Antietam. He was commissioned, October 14, 1862, Captain of Company K., Fourth Regiment. Subsequently he was compelled to resign from the service on account of physical disability brought on by the hardships of the campaigns in which he participated. He married, first, in 1860, Cornelia Corse, of Flushing, L.I., who died May 13, 1864; and second, April 20, 1870, Caroline E. Hyatt [dau of Stephen] who died January 11, 1911. Caroline was a lineal descendant of Abraham Hyatt, a lieutenant in the Second New York Regiment during the Rev. War. He was a member of the old firm of Pell & Company, Pearl Street, NYC.
