ALFRED SCOFIELD

ID: 5.2.3.2.5.2 | Source: James
(5.2.3.2.5.2) ALFRED SCOFIELD (Deacon) born May 28, 1805 Stamford and died there February 14, 1877; married January 19, 1837 in Stamford, Anna Maria Keeler [dau of Walter & Hannah (Waring) Keeler] born January 14, 1811 Bedford NY and died September 8, 1879 Stamford; both buried there Woodland Cemetery; and born to them were: (5.2.3.2.5.2.1) WALTER KEELER SCOFIELD (Photos) born April 28, 1839 Stamford and died there August 5, 1910; Civil War Veteran – served in the Navy beginning in 1861 as assistant surgeon on the US gunboat Sagamore, interrupting his studies at Yale Medical School; the Sagamore was on blockade duty in Floriad waters and participated in raids along the Florida coast; in 1864 he was assigned to the steam Union; for many months his skill as a surgeon was employed in caring for the sick and wounded in various hospitals in New Orleans, Key West and Pensacola; by the end of the War he was on the staff of the naval hospital in Norfolk VA; remaining the Navy he was allowed to complete his medical studies at Columbia U; in the years following the War, Dr. Scofield was stationed in China, Japan, South America, Africa and Europe; he was a guest at the palace of the Czar in St. Petersburg as a member of the Fox Commission in 1877 to thank the Russian people for their assistance to the Union; married January 14, 1876 Mary Eloise Candee [dau of Julius Alonzo & Caroline (Hanks) Candee] born 1849 NYC and died August 1, 1891 Stamford; both buried there Woodland Cemetery; Walter was a quiet, cultured man with an extensive knowledge of world affairs; spoke six languages fluently; reached the highest rank a surgeon can attain in the Navy, that of Rear Admiral; retired in 1901 and spent his summers in Stamford; winters in Florida and Cuba; and born to them were:
