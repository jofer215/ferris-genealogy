CAMPBELL K. PECK

ID: 3.10.1.2.6.3.3 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.6.3.3) CAMPBELL K. PECK (Campbell Kennedy Peck) born April 8, 1831 Watervliet NY and was living in Keokuk IA 1875, and died there December 3, 1879; President of Northwest Transport Co, trading with Indians for furs; Fort Peck was named for this guy; it was established as a fur trading post for Durfee & Peck (brothers-in-law) in 1866-67 near the mouth of the Milk River; from 1873 until 1879 the government used a portion of the fort as the Fort Peck Indian Agency; the site of Fort Peck was abandoned in 1879 and is now under the waters of Fort Peck Reservior in Montana was named for him; married 1856 Helen Augusta Higbie (Higbee) [dau of Nathan & Gulielma (Wing) Higbie] born April 1836 Penfield and died 1905 Keokuk IA; and born to them were:

Children (2)

  • HELEN PECK 3.10.1.2.6.3.3.1
  • CADY KENNEDY PECK 3.10.1.2.6.3.3.2
