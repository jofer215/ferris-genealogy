DANIEL VAIL FERRIS

ID: 3.1.2.3.6.10.12 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.2.3.6.10.12) DANIEL VAIL FERRIS born February 4, 1838 Scott, Cortlandt, NY; educated mainly Allegany Co, where he attended the Friendship Academy under Prof. Prosper Miller and where in 1856-57, he learned telegraphing; 1861 came to Syracuse and the same year enlisted in the U.S. Military Telegraph Corps, being stationed at General Blenker’s HQS at Hunters Chapel, whence in the spring of 1862 he went to Fairfax Station. While repairing wires between Bull Run and Manassas Junction, he was struck by lightning. He returned to Syracuse in 1863 and became associated with the Western Union Telegraph Company, becoming chief operator 1869; married May 3, 1865 Della Azelea Lyons (dau of William] of Syracuse; and born to them was a daughter who married a W. W. Wilson, of Buffalo

No children recorded
