ELEANOR BLASDEL

ID: 3.1.1.6.7.1.6 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.1.6.7.1.6) ELEANOR BLASDEL (Eleanor Chase Blasdel) born June 20, 1838 Stringtown, Ripley, IN and died March 27, 1920 Farmer City, DeWitt, IL; married December 20, 1858 Jacob Blasdel Chase [son of Leonard & Sarah B. (Blasdel) Chase] born April 5, 1836 Lawrenceburg IN and died September 30, 1898 Kirksville, Adair, MO; both buried Blue Ridge Cemetery (there are 3 in MO?); Obit, April 2, 1920: “Last Saturday morning at 8 o’clock the golden cord which bound “Grandma” Eleanor Chase to this life was loosed and her soul took flight to that brighter and better world toward which her kindly eyes had so long been turned. She was stricken with a general physical breakdown at the time her loved granddaughter, Mrs W. S. Mitchell, was taken to the hospital after she had freely spent her strength in caring for the patient at home, and less than a week after the granddaughter succumbed to her operation the vital spark of the grandmother was quenched. She was conscious only at short intervals during the sickness, but in these brief moments she was able to communicate with her son, Enoch, who had come home from Oklahoma, her brother, B. F. Blasdel, who was here from Catlin, her daughters, Mrs. Stella Kirk of Lyons IN and Mrs Ella J Melton of Bellflower, and the members of the household. The funeral was conducted in the Christian church on Tuesday afternoon by the pastor, rev J Ralph Roberts, and interment was made in the Blue Ridge cemetery by the side of the husband, her dearly loved granddaughter, Mrs. Mitchell, and other relatives who had gone before. Eleanor Blasdel was born near Milan IN June 20, 1838 and died March 27, 1920, age 81 years, 9 months 7days. She was married to Jacob B Chase on December 20, 1858, who preceded her in death September 30, 1898. To them were born ten children, of whom three daughters and one son survive her – Mrs Kirk of Lyons IN, Mrs Melton of Bellflower, Mrs Edna Myers of Farmer City and Enoch Chase of Tonkawa, OK. Two brothers, B F Blasdel of Catlin and Thomas N Blasdel of Milan IN, also survive. Mrs. Chase was not only the mother of ten children, but the grandmother of 32 grandchildren, of whom 27 survive her, and great-grandmother of 26. She was a tower of strength and Christian power in the rearing and guiding of her descendants and the only mother Olive Mitchell, who was buried Wednesday of last week, ever knew. Unit the beginning of her last illness she was able to get about among her friends and take a part in the management of the household. She was granted by the Great Maker her earnest desire that she be not permitted to live an invalid, though she knew none could be in better hands and receive better care than her daughter, Mrs. Myers, and her cousin Dr. J. A. Nowlin, could give. She was a member of the Farmer City Church of Christ for 20 years.”; and born to them were:
