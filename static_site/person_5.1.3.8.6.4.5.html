THEODORE ERNEST FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.8.6.4.5 | Source: James
(5.1.3.8.6.4.5) THEODORE ERNEST FERRIS (Photograph) born August 17, 1872 Stamford CT and died May 30, 1953 New York City; 1890 he became associated with A. Cary Smith, noted ship designer and was advanced to partnership in 1902. At the death of Mr. Smith, 1911, T. Ernest took over the business, retaining the name of A. Cary Smith and Ferris. He was a manager of the marine department of Joseph Thomas & Sons, Baltimore MD. He was chief ship designer with the Emergency Fleet Corp., of the U. S. Shipping Board in WWI and was honorary Vice President of the Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers. In 1924, he had the distinction of designing more ships than any other professional naval architect and marine designer in the world. [Jim - I am in the Shenandoah, designed 1902 by T. E. Ferris possession of a pamphlet entitled: Discussion on Paper Design of American Super Liners prepared and read by Theodore E. Ferris, Naval Architect & Marine Engineer, at the annual meeting of the Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers, together with Mr. Ferris replies to the various discussions. November 1931. - interesting reading]; married August 25, 1912 Lois Davis Blake [dau of James E.] of New Bedford; Brooklyn Eagle, April 10, 1902: The three-masted schooner yacht, Shenandoah, the first vessel of that class which has yet been built, will be launched on Saturday morning, at Shooters Island, from the yards of the builders of Emperor William’s schooner yacht, Metcor III. The Shenandoah has been built to the order of Gibson Comstock of Newport, R. I., a member of the New York Yacht Club. It is said that the Shenandoah has been entered for the races during the Kiel regatta and it is expected that she will beat the Kaiser’s American-built yacht. The Shenandoah on the water line measures 100 feet with a beam of 16 feet. Over all her length is 110 feet and her beam 27 feet. Theodore E Ferris is the designer. and born to them were:
