SARAH JANE ARMSTRONG

ID: 5.1.2.9.6.2.5 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.6.2.5) SARAH JANE ARMSTRONG born December 22, 1846 Putnam Valley and died October 18, 1946 in her sleep in the home she entered as a bride, 2 months short of her 100th birthday; married Rueben Perry [son of Rueben & Lucinda (Pratt)] born June 2, 1840 (1849) near Oscawana Lake, Putnam Valley and died there October 31, 1908 at his home in Peekskill Hollow near Bryant Pond road in Tompkins Corners; both buried Hillside Cemetery, Peekskill. Rueben was educated in the district schools and was always a farmer. Sarah, for many years tended her flower gardens, assisted with the dairy farm business and completed hundreds of hooked rugs which she gave to friends and relatives and offered to church fairs for fund-raising purposes, lived a quiet life in Putnam Valley. When not out-of-doors in her garden and after tiring of rug making, she spent a great deal of time with books and reading matter of any description. She was a constant reader of the Courier, read daily the New York Times and the World-Telegram. Only once did she ever leave the farm where she spent nearly all the days of her married and widowed life; she and a niece went to California in 1928. She was affiliated with the Tompkins Corner Methodist Church. Born to them were:
