ARD REYNOLDS

ID: 4.5.2.1.5.2 | Source: Mary
(4.5.2.1.5.2) ARD REYNOLDS (his mother was Deborah) born March 20, 1781 about a mile south of Stanwich CT and died April 26, 1857; married December 13, 1810 Ann Eliza Doell/Duell born in Germany and settled in Albany NY at age 9 and died February 25, 1858. Both buried in a private cemetery on the ‘home farm’. In politics, he was a Whig and as such was one of the leading men of his town. He held various town offices such as magistrate and selectman, and was known as ‘Squire Reynolds’. He represented his town in the State Legislature, and took an active part while there. They were members of the Methodist Episcopal Church of Stanwich and he was one of the leading men of same. His home was always open to ministers and many there were who found it a welcome place. Born to them were: (4.5.2.1.5.2.1) ELIZABETH PHILIPINA REYNOLDS born 1812 CT and died c1880 Joliet IL; married August 28, 1838 Rev Warner Hoyt (1810-1844); and born to them were:
