JOHN FERRIS JR.

ID: 1.6 | Source: John
(1.6) JOHN FERRIS JR. (probably first born of John) born c1661 (birth dates conflict considering the purported birth date of his mother) Westchester NY and died there November 1729. This John was referred to as John Jr. in the 1682 record. John Jr. took the oath of allegiance to the King of England in 1698. John married, c1714, rather late in life, Elizabeth Clarke born circa 1700 (based on these dates, Elizabeth would have been 14) and died before 1742 [dau of Daniel and his second wife Hannah (Underhill) Barnes]. Will: In the name of God, Amen. October 19, 1729. I, JOHN FERRIS, of the Borough Town of Westcheter, Esq., being sick. My executors are to sell all my estate and pay all debts. I leave to my wife Elizabeth, oe100. Whereas my wife is now with child, if it be a boy he shall have oe100, to be put in interest, and the interest to be paid to my wife, in consideration of bring him up. If it be a girl, she is to have oe40. I leave all the rest to my son, John, and my executors are to pay for his bringing up and schooling. I make my brother, James Ferris, and my brother-in-law Edward Burling, executors. Witnesses, William Forster, Henry Gillam, John Smith, Proved, November 15, 1729. After John’s death Elizabeth remarried, 1736, John Griffin. (Jim – in previous editions, I listed the wrong John as 1.6.1 – Chaplain Ferris’ Volume 1 was dated in 1980 and in 1984, based on his and Gail Moore’s research, it was determined that the John Ferris who was formerly known as the “Griffin Fragment” was the son of John and Elizabeth (Clarke) Ferris and was the correct (1.6.1), so that correction has been made. Gail called my attention to this error and provided the documentation – thanks Gail!) (For informational purposes, I have included the “other” John twig at the end of this chapter.)
