FERRIS J. STEPHENS

ID: 5.1.3.4.10.8.2.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.4.10.8.2.1) FERRIS J. STEPHENS (Photograph) born December 26, 1893 Fayette Co IN and died October 31, 1969 New Haven CT; buried Montowese Cemetery, North Haven, New Haven, CT; received his preliminary education at the public schools in Connersville IN and he played the cornet in what is considered the first high school band in American. He was sent to Sunday school in town and it was there he met Beulah, who played the organ in her father's church. He was graduated with B.A., 1915, Butler College (later Butler U.) and B. D., 1923 and Ph.D., 1925 at Yale University. Meanwhile he was minister of the Disciples of Christ Church, Morristown IN, 1916-20 and of the Montowese Baptist Church, North Haven CT, 1920-24 and to support his family, as worked summers at the A. C. Gilbert toy factory, as a carpenter's assistant, laying bricks, etc. Upon obtaining his Ph. D., he became professor Old Testament and ancient history at Culver-Stockton College (later U.), Canton MO, a post he held until 1928, when he joined the faculty of Yale, where he was assistant professor of Assyriology until 1940 and associate professor until 1962, when he retired as associate professor emeritus. During 1933-36 he was also acting chairman of the department of Semitic languages; additional he served as assistant curator of the Babylonian Collection in Yale's Sterling Memorial Library, 1931-33; as acting curator, 1933-36, and as curator, 1936-62. He also reviewed books on his specialty for various technical magazines. He was a member of the American Council of Learned Societies and the Society of Biblical Literature and Exegesis; the American Oriental Society of which, in 1939, he became the SecretaryTreasure and remained in that office for 30 years. Ferris married, June 21, 1916, Beulah Butler Burkhardt born May 15, 1894, Indianapolis IN and died April 18, 1974, New Haven CT. Born to them were:
