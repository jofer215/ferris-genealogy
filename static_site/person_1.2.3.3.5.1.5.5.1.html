GENTRY GOHLMAN

ID: 1.2.3.3.5.1.5.5.1 | Source: John
(1.2.3.3.5.1.5.5.1) GENTRY GOHLMAN (1.2.3.3.5.1.5.6) LEWIS DENNIS (Louis E) born August 6, 1845/47 Long Point, Norfolk Co, Ontario and died April 25, 1937 Ashland, Jackson, OR; in 1873, Louis left Deep Creek IA, on a 11 month exploratory trip by boat to the Pacific Coast; after exploring northern California, and also making a water trip to Portland OR, he returned home with reports of promising opportunities near Big Springs, not far from Yreka CA; in 1874, at least 4 couples (Nathaniel & Hannah, Louis & Caroline, Jim Lauderbaugh and Cindy (Dennis), and John Edmonds & Martha (Dennis)) and their children left for California, traveling in freight train box cars with camping gear, household goods, tools, food, etc. When the train stopped at sidings, at times up to 4h, they built open fires and baked bread in a dutch oven, and also washed clothes, water was carried in a 50-gal vinegar barrel; married September 14, 1867 in Fairfield IA, Caroline Jones born April 24, 1848 and died July 1914; and born to them were:

No children recorded
