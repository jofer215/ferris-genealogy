ELIJAH FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.1.7.2.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.7.2.1) ELIJAH FERRIS born January 30, 1806 Phelps, Ontario, NY and died April 17, 1856 Montcalm Co MI; buried Ferris Center Cemetery, Ferris Township, Montcalm Co MI (Photos); married 1st January 6, 1832 Jane Russell born September 24, 1814 NH and died May 2, 1848; buried Newbury Center Cemetery, Geauga Co OH; and 2d August 16, 1849 Esther Gillett born Kirtland, Lake, OH and died July 25, 1851; and 3d August 17, 1854 Eliza Bump (Parks) born May 17, 1823 Burton, Geauga, OH. Elijah went to Michigan in the early 1850s, cut his way through the vast forest, then a howling wilderness and settled in Montcalm County, where the town of Ferris [named after him] is now located; first settler. From Doug Ferris: “As he was cutting his way through the forest in Montcalm Co to establish this town, he stumbled across Nathaniel Ferris (yet unidentified), his mother and 230 other folks in Fairplain, Montcalm Co; Fairplain was obviously the jump off point as Montcalm property was put on sale in 1849; Elijah’s first son, Albert, and my Nathaniel bought land and helped name the town of Sidney in Montcalm; they both bought land in the same section; Nathaniel also named his first son Albert; these people ending up in the wilderness and not knowing each other is a hard stretch.” Born to Elijah and Jane was:
