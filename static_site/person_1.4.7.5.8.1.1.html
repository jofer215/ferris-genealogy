MORTIMER YALE FERRIS

ID: 1.4.7.5.8.1.1 | Source: John
(1.4.7.5.8.1.1) MORTIMER YALE FERRIS born March 29, 1881 Brookline and died March 9, 1941 Ticonderoga; S.B., M.I.T. 1903, Civil Engineer, his theses being “An Investigation of the Bond of Union between Concrete and Steel for Concrete-Steel Construction; he entered the engineering department of the Pennsylvania Railroad, in 1905 moved to Ticonderoga, where he was actively engated in the practice of his profession; member of the Ticonderoga Board of Education, 1908-18, President of same 1916-18; President of Village Corporation; President and Director Ticonderoga National Bank; president of the Moses Ludington Hospital; director of the Northern New York Telephone Co; NY State Senator 1919-26; on the expiration of his last term as State Senator, he was appointed by Governor Smith a member of the Lake Champlain Bridge Commission and was elected its chairman; 1926 elected a member of the Republican State Committee of Essex Co; fraternally affiliated with Mount Defiance Lodge, Free and Accepted Masons, Ethan Allen Chapter, Royal Arch Masons, all of Ticonderoga; Knights Templar of Fort Henry, Albany Sovereign consistory, Oriental Temple, Mystic Shrine of Troy, the sons of the American Revolution; married February 14, 1905 at Newton MA, Elizabeth Leavitt [dau of John & Jeanette A (Huff) Leavitt] born April 5, 1880 and died June 15, 1959 Ticonderoga NY.
