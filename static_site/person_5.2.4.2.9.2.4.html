ISAAC BELL

ID: 5.2.4.2.9.2.4 | Source: James
(5.2.4.2.9.2.4) ISAAC BELL Jr born November 6, 1846 NYC and died January 29, 1889; successful cotton broker and investor; resided in one of NYC first co-op duplex apartment buildings, Knickerbocker; in 1883, built the Isaac Bell House, which is one of the famous Guilded Age summer “cottages” in Newport RI; he was active in politics, served as the US Minister to Netherlands 1885-88 as well as delegate to Democratic National Convention from RI 1888; he as known for his ties with the French bank Lentilhon Co and was one of the key investors in the Commecial Cable Co that broke the Transatlantic cable monopoly; like many of Newport’s upper-class families, Bell attended the extravagant costume balls and dinner parties that demonstrated upper-class status (see photo); married 1878 Jeanette Bennett [dau of James G] and born to them were:
