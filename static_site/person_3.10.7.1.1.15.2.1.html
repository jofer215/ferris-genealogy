BERTRAM JOHNSON

ID: 3.10.7.1.1.15.2.1 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.7.1.1.15.2.1) BERTRAM JOHNSON born July 9, 1877 Brooklyn NY and died September 12, 1945 Witchita KS; buried Greenwood Cemetery, Eurkea KS; graduated from high school in Minneapolis; attended the U of Minnesota for 3y; 4y at Jenner Medical College, Chicago and worked as chief chemist with the Illinois Malleable Iron Company of Chicago; received his M.D. 1908; internship in Philadelphia PA. 1910 relocated to Avant OK and practiced medicine until 1915 – served as doctor for the Osage Indian Reservation in Avant – Bertram and his wife rode horseback or buckboard on their calls; 1915 moved to Eurkea where he practiced medicine until his death; married June 21, 1911 in Galena MD, Elizabeth Whitaker [dau of William Alexander & Ella Gooding (Rodgers) Whitaker] born February 14, 1888 Galena, Kent, MD and died March 17, 1972 San Mateo CA; buried with Bertram; she attended Dover Academy; her application to Johns Hopkins U was rejected because she was a woman; applied and was accepted at the Herring Medical College, Chicago (Herring was later absorbed by Northwestern U), receiving her M.D. 1909; served her internship at Woman’s Hospital in Philadelphia; after Bertram’s death, she moved back East; was associated with the Grier School in PA and with Bennet Junior College, Millbrook NY; early 1960s moved to Calif.

No children recorded
