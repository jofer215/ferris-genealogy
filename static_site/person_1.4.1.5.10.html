CLAIBORNE FERRIS

ID: 1.4.1.5.10 | Source: John
(1.4.1.5.10) CLAIBORNE FERRIS born June 17, 1811 Westchester Co NY and died there September 23, 1898; married Anna Williams Lamb (1816-1902); no issue. He was Clerk of Ward Courts in New York and in 1869 he represented his district in the State Assembly. In the New York Herald, September 29, 1901: Relatives of Mrs. Anna W. Ferris bring action in Supreme Court to prove aged woman insane. Estate worth $300,000. Cranston Hamilton objects to Henry Ferris managing his aunt’s property. Doctors testify to insanity. Henry Ferris, nephew and lawyer who lives with his family in the same house with Mrs. Ferris is one of the executors of the estate of Claiborne Ferris, which he willed to his widow. Cranston Hamilton of Jersey City was a grandnephew of Claiborne Ferris. The 1850 Census shows the household of Claiborne to contain himself, Anna, Mary Lamb, age 57, Henry Ferris, age 9, and 3 Irish servants. Brooklyn Eagle, November 10, 1901: The Second Judicial Department of the Supreme Court has appointed the Long Island Loan and Trust Company committee of the estate of Anna W. Ferris, the widow of Claiborne Ferris. This estate amounts to about $250,000 Brooklyn Eagle, November 17, 1902: Notice that the will of the late Mrs. Anna W Ferris of Westchester, who died on July 15 last, would be contested was filed with Probate Clerk Jacob Washburn in Manhattan today, on behalf of several heirs-atlaw and next of kin of the testatrix. Mrs. Ferris was the widow of Claiborne Ferris, one of the richest m en in the old town of Westchester and her estate, although the petition to the probate of the will gave the value of both real and personal property as unknown, is said to be in the neighborhood of $250,000. Mrs. Ferris left about seventy relatives including one cousin and the children and grandchildren of many other cousins, who reside in New York, Connecticut, Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Wisconsin. Brooklyn Eagle, December 11, 1902: Testimony in the contest of the will of Mrs. Anna W. Ferris was begun before Surrogate Fitzgerald in Manhattan this morning. Mrs. Myrn C. E. Mead of Brooklyn testified to having been one of the subscribing witnesses and to having received instructions for the preparation of the will from Mrs. Ferris and transmitted them to the lawyer who drew up the will. She said that Mrs. Ferris, who was over 80 years of age, was active in mind and body.
