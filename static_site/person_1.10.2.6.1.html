WILLIAM FRANKLIN MOTT

ID: 1.10.2.6.1 | Source: John
(1.10.2.6.1) WILLIAM FRANKLIN MOTT born January 11, 1795 (January 11, 1785) NYC and died there May 3, 1867; in partnership with his brother, Samuel F., he established the first house in NY that carried on the domestic commission business. This trade increased rapidly during the War of 1812-15 when an embargo shut out foreign goods. He retired from business c1830, and during the remainder of his life was engaged in works of benevolence. He labored to prevent the inhuman treatment of foundling children when they were farmed out by the city authorities, and also to check on cruelty to animals, long before there was a society for that purpose. He was an active member of the Society of Friends, and a founder or benefactor of many charitable corporations, among them the Manumissoin Society (1807), the House of Refuge, the Eastern Dispensary, the Home for the Friendless, the Colored Orphans’ Asylum, etc; married December 10, 1807 Phebe Merritt (1.2.2.2.6.2); and born to them were:
