MYRON CHARLES TAYLOR

ID: 1.10.2.4.1.4.3.3 | Source: John
(1.10.2.4.1.4.3.3) MYRON CHARLES TAYLOR (Photos) born January 1874 and died May 6, 1959 NYC; buried Locust Valley, L. I., NY; Cornell School of Law; attorney; practiced law and then ran a group of textile mills in New England; corporate executive, financier, philanthropist; in 1932 succeeded J. P. Morgan, Jr., as U.S. Steel Corp CEO/COB; 1938 Conference on Political Refugees, Evian, France, Chair (requested by President Roosevelt); 1939-50 President Roosevelt’s envoy to the Vatican; 1950-53 Ambassador, Special Mission; gave Myron Taylor Hall and Anabel Taylor Hall, Cornell U; other info available on the ‘Net; married 1906 Anabel S. Mack born 1880 and died December 12, 1958 NYC; buried with Myron

No children recorded
