GEORGE WESLEY MEAD

ID: 4.4.9.2.1.1.7 | Source: Mary
(4.4.9.2.1.1.7) GEORGE WESLEY MEAD born March 22, 1820 Cortland Co NY and died February 17/19, 1863 Young’s Point LA; buried Riverside Cemetery, Anamosa, Jones, IA; relocated to Jones Co IA in the Spring of 1847 and started farming in Section 36 near Anamosa; Civil War Vet enlisted August 13, 1862 with Company E, 31st Iowa Infantry Regiment, participated in the Battle of Arkansas Post before he died of typhoid fever; “mustered into service at Davenport by Captain Hendershott on October 13 1862 and under the command of Col. William Smythe took transport and moved down river November 1st, arriving Helena, Arkansas the 20th. On the 27th of the same month, they started on an expedition to the Cold Water River, in Mississippi, returning to Helena December 7th. Two weeks later they started on the Yazoo River expedition and the regiment was partially engaged in the Battle of Chickasaw on the 27th, 28th and 29th of December. On the 2nd of January 1863, they started for a point near Arkansas Post, where there arrived on the 9th and on the night of the 10th marched through swamps and mire to the rear of the enemy’s works, where on the 11th they hotly engaged in the capture of the place. From this point, the regiment was ordered to Young’s point, Louisiana where they remained in camp until April 2nd, when they moved again up river to Greenville, Mississippi and after some skirmishes and considerable foraging for mules, cattle, horses, hogs and even negroes, they returned to Young’s Point on the 26th of same month. From here they moved with Grant’s whole army toward Grand Gulf arriving May 7th, when they again moved toward Jackson, Mississippi. The regiment was under fire at Black River on the 12th, was at the taking of Jackson on the 14th and again under fire at Black River on the 16th, reaching the rear of Vicksburg on the 18th, where it was engaged on the 22nd in a terrible but successful charge on the enemy’s works and was from this time steadily under fire until the fall of Vicksburg on July 4th;” married April 27, 1845 Sarah Jane Harvey born 1829 Chenango Co NY and died in Anamosa IA; buried with George; and born to them were:
