RALPH J. FERRIS

ID: 1.6.1.2.7.2.1.2 | Source: John
(1.6.1.2.7.2.1.2) RALPH J. FERRIS born August 21, 1884 and died October 7, 1960; Cornell, B.C.E, 1907; after graduation, he joined the New York State Highway Department and his first assignment was as engineer-in-charge of building the first macadam road in Tioga County, this being from Spencer Four Corners to West Candor Line. After several years he transferred to Pennsylvania State Highway Department and there, his first assignment was to buy, clear and rent the land to farmers until such time as the Commonwealth had sufficient funds to build the Pymatuning Reservior, located some 80 miles Northwest of Pittsburgh. During WWII, he served with the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. After the end of the War, he resumed work on the Pymatuning Reservior and saw it to completion before his death. In Linesville, pay site of the reservoir, he was known as "Mr. Pymatuning". He married Mabel L. Lange born August 31, 1892 and born to them were:
