HENRY LAMBERSTON CYPHERS

ID: 1.2.3.3.6.7 | Source: John
(1.2.3.3.6.7) HENRY LAMBERSTON CYPHERS born July 19, 1800 Newburgh, Orange, NY and died July 5, 1865 Vails Gate, New Winsor, Orange, NY; worked his mother’s property, his late father’s farm; married 1st January 9, 1823 in Bethlehem, Orange, NY Eliza Hunt died age 33y 5m 22d; buried Vails Gate Cemetery which adjoins the Methodist Church at New Winsor; 2d c1837 in New Windsor NY, Abigail Davis; and born to him and Eliza were (not not include several unknowns); (1.2.3.3.6.7.1) JOHN WOODRUFF SYPHERS (going back to original spelling as that is how source has it) born c1823 Vails Gate, New Winsor, Orange, NY and found dead December 17, 1863, in the bush, 3 miles from South Gundagai, New South Wales, supposedly fell from a horse; buried in the General Cemetery, North Gundagai; he arrived in Australia on the ship Kelso 1848, as a member of the crew – he joined the crew of the Kelso in London 1847 signing on for 2 years and was 1 of 5 crew members that deserted when the ship arrived in Sidney; naturalized 1857; The Wynard Times, 21 December 1863 reads: “It is with regret that we have to record the death of Mr. John Syphers, the more especially that it occurred in so sad a manner. For some years past he deceased had beenc complaining, now and then, of severe pains in the chest, which his medical advisor attributed to disease of the heart. Early on last Thursday morning he started from his house at Tarrabandra to go to Gundagai and see the advise of a medical man, having suffered more than usually from his affliction. About 11 o’clock on the same day, two brothers found the body of the unfortunate man stretched out by the side of the road, and supposing him to be asleep, one of the boy got off his horse to awake him, but found to his amazement that his life was gone. One of the youths remained with the body while the other returned to Gundagai and gave information to the police. The body was conveyed to Gundagai and an inquest held the same day, and on Friday forenoon waster interred in the cemetery at North Gundagai. During the many years that Mr Syphers exercised his vocation of blacksmith in this town, he was universally respected, as joined to his excellence as a tradesman there was a warmheartedness which brought him many friends. He leaves a widow and seven children. It will probably afford some consolation to those who have most cause to mourn his death, that the sympathy expressed for their loss is universal”; married October 29, 1851 in Tumut, New South Wales, Mary Anne Real [dau of Bartholomew & Mary (Doherty) Real] born January 3, 1828 Pittwater, NSW and died May 18, 1899 Grahams Town, NSW; buried Adelong; and born to them were:
