GEORGE FERRIS

ID: 3.1.2.1.6.10.9 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.2.1.6.10.9) GEORGE FERRIS (Photos) born January 21, 1840 Eaton Rapids (Eaton Co) MI and died August 19, 1906 Rawlins WY (tombstone looks like his death date was 1900); killed returning to Rawlins from his mines when his team of horses ran away. George went to Wyoming after the Civil War; developed a hotel in Rawlins and was involved in mining; and sheep ranching; member of Wyoming Territorial Legislative Assemblies 1873 and 1875 and a delegate to the Wyoming Constitutional Convention from Carbon Co; acquired sole ownership of the Ferris-Haggarty mine in the Grand Encampment copper mining district; married Julia Alspaugh (1853-1931); Julia was elected to the Board of Directors, Rawlins National Bank, in 1904; at the time of George’s death, plans had been made for the construction of his residence at 607 W Maple – the plans were carried out by his widow; following Julia’s death, the mansion declined over the years before turned into apartments and is now a B&B (Ferris Mansion); the house is alleged to be haunted by the ghost of their 7y old son, Cecil, who was accidently shot by one of his brothers who was playing with a loaded pistol.
