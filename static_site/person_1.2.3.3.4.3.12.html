THERESA NEWCOMER HARRIS

ID: 1.2.3.3.4.3.12 | Source: John
(1.2.3.3.4.3.12) THERESA NEWCOMER HARRIS (Teresa Newcomen Julia Everleigh Harris) (Peusa) (Photo) born August 12, 1839 Eldon House, London, Ontario and died November 1, 1928 Hertfordshire, England; in 1881 she resided at Wick Hill House, Warfield, Berks, England; received Gold Medal at R.G.S. for Central Asian Exploration; married 1st August 18, 1859 in London, Ontario, William John Scott born (1825) 1837 Jedburgh, Scotland and died June 16, 1875 on board HMS Palestine, near Suez; 2d February 27, 1877 in St Georges, Hanover Square, London, Lieutenant Clement St. George Royds Littledale [son of Captain Thomas & Jula (Royds) Littledale] born December 8, 1851 Highfield House, West Derby, Liverpool, England and died April 16, 1931 Wick Hill House, Bracknell, Berks, England; buried Easthampstead Church Cemetery, Berks, England; Clement was an explorer and Theresa traveled with him, including being the first female to ever enter the forbidden Tibetian monastery of Lhassa; in July 1896 he received a Royal Geographical Society Gold Medal for journeys through the Pamirs in 1890, China in 1891

No children recorded
