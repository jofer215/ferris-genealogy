WILBUR J. CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.1) WILBUR J. CHADEAYNE (Photo) born September 4, 1859 Inningwood Road, Ossining NY and died November 4, 1925; buried Fair View Cemetery, Chappaqua NY; never married; operated a home dairy on the Maplehurst farm, Inningwood Road, Ossining. He delivered milk daily in Ossining and had a clientele of patrons, including about every household in town. He was good-natured, hale and hearty in health and manner and he numbered each customer as a personal friend. (1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.2) JACOB CHADEAYNE (Photos) born September 17, 1862 Inningwood Road, Ossining NY and died November 22, 1926 Ossining; buried there Dale Cemetery; married July 2, 1896 in Ossining, Sarah Eliza Fisher [dau of Leander & Sarah (Anderson) Fisher] born July 2, Ossining and died there December 23, 1939; buried there in Dale Cemetery. Jacob, as a young boy, attended a one-room school near the Maplehurst farm and upon graduation attended Albany State Normal School from which he graduated. For a year after graduation he taught at the Teachers College in Albany NY and then decided to go into business for which he had been trained in Normal School. He had specialized in bookkeeping and mathematics. Jacob took a clerical position with Van Tassell Lumber Company in NYC for a few years and then, on October 29, 1894, went with Metropolitan Life Insurance Company as a bookkeeper. He advanced steadily in the company, becoming Manager of the Intermediate Department (April 28, 1908), Manager of the Ordinary Department (February 18, 1919), and Assistant Secretary (August 23, 1922). He was a member, trustee, and treasurer of the First Presbyterian Church of Ossining, first president of the YMCA, trustee of the Village of Ossining, trustee of the Ossining Board of Education, Director of the First National Bank and the Bank of Savings. His death occurred just after addressing a special school board meeting involving the construction of the new Ossining High School. Sarah was the President of the Dale Cemetery Association; trustee of the First Presbyterian Church and Vice Regent of the Mohegan Chapter of the DAR. Born to them were:

No children recorded
