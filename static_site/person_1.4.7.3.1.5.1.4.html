HOWLAND HAGGERTY PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.1.5.1.4 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.1.5.1.4) HOWLAND HAGGERTY PELL (born JOHN HOWLAND, but changed his name) born May 30, 1872 NY; graduated from the Flushing Institute at Flushing, L.I; 1892 he entered the First Battalion, New York Naval Militia, resigning May 1896, on account of going abroad. With his family he traveled extensively for six years, returning to New York 1902. The following year he joined the New York Stock Exchange and became a partner in the firm of S.H. Pell & Company, selling his seat in March 1915. He rejoined the First Battalion of Naval Militia in 1911 as paymaster, resigning 1915. He again joined the battalion on September 30, 1916, was later mustered into the United States service and in 1918 was paymaster with rank of Lieutenant, USN, stationed on the battleship Indiana. He married, November 27, 1895, Mary Willets [dau of Frederick & Anna (Whitson) Willets] of Westbury, L.I. Born to them were:
