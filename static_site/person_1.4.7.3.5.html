MARIA PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.5 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.5) MARIA PELL married Jacob Tredwell Walden [founder of village that bears his name in Orange Co NY] - 1812 – Jacob T. Walden, a prosperous shipping merchant of New York City who was Walden's real father, recognized the immense manufacturing value of the area’s natural water power, sold out his New York City interests and established himself on the site of Walden. Walden helped build the cotton and wool mills in an area known as Kiddtown and High Falls. 1813 – Jacob T. Walden bought the house in 1813, when he came to the village (when it was called Kiddtown) to build textile mills powered by the Wallkill River. Jacob T. Walden House. North Montgomery & Wait Streets, Walden. 18th century Dutch colonial house. made from shale and limestone blocks, its original Dutch double front door still intact Once owned by Jacob T. Walden for whom the village of Walden is named. 1822 – Walden convinced New York City businessmen Jesse Scofield and Seth C. Capron to create a woolen mill and in this year the investors formed the Franklin Company. Born to them were:
