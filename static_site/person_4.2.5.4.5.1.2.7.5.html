ROBERT ELIHU STAUFFER

ID: 4.2.5.4.5.1.2.7.5 | Source: Mary
(4.2.5.4.5.1.2.7.5) ROBERT ELIHU STAUFFER born July 25, 1884 Kent, Portage, OH and died November 25, 1981 Sebring, Columbiana, OH; graduated 1st in his class at Kent High School; valedictorian when he graduated from Mount Union College OH; received A.M. from Harvard and did further graduate study at the U. of Chicago and New York State U where he received the B.L.S. degree; Carnegie scholar at Harvard summer of 1931; on his retirement from Mount Union College awarded the honorary degree of L.M.D.; held the position of Professor of English at West Virginia Wesleyan College 1909-1915; and same at Willamette U, 1915-17; returned to his alma mater in 1920 to reorganize the college library after being chief classifier at the New York Public Library 1919-20; served as head librarian at Mount Union 1920-55; Professor of English 1920-21; Professor of Greek 1921-59; author of The American Spirit in the Writings of Americans of Foreign Birth; and other articles and poetry reviews; married August 29, 1912 Lourena Carrie Geisuiger born December 17, 1887 Massillon OH and died 1973; and born to them were 2 kids, 1 of which was:
