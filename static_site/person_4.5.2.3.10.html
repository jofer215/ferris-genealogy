IRA LOCKWOOD

ID: 4.5.2.3.10 | Source: Mary
(4.5.2.3.10) IRA LOCKWOOD (#642) born October 19, 1769 Greenwich CT and died April 18, 1846 having lived nearly 77 years on the same farm. Ira was one of the most successful farmers in the town, though not a large one. He commenced life poor, but by energy and economy became well off. It is related of him that at one time during the Rev. War he was standing guard, though not a soldier, and one dark night he heard footsteps of something coming, and three times said, “Who comes there?” No answer came, and he fired when he discovered he had shot a colt, thinking it was a Tory. Ira was a Whig in politics and was a constable and collector of Greenwich for a number of years. He married (Clemence) Clementine Mills.
