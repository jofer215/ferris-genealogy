JOHN MEAD

ID: 4.4.9.1 | Source: Mary
(4.4.9.1) JOHN MEAD (#331) (General) born 1725/27 Horse Neck, Greenwich and died December 3, 1790; buried in the old graveyard at Horseneck; spent his early life with his father on the farm. Before the Rev. War, he was commissioned and had command of a company. After the war had commenced, he was promoted to Colonel and for three years commanded the American lines at Horseneck, and for several miles either way from it, saw some active service. He was with our troops when New York was given up, and his regiment was the last to leave the city. He was subsequently promoted to BrigadierGeneral. John was short of stature and very fleshy. After the War, he was employed a greater part of the time in public matters. He built several vessels, some of which were purchased by the Government for war-ships. He was for nineteen consecutive years, and to the close of his life, a member of the Connecticut legislature, and was at the time of his death, Judge of the Court Probate. He married 1st 1752 (August 25, 1754) Mary Brush, of Huntington, L.I., [dau of Benjamin] (1730-1785) (died October 28, 1784); married 2d Mehetabel (Blackman) Peck, widow of Jonathan Peck.
