(5.1.2.9.4.4.2.1.8) JOHANNA SMITH Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Thursday, Dec. 10, 1970: Mrs. Johanna Harkins, 74 of Schudder Hook Rd., Livingston, died Nov. 24 in Columbia Memorial Hospital, Hudson, after a brief illness. She was the widow of William Harkins and moved to Livingston six years ago after living on Crane Rd. in Mahopac for 27 years. She born in Kent June 12, 1896, the daughter of Orlando and Sarah Bennett Smith. She is survived by eight stepchildren, Edward of Mahopac, James and Charles of Mahopac Falls, John W. of Miami, Fla., Calvin of Livingston, Robert of Pompono, Fla., Mrs. Ann Crum of Olsmore, Fla., and Mrs. Marjorie Walsh of Haverstraw; a sister, Mrs. Mary Nelson of Carmel; a brother George Smith of Tilly Foster; 25 grandchildren and two great-grandchildren. Burial was in Raymond Hill Cemetery. Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Thursday, Oct. 29, 1970: William Harkins, 89, of Shudder Hood Rd., Livingston, died suddenly Oct. 20 at his home. He born in Towners on May 3, 1881 the son of Patrick and Sarah (Cannon) Harkins. Mr. Harkins is survived by his wife, the former Johanna Smith, six sons, James, Charles and Edward of Mahopac, Calvin of Livingston, Robert of Pompono, Fla. and John of North Miami, Fla., and two daughters, Mrs. Willard (Ann) Crum of Olsmore, Fla., and Mrs. Donald (Marjorie) Walsh of Haverstraw. He is also survived by 25 grandchildren and two great grandchildren. Mr. Harkins was educated in Towners and was a member of the Methodist Church. He was a self-employed farmer who lived on Crane Road in Mahopac for 27 years before moving to Livingston six years ago. Interment was in Raymond Hill Cemetery, Carmel.
