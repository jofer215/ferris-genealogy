JAMES WOOD HOYT

ID: 4.6.5.5.1.6.1 | Source: Mary
(4.6.5.5.1.6.1) JAMES WOOD HOYT (HOYTE) (#5862) (Rev.) born June 19, 1823 NY; graduated from Union College 1847; taught in Syracuse NY for 18 months; entered Theological Seminary, New Albany IN, 1849; licensed in 1851; was vice-president and manager of a classical institute at New Albany until 1853, when he moved to Nashville TN where he taught female school, 1853-55; ordained and installed pastor of the Smyrna Presbyterian Church, Nashville, 1855; studied and graduated in medicine, 1856-58, at the same time, preaching every Sabbath; appointed Professor of Ethics and Belles Lettres, U. of Nashville, 1858, a position he still held in 1871. James and one brother {don’t know which one} adopted the Hoyte spelling. James married 1st July 5, 1852 Elizabeth Berrien Lindsley [dau of Philip & Margaret Elizabeth (Lawrence) Lindsley] born c1825 Davidson Co TN and died March 1, 1865 at age 40 years Nashville, Davidson, TN [Jim - probably of childbirth complications.], and married 2d July 14, 1868 widow Manilla L. (Massey) Gleaves [dau of William]. Born to James and Eliza were:
