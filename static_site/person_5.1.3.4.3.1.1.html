ETHAN FERRIS BISHOP

ID: 5.1.3.4.3.1.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.4.3.1.1) ETHAN FERRIS BISHOP (Rev.) born March 27, 1825 Madison NJ and died December 7, 1883 Bridgeport CT; attended Yale (1845-46), but through an unfortunate accident resulting in the loss of an eye, was obliged to discontinue his studies and return to Bridgeport. He received a degree of M.A., from Trinity College, Hartford CT, 1851. He was licensed as a lay reader by Bishop Brownell under the Rev. Gurdon S. Coit, D.D., rector of St. John's Church, Bridgeport on November 21, 1856. He and his family founded the Church of Nativity, Bridgeport, 1858; ordained Deacon by Bishop Williams on May 21, 1860 and priest by Bishop Williams on September 22, 1863; served at the Church of the Holy Nativity 1860-83; 1864 built School for Needy and Orphaned Boys; President of Bridgeport Steamboat Company 1865, and Naugatuck Railroad 1873; married March 22, 1847 at Brooklyn NY, Georgianna Moody born February 5, 1826 England and died January 17, 1898 Bridgeport CT. She wrote at least two books, The Christians Seasons and The Yule Log. Recently, Ethan's diary, dated c1870, was located and purchased at a tag sale. Born to them were:
