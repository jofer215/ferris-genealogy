GIDEON HAIT

ID: 4.6.5.5 | Source: Mary
(4.6.5.5) GIDEON HAIT (#2901) (NM7W-7M); (LDS has name HOYT) born (baptized) March 20, 1736/37 Stamford and died July 26, 1801, Greenfield NY. He resided in Old Pound Ridge in 1773 and up to the time of the revolution; afterwards moved to Greenfield, Saratoga County NY and was buried there. He married February 7, 1760 at Stamford, Elizabeth Weed (c1736-c1815) (2.1.2.2.3).’My greatgrandfather, at an early day, removed, with a robust, healthy wife and several sons and daughters, into Greenfield NY. It is said that good old Gideon was inferior in size and strength to his better half, that she was a peculiar woman, and somewhat eccentric, possessing good common sense, economical, and consequently a helpmate to her meek husband. Undoubtedly many of her descendants inherited their physical organization, together with some traits of character, from this good woman. It is said that soon after settling in Greenfield, when she was traveling through a piece of woods near her home, an owl flew down from a tree, perched upon the old lady’s bonnet, and flew off with it. While on the wing with the valuable article of furniture, the old woman, in her amazement, exclaimed, ‘Art thou an angel of light or darkness? If thou art an angel of darkness, get thee hence Satan, for I am none of thine. I belong to Mr. Gilbert’s church.” Born to them were:
