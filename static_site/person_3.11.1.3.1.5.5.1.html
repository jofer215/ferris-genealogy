THOMAS HARPER MILBURN

ID: 3.11.1.3.1.5.5.1 | Source: Joseph
(3.11.1.3.1.5.5.1) THOMAS HARPER MILBURN born March 11, 1836 Easton’s Corners, Ontario and died May 28, 1910 Pittsburgh, Allegheny, PA; buried Memphis TN; moved from Canada to Mishawaka, St. Joseph, IN in the fall of 1854 where he became a salesman, bookkeeper and partner in the general merchandise business; sold out in 1860 and moved to Lincoln IL where he was a partner in a hardware business, Niles & Milburn; vice president and manager of the agencies and sales department of Milburn Wagon Company until 1872; April 1873 moved to Memphis where he was a member of Milburn, Walker Company and became president of the Fourth National Bank of Memphis; 1879 purchased controlling interest in the German National Bank, closed Fourth National and died all business from German Bank until May 1886 when he sold all interest in the bank; c1883 became Treasurer of Milburn Gin and Machine Works; well known in the Memphis Cotton Exchange for 30y; married September 27, 1858 in Mishawaka, Mary E Luce born 1838 NY and died November 6, 1890 Memphis TN; buried there; and born to them were: (3.11.1.3.1.5.5.1.1) FRANK MILBURN born August 4, 1860 IN and died October 7, 1881 Des Plaines, Cook, IL; buried Memphis TN
