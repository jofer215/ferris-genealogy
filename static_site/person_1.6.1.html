JOHN FERRIS

ID: 1.6.1 | Source: John
(1.6.1) JOHN FERRIS born April 13, 1723 Westchester NY and died at North Castle, Westchester County NY October 6, 1808; married c1737 Elizabeth Griffin [dau of John and Hannah (Clark) Griffin] born April 24, 1723 and died at North Castle on January 14, 1789. John was the first Ferris in Bedford on April 7, 1747 when at a lawful town meeting for choosing town officers for North Castle, he was appointed "highway master for the year ensuing for the east side of Long Bridge". The Ferris Family was farmers, and it is written the farm originally comprised six hundred acres. The crops were harvested and sold to New York, going by wagon to Port Chester or across Westchester to barges on the Hudson River. When America declared its independence in 1776, he is recorded as being the seventh volunteer in the Bedford colonial army. His home was one of those burned to the ground by the British Second Regiment Westchester County Militia on April 27, 1777. He later built a new home near the site of the old one. He died at the age of eighty-five from falling from his horse. A farmer, his remains lie in the Ferris Cemetery on his farm (plot 7) and Elizabeth, plot 6 (Route 22 near North Castle line). In the early 1900's farming no longer became profitable in Bedford and the lands were subdivided and sold. The last lot being sold by the estate of Hannah E. Ferris to Daniel O'Brien, in turn to Edward Boise, in turn to Helen Frick in 1939, who generously donated it to the Westmoreland Sanctuary in September 1978.The cemetery plots referred to are in the Ferris Family Cemetery in Westmoreland. There are forty-eight graves in this cemetery; the oldest stone is Elizabeth Ferris, 1789. The last stone is John B. Ferris, 1898, giving the cemetery 109 years of use.
