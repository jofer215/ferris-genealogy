MARY FERRIS

ID: 1.2 | Source: John
(1.2) MARY FERRIS born February 22, 1663 (c1663-1666/69/70) Throgs Neck, Westchester, NY and died before May 9, 1715 (only because her father’s will does not mention her) at Westchestertown (now East Bronx), Westchester Co. NY; married September (March) (October 10) 12, 1685 (Jim - there are a variety of dates of the marriage, but they are all 1685, which is when he moved to Westchester.) Nathaniel Underhill [son of John & Elizabeth (Feake) Underhill] born December 22, 1663 Killingworth, Oyster Bay NY and died November 10, 1710 Westchester Co NY. He was Warden (Mayor) of the Borough of Westchester. Nathaniel, a Quaker and a carpenter, was the youngest son of Captain John Underhill (1609-1672) and his second wife, Elizabeth Feake (1633-1674). In January, 1685/6, he was ‘late of Oyster Bay’ when he purchased land near the bridge of Westchester upon ‘ffrog’s Neck from John and Sarah Turner; on March 22, 1686/7, he and his wife sold Oyster Bay lands to John Underhill. He conveyed most of his land to his son, Nathaniel, Jr., January 23, 1709/10; this land in what was later called Williamsbridge, became part of New York City.
