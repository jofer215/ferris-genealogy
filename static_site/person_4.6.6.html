ISRAEL LOCKWOOD

ID: 4.6.6 | Source: Mary
(4.6.6) ISRAEL LOCKWOOD (#67) born June 4, 1710 and died in 1776 of a camp distemper (1778 Pound Ridge NY) as a Rev War soldier; married September 1, 1748 in New Canaan CT, Susannah Smith [dau of Caleb & Susannah (Scofield) Smith] born February 6, 1720/21 Stamford and died 1811; no offspring – on February 15, 1760, Israel was a member of the committee to act on the building of the meeting house at Pound Ridge; in 1763, he was on a list of freeholders at Pound Ridge; his will, dated October 3, 1776, probated December 16, 1778 leaves to cousin (i.e., nephew) Hezekiah Lockwood one-half his lands and to cousins Reuben, David, and Nathan, sons of Captain Reuben Lockwood, the other half ; legacies to the daughters of Captain Reuben Lockwood, viz., Lydia, Ann, Sarah, Rebecca, Jerusha, Elizabeth; Jemima Briggs (probably niece of Irrael’s wife, daughter of Hannah (Smith) Briggs; “the wife of Gilbert Weed” (Abigail Hoyt, daughter of Elizabeth (Lockwood) Hoyt, Israel’s sister] and “the wife of Hezekiah Lockwood (perhaps Mary Birdsall?) (Some Descendants of Edmund Lockwood by Harriet Woodbury Hodge)

No children recorded
