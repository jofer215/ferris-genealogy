PHEBE FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.3.1.10.3.3 | Source: James
(5.1.3.3.1.10.3.3) PHEBE FERRIS (listed in the Overmyer History and Genealogy, 1905) born January 27, 1850 Elmore OH; married July 3, 1870 at Elmore, Isaac Overmyer [son of Michael H. & Elizabeth (Alter) Overmyer] (Overmeyer) born April 30, 1846 Sandusky Co OH and died September 8, 1872 Elmore OH; buried there. Isaac was educated in the common schools of Sandusky County. After growing to manhood he enlisted as a soldier of the Civil War, was assigned to Company C., 72d Ohio Volunteer Infantry Regiment; was captured at Guntown and confined to Andersonville Prison. While confined there, he and several comrades, once made an escape and proceeded toward the union lines a few days, but were recaptured by the dreaded bloodhounds, who compelled them to climb saplings in the woods to keep them from being torn to pieces by the ferocious animals. In 1865 he was discharged from prison and returned to Hessville OH; after resting and regaining health and strength for several months, he served as clerk in a general store for Elias Lay at Hessville for a short period of time; then went to Elmore and clerked for John H. MaGee in a similar store until his death. Born to them was:
