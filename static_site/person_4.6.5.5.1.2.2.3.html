CHARLES HENRY EIGHMEY

ID: 4.6.5.5.1.2.2.3 | Source: Mary
(4.6.5.5.1.2.2.3) CHARLES HENRY EIGHMEY (Photos) born November 28, 1834 NY and died August 28, 1921; the first 14y of his life were spent in his native state, and in 1848 he accompanied his parents on their move to the west. He entered Cornell College, of Mt Vernon IA, and was later a student of Mt Morris Seminary of Mt Morris IL. When his literary education was completed he studied law in the office of ex-Governor Hempstead, was admitted to the Bar in 1860, and for 10y successfully practiced his profession, and was a member of the well known firm of Cooley & Eighmey. In 1870 he became Cashier of the First National Bank of Dubuque, was afterward Vice President, and in 1890 was elected President. He was a stockholder in the Novelty Iron Works Co, the Dubuque County Bank, and of the First National Bank of Plattsville WI. He was president of the Norwegian Plow Co and also of the Westphal Hinds Hardware Co; president of the Board of Trade; member of the Main Street Methodist Episcopal Church; married October 6, 1864 Elizabeth Jane Stewart [dau of William G & Caroline] born May 20, 1843 Dubuque and died November 30, 1912; both buried Linwood Cemetery, Dubuque Co IA; she served as director on the boards of both Finley hospital and the Home for the Friendlessand born to them was:
