GRACE MARIE CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.10.3.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.10.3.1) GRACE MARIE CHADEAYNE born May 3, 1892 and died May 2, 1974 Ossining NY; buried Sleepy Hollow Cemetery, Tarrytown NY; married October 7, 1921 in Ossining, Robert Fellows Gowen [son of Charles Sewall & Alice Jerusha (Fellows) Gowen] born December 30, 1882 Lowell MA and died January 2, 1966 Ossining; buried Sleepy Hollow Cemetery. Robert graduated from Harvard, 1906. He was chief engineer and plant manager of the De Forest Radio Telephone and Telegraph Company. He held the world’s record in radio transmission on low power having, in 1920, transmitted speech and music from his home in Ossining to Valley City ND, a distance of 1500 miles. He was a member of the Institute of Radio Engineers, American Institute of Electrical Engineers, the Harvard Engineering Society, and the Harvard Club of New York. In May 1945, Grace was reappointed Chairman of the American Red Cross. Born to them were:
