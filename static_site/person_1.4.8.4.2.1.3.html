JOSEPHINE WILLIAMINA NICHOLLS

ID: 1.4.8.4.2.1.3 | Source: John
(1.4.8.4.2.1.3) JOSEPHINE WILLIAMINA NICHOLLS born 1820 Donaldsonville, Ascension Parish, LA and died September 24, 1868 Assumption Parish LA; married March 12, 1844 as his 2d wife, William Whitmell Hill Pugh [son of Whitmell Hill & Mary Whitmell (Byran) Pugh] born January 18, 1811 Woodville, Bertie, NC and died January 3, 1906 Woodlawn Plantation, Napoleonville, Assumption Parish LA; buried there; in the fall of 1834, on the death of his father, William returned to LA to superintend the plantation and to settle the estate; served in the state legislature, representing Assumption Parish, 1848-50, 1852-56, in the latter term as Speaker of the House in LA; in the Spring of 1835, he purchased Woodlawn Plantation on the east bank of Bayou Lafourche, three miles below Napoleonville and built the main part of the Woodlawn residence, which stood for over 100 years and was razed in 1946; he served as the state’s first superintendent of schools after reorganization of the education system in 1847; and born to them were:
