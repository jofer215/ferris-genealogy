MARY MEAD

ID: 4.4.9.1.4 | Source: Mary
(4.4.9.1.4) MARY MEAD (#817) (twin) born Greenwich (December 11/13, 1759); married August 1782 Levi Hanford [son of Levi & Elizabeth (Carter) Hanford] born September 19, 1759 and died October 19, 1854; Levi remained with his father on the farm and the mill until the year 1775, when at age 16, enrolled in a company of minute men, commanded by his maternal uncle, Captain John Carter. In the spring of 1776, he volunteered under General Lee, to defend the city of New York, and worked on the fortifications at Governor’s Island. In October, he enlisted in a company of Light Horse, under Captain Seth Seymour. On March 13, 1777, he was taken prisoner at Old Well and carried to Huntington, L. I., thence to Flushing and to New York, where he was incarcerate in the old prison and ships, suffering everything but death; he was finally exchanged. Born to them were:
