ETHEL MAY CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.10.5.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.10.5.1) ETHEL MAY CHADEAYNE (Photos) born August 21, 1889 at “Just a Mere” farm, Yorktown NY and died May 17, 1964 Ossining NY; buried Kensico Cemetery, Mt. Pleasant NY; was schooled in Tarrytown; attended a girl’s finishing school; very active in the historic First Baptist Church of Ossining; married June 1, 1909 in Westchester Co NY, John Edward Magner [son of James F. & Margaret (Crane) Magner] born March 27, 1887 Norwalk CT and died March 30, 1945 Ossining NY; buried in Magner family plot at St. Mary’s Cemetery, Norwalk CT. John was a first generation Irish-American born and raised in Norwalk CT. During WWI, he was active in the Connecticut National Guard. During the depression years, he worked at various jobs in Ossining; he worked for the welfare department in Ossining and also for Floyd Waterbury, his brother-in-law. John loved the sea and knew a great deal about boats, instilling in both sons, the love the sea. After John’s death, Ethel worked at the Sing Sing Prison for the then Warden Denno. She was one of the original contributors to the founding of the Ossining Historical Society. She later married Stuart Ryder. Born to Ethel and John were:
