SILAS C. CROFT

ID: 5.1.2.9.6.1.9 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.6.1.9) SILAS C. CROFT born October 11, 1848 Putnam Valley and died April 12, 1902 NYC; married Caroline B. Smith born January 27, 1842 and died September 19, 1911; both buried Hillside Cemetery. Silas located in New York City in 1868, and for a time was employed in a carpet store and soon after went into business for himself, he was senior partner of the firm of Croft Brothers, dealers in carpets in Harlem. His connection with the firm ended in 1895, when he was appointed a Commissioner of Charities by Mayor Strong. In 1897, he was appointed Surveyor of Customs for the Port of New York by President McKinley, and was reappointed by President Roosevelt. He was founder of the Central Republican Club, and for years was the Republican leader in the 31st Assembly District. Born to them was:
