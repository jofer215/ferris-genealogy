FRANK HALLIDAY FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.6.4.5.5.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.6.4.5.5.1) FRANK HALLIDAY FERRIS (Photo) born May 5, 1892 Brooklyn NY and died April 30, 1974 Jamestown NY; graduated from Hackensack High School, 1910; received his A. B., Amherst, 1914; his A. M., Columbia U., 1917; his B. D., Union Theological Seminary, 1917; and his Ph.D., Drew U., 1931. Frank was ordained to the Congregational ministry, 1917; transferred to Presbyterian ministry, 1920; Pastor, First Congregational Church, Pulaski NY, 1917-20; [Chaplain, U.S. Navy, 1917-19 on board USS Mercy and USS Texas?] First Presbyterian Church, East Cleveland OH, 1920-26; South Park Church, Newark NJ, 1926-31; Fairmount Presbyterian Church, Cleveland Heights OH, 1931-50; Baccalaureate Speaker, Oberlin College, 1945; visiting professor of religion, College of Wooster OH, 1952-53; Interim Minister of the First Congregational Church, Jamestown NY. He married, May 15, 1917, Mina Proctor [dau of Robert & Mary Elizabeth (Jackson) Proctor] born October 5, 1891 and died March 2, 1972 at Westfield NY. In the 1950s their home was listed as in Mayville NY. Numerous articles published in Atlantic Monthly and Harper’s. Author: Standing up to Life. Born to them were:
