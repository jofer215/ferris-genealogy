ISRAEL PECK

ID: 3.10.7.12.6 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.7.12.6) ISRAEL PECK (Photo) born December 7, 1794 Greenwich CT and died after 1876 Portchester NY; married 1st Darinda Peck (3.10.7.6.3.1); married second, Almira M. (Mead) Platt (widow of Rev. Ebenezer Platt) (dau of Ebenezer & Zetta S. (Mead) Mead). He was reared a farmer, which was always his business until infirmity caused him to sell his farm, he owned the old homestead known as “Peck’s Landing”, which he sold c1866/68, and after residing at Port Chester a few years, settled in the borough of Greenwich 1873. He was a captain of a light militia company for some 4 years. In politics he was a strong Republican and for more than 51 years he was a worthy member of the Second Congregational Church at Greenwich and one of its main pillars. He was a very strong temperance man, and believed that total abstinence is the only true safeguard. Born to them were:
