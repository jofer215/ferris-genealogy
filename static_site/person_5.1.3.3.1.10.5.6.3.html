ROBERT AUGUSTUS FERRIS, J.D.,

ID: 5.1.3.3.1.10.5.6.3 | Source: James
(5.1.3.3.1.10.5.6.3) ROBERT AUGUSTUS FERRIS, J.D., (Photographs) born April 2, 1913 Hoisington, Barton, KS and died his sleep April 6, 2004, Tulsa OK; received his B.A., and J.D., from the U. of Kansas; employed in Land Department of Phillips Petroleum Company; Land Department of Sinclair Prairie Oil Company; and for 35 years with Amerada Petroleum Company and Amerada Hess Corp., serving at different periods as Contracts Analyst, Supervisor of Title Section, Landman, and Administrative Manager of Regional Office. Afterwards he joined KWB Oil Property Management as Land Manager retiring in 1991. He belongs to the American Association of Petroleum Landmen; Tulsa Association of Petroleum Landmen; Oklahoma Bar Association; and the Mineral Law Section of the Oklahoma Bar Association. Robert married March 30, 1940 in Wichita KS, Sarah "Sally" Elsie Voth [dau of John & Mary - missionaries] born Hyderabad, India. Sarah graduated from Tabor College and had graduate study at the U. of Iowa. She was a Newton KS high school algebra teacher at the time she and Robert met. They reside in Tulsa, in the same house they bought in 1942. Bob was the provider of this ‘twig’ of the Tree - thanks, Bob. Bob encouraged me a lot on the Ferris Tree – he will be missed! An email from Bob: Hello Jim, I was watching a TV documentary on Ireland last night and it reminded me that Ferris is an Irish name. It’s also a Scottish name. But of course I know that Jeffrey Ferris came from England. Craig Ferris, an acquaintance in Tulsa (no relative), told me of the Ferris Castle about 2 or 3 miles out of Kilarney, Ireland. It is a National Monument. It was destroyed by Cromwell in 1652 in the English invasion of Ireland. Craig said that a photographer by the name of Ferris has a nice studio on the main street of Kilarney, and that a Richard Ferris took Craig and his wife on a horse drawn carriage tour of Kilarney. When Sally and I were in Edinborough, Scotland one time I looked at the Ferris names in the phone book and found a Rev. John Ferris. Since we have a son John I decided to call. The Rev. was out of town, but Mrs. Ferris invited us over for tea. We went and had an interesting visit. She said she and her husband were both born and reared in Ireland; they had been missionaries in India, as Sally's parents had been, and their children had gone to the same British boarding school that Sally had attended. Also, in Edinborough I stopped in a woolen shop where there were neckties with the various Scottish plaids. I asked the clerk if there was a Ferris plaid. He said no but that there was a Ferguson plaid and that the Fergusons and the Ferrrises were cousins, so I should buy a Ferguson tie. I thought he was "pulling my leg" to sell a tie but I bought one anyway, as I liked it. Awhile back I was browsing through a book in the Tulsa genealogical library on Scottish history and it said that the Ferrises and Fergusons were cousins. The maiden name of my great grandmother, wife of Andrew Ferris, was Ferguson. I just wanted to tell my story, and hope it’s of some interest to another Ferris. Bob Born to them were:
