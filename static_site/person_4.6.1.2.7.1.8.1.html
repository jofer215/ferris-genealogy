RAY WARING KEELER

ID: 4.6.1.2.7.1.8.1 | Source: Mary
(4.6.1.2.7.1.8.1) RAY WARING KEELER born November 11, 1869 Howe, Lagrange, IN and died January 17, 1940 Chokio, Stevens, MN; buried Morris, Stevens, MN; born in Indiana while his parents were in the process of migrating to Iowa; attended Cedar Falls Normal College, Iowa where he met his wife; earned 50 cents a day as a carpenter at a lumber yard in Lake View IA; moved to Mendon MO, 1907 and Chokio 1909; a farmer, carpenter and organized farmer cooperatives; elected to Minnesota legislature 1927-33; at the time of his death, he was a member of the boards of the Chokio Equity Exchange (previously president of the board); Chokio Cooperative Creamery, and the Chokio Livestock Shipping Association; secretary of the Central Cooperative Association of St. Paul since its organization; promoter of the first farm club of the county – organized among the farmers of Stevens Township – it predated the Farm Bureau; County Commissioner from the 4th district, 1921-35; one of the 1st large-scale turkey raisers in the county; married December 26, 1895 in Harper IA, Clara Winget [dau of James Cochran & Martha Almira (Sheldon) Winget] born January 5, 1870 Harper, Keokuk, IA and died June 22, 1963 Adams, Adams, WI; buried with Ray; high school graduate 1888; Iowa State Normal School, 1892; teacher and homemaker; and born to them were:
