WILLIAM BARNETT UNDERHILL

ID: 1.2.6.2.1.1.2 | Source: John
(1.2.6.2.1.1.2) WILLIAM BARNETT UNDERHILL (Photo) born 1818 Washington Co OH and died April 16, 1905 Schuyler Co IL; Civil War Vet with Company D, 115th Illinois Infantry Regiment; Company D was the company that held Hood’s army of 30,000 strong in check for 10 hours on that memorable day of October 13, 1864 at Buzzards Roost Gap – the Battle of Chattanooga TN would not have been won by the Union; married July 23, 1840 Zanesville OH, Elizabeth Jane Robinson (DAR) [dau of Ebenezer & Elizabeth (Hamilton) Robinson] born July 30, 1817 Meadville, Crawford, PA and died January 4, 1914 Rushville, Schuyler, IL; buried Rushville Cemetery; they migrated from OH to Schuyler Co IL c1847 and made their home in Bainbridge Township and engaged in farming until 1862 when he enlisted in Company D; and born to them were:
