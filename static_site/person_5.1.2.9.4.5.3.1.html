GEORGE W. BAXTER

ID: 5.1.2.9.4.5.3.1 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.4.5.3.1) GEORGE W. BAXTER born September 21, 1865 and died March 28, 1952 Peekskill NY; married Louise Marie Smith; both buried Hillside Cemetery, Peekskill NY; Obituary, Star, Peekskill, NY, June 9, 1956: “Mrs. Louise Marie Smith Baxter widow of George W. Baxter, died this morning at her residence in Annsville following a long illness. She was in her 81st year. Mrs. Baxter had been active in the organizing of the Annsville Union Church and the Cal. Memorial Church in the Boe Park. She was a Sunday school teacher at both churches, and also taught at the Bethel Mission held at the First Baptist Church in Peekskill of which she was a member. Mrs. Baxter was an associate member of the Annsville Union Church and the Calvary Memorial Church. A resident of the Peekskill area since 1913 she born in Putnam County on Jan. 10, 1876, the daughter of Isaac H. Smith. She resided in Annsville at the C.W. Smith Memorial Cottage, which is connected with the Annsville Union Church. Interment will be in Hillside Cemetery.” Obituary, Star, Peekskill, NY, March 29, 1952: “George Baxter, 86, the dean of carpenters in Peekskill, and former member of the firm of Wiberly & Baxter, which constructed the Uriah Hill School and the Ballard Building at Park Circle, died suddenly yesterday afternoon at his home, 308 Ringgold Street. He had been up and around yesterday and appeared in good health until stricken unexpectedly with a heart attack. He was dead upon the arrival of a physician. Mr. Baxter was one of Peekskill's best-known carpenters and contractors and had been in business more than a half century. He born at Wiccopee, Putnam County, Sept. 21st, 1865, the son of Gilbert and Sarah Wilson Baxter. At an early age he decided to become a carpenter and builder. After his apprenticeship, he went into a partnership business with James Thompson, under the firm name of Thompson & Baxter. While with this partnership he built one of the large buildings at St. Mary's School. Later he formed a partnership with the late William Wiberley, under the firm name of Wiberley & Baxter, which carried on for 45 years. This firm built Uriah Hill Jr. School; St. Patrick's Parochial School in Verplanck; and many of the smaller buildings at the Friars of the Atonement, Graymoor; an addition at Valeria Home; a building at the Peekskill Military Academy and the big church and parish house for the Catholic Church in Liberty, N.Y. For many years the firm had its workshop and office in the former Nelson Law office building on Nelson Avenue, which building was recently removed to Franklin and Depew Street. He and Mr. Wiberley had the contract for the construction of many private dwellings in Peekskill, the town of Cortlandt and Putnam County. After Mr. Wiberley's death, Mr. Baxter continued with carpentry work for several years. Interment will be made in Hillside Cemetery.” Born to them were:
