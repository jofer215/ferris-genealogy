NATHANIEL BETTS FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.8.6.4 | Source: James
(5.1.3.8.6.4) NATHANIEL BETTS FERRIS (Photographs) born October 31, 1839 Greenwich on the paternal farm, where he passed his early life; education was acquired in the local public schools and Greenwich Academy, and he lived at home until 1870, when he relocated to a place in Prospect Street, just north of Enos E. Lockwood's, whereon he made his home for about seven years, during which time he gave his attention to private affairs, and acted as administrator of various estates. Meanwhile the home farm had been sold to a New York man, and returned to the possession of the Ferris family through foreclosures of mortgage, and in 1878, Nathaniel returned to it, and resided there until his removal, in 1890, to Stamford, where he lived until his death, November 11, 1921. [Chaplain Ferris has him dying in New York City.] Nathaniel had an enviable standing among his fellowmen of that city, and was regarded as one of the substantial, progressive men of the community. He was prominent in religious circles as an earnest member of the Congregational Church, in which society, during his residence in Greenwich, he was a zealous worker. For twenty years he was clerk of the First Congregational Church at South Beach (Old Greenwich), was Superintendent of the Sunday School 1867-89 and an active member of the Ecclesiastical Society. He married, October 12, 1864, Louisa Keeler (5.1.3.4.2.3.5). Born to them were:
