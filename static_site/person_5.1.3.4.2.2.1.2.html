ISAAC FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.4.2.2.1.2 | Source: James
(5.1.3.4.2.2.1.2) ISAAC FERRIS (Photographs) born June 5, 1875 Hilltop Farm, Greenwich and died February 1959 Greenwich. Isaac's was 19 when father died; he probably should have been a doctor, but was a farmer through necessity. He was active in affairs of the First Congregational Church from his teens. From 1935-45, he was caretaker of the cemetery of at the Church. Isaac married, May 30, 1906 at Greenwich, May Estella Munson born January 27, 1876 at Salisbury MD and died January 9, 1917, Stamford CT. May was an exhibition typist for Mr. Blickensderfer {who made a typewriter} at the 1893 Columbian Exposition in Chicago, at age 17. [Jim - wonder if she rode on her cousin's Ferris Wheel there?] At the Fair she made contacts, which led to employment in New York City. At her marriage, she was secretary to Walter Baker of Baker Chocolate. Because she died so early, the 13 letters she wrote to her family from the Fair are the family's finest legacy. Her daughter, Margaret, transcribed them all for her nieces for Christmas, 1965. Isaac and May sang in the Church choir and ran the Christian Endeavor. Both buried in the Ferris Plot, at the cemetery at First Congregational Church, Old Greenwich. Born to them were:
