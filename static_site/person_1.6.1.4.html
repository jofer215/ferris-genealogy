DANIEL FERRIS

ID: 1.6.1.4 | Source: John
(1.6.1.4) DANIEL FERRIS born North Castle, Westchester, NY April 23, 1752 and died Bedford May 16, 1831; married 1771 Hanna (Hannah) Banks [dau of John & Deborah (Newman) Banks] from Middle Patent born North Castle November 23, 1753 and died Bedford June 22, 1824. Daniel was a farmer and lived at the foot of the hill on the Danbury Post Road just on the Bedford Town Line. He inherited his father's Bedford lands. He did sell one hundred acres to James Ferris (his son?) for $1,000.00 as evidenced by an 1827 deed. He was a private in the Westchester County militia and saw active service. He was taken prisoner by the British, and was confined to the Sugar House prison in New York and later exchanged. Hannah and Dan were buried in the Ferris Cemetery, plots 4 and 5, respectfully. From the Town of Bedford Westchester County NY Historical Records Volume VII: Houses outside Bedford Village generally escaped burning in the July 11, 1779 raid. How one such home was saved was described in this family story written down in 1885 by Mrs. William Benedict of Pleasantville as related to her by her grandparents, Daniel and Hannah Ferris. The confusion of dates is characteristic of many of these reminiscences. “In April 1777 my grand parents saw the village of Bedford burned by the British soldiers when returning from the burning of Danbury Ct. On the east side of the road in the south part of Bedford leading to White Plains was the farm house of my grandfather Daniel Ferris. The British soldiers went in the house to burn it, There being no fire to do so It having been all quenched. The three soldiers went directly to a log house at a short distance west to get a brand of fire with which to burn the farmhouse. On returning with the fire as they were nearly there, they were fired into by a party of six Americans who secreted behind the trees on the hill above the house. One of the three men fell to the ground, together with the brand of fire. His two comrades took him up in their arms, and carried him on the road south. In this way the house was rescued from being burned by the British soldier. On the road from Bedford to White Plains in the northern part of North Castle was the farmhouse of my great grand father John Ferris. The British soldiers burned his house to the ground on the same day that they burned the village of Bedford. In July 1777 Daniel Ferris my grandfather was taken prisoner by the British soldiers and imprisoned in the Sugar House in New York City where he was kept three days and three nights without food of any kind to eat. And only a pint of water each day to drink. When almost starving he was exchanged with other for British prisoners and set at liberty. I always saw and heard my grand father relate this circumstance of his life with tears”. We have another conflict here – the following contradicts the above: In the Rev. War Daniel sympathized with the British. When he married he removed to Fishkill Flats. He was a prisoner in the Sugar House in NY where he nearly starved, when he was exchanged for a British prisoner and set free. He went to Upper Canada with part of his family and remained there. This was told by Catharine (Ferris) Benedict to Mrs. Lora C. Little of Croton-on-Hudson, who was preparing a history of the Ferris Family in America. Catharine is a great niece of Daniel.
