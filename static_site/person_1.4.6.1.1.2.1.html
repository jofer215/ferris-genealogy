WILLIAM FERRIS WATERBURY

ID: 1.4.6.1.1.2.1 | Source: John
(1.4.6.1.1.2.1) WILLIAM FERRIS WATERBURY born March 1, 1851 Stamford CT and died there March 20, 1915; his boyhood days spent on his father's farm on Richmond Hill; there he remained until sixteen years of age when he entered the hardware store of S.W. Scofield & Co., at Stamford, remaining for one year. Following this he spent one year in the dry-goods store of Hubbard & Holly. Next he passed four years as clerk in the hardware store of C.E. & A.J. Holly. William then went into business for himself, forming a partnership in the clothing business with J.W.Hurlbert, under the firm name of J.W.Hurlbert & Co., two years later the firm becoming Waterbury & McDonald. From 1876-79, William was assistant town clerk and in 1878-79, he served as a borough tax collector. On July 25, 1891, he was appointed town clerk to fill the unexpired term of Charles E. Holly, and he was elected to the office the following October. William was clerk of the Probate for over ten years. Socially, he was active in various Councils and Lodges. He was active in the Church and its auxiliary societies and was a member of the Baptist Church in Stamford: being president of the Stamford Christian Endeavor Union and was the superintendent of the Sunday school. William was one of the directors of the Y.M.C.A. and was a member and registrar of the Society of Connecticut Sons of the Revolution. On December 10, 1877 William married (Carrie) Harriet Lucinda Kenworthy (1852-1921) [dau of George A & Harriet (Weed) Kenworthy]; both buried Woodland Cemetery, Stamford; and born to them were:
