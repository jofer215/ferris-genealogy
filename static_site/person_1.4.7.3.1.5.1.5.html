STEPHEN HYATT PELHAM PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.1.5.1.5 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.1.5.1.5) STEPHEN HYATT PELHAM PELL born February 3, 1874 Flushing, L.I and died 1950; at age 8, Stephen, found an 18th century tinderbox among the ruins of the old Fort Ticonderoga. The artifact ignited his desire to restore the site to its former glory. Reconstruction began in 1908; President Taft celebrated opening the museum the next year and in 1992, the Thompson-Pell Research Center was dedicated. Stephen served on the USS Yankee in the Spanish-American War. In 1917, he was paymaster in the Naval Militia and later went to France in the ambulance service where he was decorated by the French government. He was a banker and a broker in NYC in the firm of S. H. Pell & Co. He married, April 17, 1901, Sarah Gibbs Thompson [dau of Robert Means & Sarah (Gibbs) Thompson]. Born to them were:
