MAY DOHERTY

ID: 1.4.6.2.4.2.3.1 | Source: John
(1.4.6.2.4.2.3.1) MAY DOHERTY born March 12, 1896 in what is now Throggs Neck NY and died July 9, 1998, Lakewood NJ; buried in the cemetery of St. Peters Episcopal Church, only a short distance from the school house she attended as a child. She was laid to rest in a family plot that dates back to the Civil War. May attended school in a small red school house in Westchester Square. Upon completing her education, May joined with her sisters working as a school teacher in the Bronx. She is remembered as a wonderful woman who touched the lives of many through her work and preservation of the history of the Bronx. In 1955, May joined with John McNamara as founding members of the Bronx Historical Society. May remained in the Bronx until 1978, when she moved to New Jersey. When May left the Bronx she donated a treasure of family artifacts, such as ledgers, books and pictures that trace the history of the Bronx back to the Civil War. She never married.

No children recorded
