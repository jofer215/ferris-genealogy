EDWARD B. PERRY

ID: 5.1.2.9.6.2.5.2 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.6.2.5.2) EDWARD B. PERRY born c1879 Putnam Valley and died April 17, 1952 while at work on his farm on the Peekskill Hollow road at the foot of Bryant Hill road; buried in the family plot at Hillside Cemetery; an active farmer (his father’s place), granger, and supervisor of Putnam Valley for several years; born in the house in which he lived all his life. His farm was one of the oldest in Peekskill Hollow and he maintained it in top condition. He carried on milk production for several years and then raised Angus cattle. He was a life long Democrat and served the party as a member of the county committee for many years and delegate to many of the party’s conventions. He was elected supervisor of Putnam Valley several times and was also the Democrat candidate for Sheriff just before the 1920s. Upon his death, the Putnam Valley board passed a resolution that the flag at the town hall would be lowered to half-staff for a month as a tribute of respect. He was a member of the Putnam Valley Grange, the Peekskill Lodge of Elks, and the Mohegan Volunteer Fire Department. He married Bessie Mead; no offspring

No children recorded
