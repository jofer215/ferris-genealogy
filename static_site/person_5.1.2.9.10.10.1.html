GEORGE WASHINGTON HORTON

ID: 5.1.2.9.10.10.1 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.10.10.1) GEORGE WASHINGTON HORTON born October 2, 1854 Putnam Valley NY and died there February 18, 1938; married Mary J. {maiden name unk.}; Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Feb. 24, 1938: George Washington Horton, who was 83 years old last Oct. 2nd, died Friday afternoon, Feb. 18, 1938 at 1 o'clock at the home of his sister, Mrs. E. A. Tompkins, of Oscawana Lake road. Death was caused by a heart ailment. Mr. Horton was born at Lake Oscawana on Oct. 2nd, 1854, the son of James W. Horton and Anne Travis. The big lake, now known as Lake Oscawana, was then called "Horton's Pond" after his ancestors. He married Mary J. Horton, who died a number of years ago. Surviving are his sister, Mrs. E.A. Tompkins, of Oscawana Lake road; two nieces, Mrs. Harry Mosher and John B. Reinhardt, both of Putnam Valley. Mr. Horton was a prominent figure in the Town of Putnam Valley where he was active in the Oregon M.E. church, served as Justice of the Peace, Assessor, School Tax Collector and Tax Collector for the township. He was also active in the Putnam Valley Grange No. 841; Pomona Grange and the State Grange. His large farm, on the Oscawana Lake road, was sold to the McGoldrick Company, which later used it in the Lake Peekskill development. Interment was made in Hillside cemetery.
