MYRON DAVIS STEPATH

ID: 1.2.3.4.1.1.1.2.1.5.1 | Source: John
(1.2.3.4.1.1.1.2.1.5.1) MYRON DAVIS STEPATH born June 14, 1912 NY and died January 2, 2001 Lancaster Co OH; graduate of Purdue U; began his career with the New York Shipyard in Camden NJ; worked with Dupont and other companies, moving 13 times in one year coast-to-coast; developed the Arcair (see ‘Net) cutting process that is used around the world, which is a variation of arc cutting; also pioneered the design and manufacture of one of the earliest commercially produced automated computercontrolled multi-arc welding systems that provided with arc or workpiece motion to completely weld special products; Who’s Who in Advertising; Who’s Who in Finance and Industry; National President of Weimaraner Club of America 1954-56; Fellow of the American Welding Society; retired 1974 to pursue various hobbies and in 1983 started Ashwood Thoroughbreds, Inc., his horses winning more than 60 races at major US tracks; Virginia continues Ashwood Thoroughbreds as per ‘Net; married Virginia M {mnu) (Ginny) and born to them were:
