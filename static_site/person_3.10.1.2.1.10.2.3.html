NATHAN SETH NASH

ID: 3.10.1.2.1.10.2.3 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.1.10.2.3) NATHAN SETH NASH (Nathaniel) born August 22, 1839 Syrmna, Chenango, NY and died (March 21) July 26, 1923 Stockton, Jo Daviess, IL; buried Plum River Cemetery. When he was six years old, he came with his parents to JoDaviess County IL. He helped his father farm and he received a fair education in the common schools. He married 1st October 25, 1860 in Prairie de Chien WI, Mary Jane Tinkham [dau of John Jr. & Elizabeth (Drake) Tinkham] born January 17, 1844 Nankin Township, Wayne Co MI and died September 7, 1900 at the old homestead in Yankee Hollow (Elmoville IL); also buried Plum River Cemetery. After they married, they moved to Humboldt County IA for a few years, but then moved back to Illinois. Nathan learned the blacksmith trade and was many years as a blacksmith, in addition to farming. He was the Clerk of Pleasant Valley township, being elected to that position in 1888. It was his privilege to assist in building five different churches. After Mary died, Nathan married, 1908, in Columbus OH, Jennie Harvey and they lived in Stockton until her death in 1921. Born to Nathan and Mary were:
