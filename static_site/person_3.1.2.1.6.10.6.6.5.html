ELLEN FERRIS

ID: 3.1.2.1.6.10.6.6.5 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.2.1.6.10.6.6.5) ELLEN FERRIS born November 22, 1913 Salt Lake City and died there October 14, 2000; married September 21, 1937 in the Salt Lake City LDS Temple, Sterling King Hixson. From Ellen’s obit: Bountiful - Ellen Ferris Hixon, age 86, passed away at the LDS Hospital in Salt Lake City, Utah on Saturday, October 14, 2000. Born November 22, 1913 in Salt Lake City, daughter of William Sherman Ferris, Sr., and Grace Leola Scott Ferris; married Sterling King Hixon September 21, 1937 in the Salt Lake City LDS Temple. Ellen was a devoted and loving wife, mother, and grandmother. Her family and home were her most important concerns and occupied her great efforts. She was a good student and excelled in sports, especially baseball, hurdling, and skating. She enjoyed dancing (winning a coral necklace in competition), painting, yard work, gardening, sewing (receiving blue ribbons in both Idaho and Utah State Fairs) and crafts of all kinds - hammers and nails were some of her most important tools. She attended beauty college, working at ZCMI and The Paris Company department stores for 20 years. She was a faithful member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, serving in many callings, including the stake Sunday School and stake Young Women’s presidency. Ellen and Sterling served a mission together in the Netherlands from 1985 to 1987. Survived by children, Richard Sterling Hixon (Luan), Judith Ellen Hixon Kuepper (Michael), Robert Lee Hixon (Shara), and Joan Hixon Tibbet (Alan); 22 grandchildren and 26 great-grandchildren. Proceeded in death by her husband, parents, three sisters, two brothers, one grandchild and one great-grandchild.
