HENRY WHELPLEY

ID: 5.1.3.5.9 | Source: James
(5.1.3.5.9) HENRY WHELPLEY (Captain) born December 1772 (c1761) Greenwich CT and died there July 17, 1865; 1803 he was listed as one of the owners of the vessel Mary, of Greenwich; the other owners were Solomon Peck (Master), Samuel Ferris and Ethan Ferris. In 1826, he was the Captain of the Emeline, which was one of the packet boats (carried fresh produce) running from Mianus to New York. He became a member of the Methodist Episcopal Church, Stamford Circuit, Cos Cob CT, 1827. He married, first, name unknown; second, December 14, 1816, widow Deborah (Marshall) Mosher born February 21, 1774 and died June 19, 1847.
