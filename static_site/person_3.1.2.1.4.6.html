MOSES SLAWSON

ID: 3.1.2.1.4.6 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.2.1.4.6) MOSES SLAWSON born September 3, 1780 Salem, Orange, NY and died November 29, 1848 Jefferson Co PA; buried there Butler Cemetery, Brookville, Jefferson, PA; married c1803 in Salem, Westchester, NY Mary Polly (Pauley) [dau of Hugh & Mary Polly] born February 22, 1787 Salem, Westchester, NY and died July 25, 1859 Potter Co PA; buried Ellisburg Cemetery, Potter PA (no cemetery by this name listed). Moses was a carpenter by trade; his youngest son Moses Jr., speaking of his father, 1918: “My father was a man of some ability in the acquiring of property, but lacked the faculty of taking care of it. He was swindled out of a farm in Luzerne County PA, for which he had taken Judgement Notes, having through slack of business methods failed to have them recorded, which resulted in a complete loss of the property as he never recovered one penny through the transaction. He was possessed of a considerable genius in the writing of verse, which he uttered of time in the exploiting of his friends and acquaintances, but it was of no profit to him.” Born to them were (not in birth sequence):
