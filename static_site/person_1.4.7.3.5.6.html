EMMA WALDEN died 1886 NYC; married Rev. Samuel Cooke, D.D.,

ID: 1.4.7.3.5.6 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.5.6) EMMA WALDEN died 1886 NYC; married Rev. Samuel Cooke, D.D., [son of Joseph P Cooke] [there are records of marriages being conducted at St. Bartholomew’s Church, Westchester Co by Rev Cooke where he was Rector, i.e., 1853, 1865] [www.stbarts.org for history] born August 15, 1815 Danbury CT; graduate of the General Theological Seminary; ordered Deacon in St Mark’s Church in the Bowery July 1, 1838; began his active ministry at Lyons, in the diocese of Western NY, where he was instrumental in building the church in that parish; from Lyons he became rector of Trinity Church, Geneva, in the diocese of Western NY. On July 2, 1845, he was elected the first rector of St Paul’s Church, New Haven CT where he remained until he came to St Bartholomew’s; he was greatly gifted as a pastor; sharing the jobs and tenderly ministering to the sorrows of his people who loved and trusted him – see The Centennial History of Saint Bartholomew’s Church. On the occasion of Emma’s death, the Vestry of St. Bartholomnew’s adopted the following Memorial Minute: ‘The Vestry of St. Bartholomew’s Church desire by this Minute to place on the records their regret on hearing of the death of Mrs Cooke, and their sincere appreciation of her Christian life and of the faithful aid she rendered in the work of the Parish as long as her health permitted. Her memory will always be kept alive by those who sought her in times of difficulty and sorrow, and who ever found in her a true, warm-hearted friend and adviser.’ Born to them were 3 kids, 2 of which were:
