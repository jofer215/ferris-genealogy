EARLE MAYNARD BOARDMAN

ID: 5.1.3.1.2.6.6.3.1.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.2.6.6.3.1.1) EARLE MAYNARD BOARDMAN born January 26, 1922 New Haven Township, Huron Co OH and died February 3, 2002 Greeley CO; married 1st January 23, 1946 at Pueblo CO, Beverly Jean Harriss [day of Joseph & Eva (Crispin) Harriss] born March 5, 1920 LaJunta CO and died September 4, 1987 Denver CO; married 2d June 4, 1988 at Greeley CO, “Babette” Rachel Violet Martin [dau of Albert & Winnifred (Kennedy) Martin] born October 15, 1926 Banbridge, County Down, Ireland. Earle was educated in the schools of North Fairfield OH, graduating in 1940; attended Kent State U., for two years until drafted into the Army Air Corps - served 3 years mostly with the 8th AF stationed in Ipswich, England. After the War he attended Oberlin College and received a Batchelor of Music Degree. He met Beverly while stationed at an AF school in Greeley CO. In the ensuring years he taught music in Greeley, Bowling Green KY (Western Kentucky State U.); Mount Clemens MI; Kearney NE; and Plattsville WI (University of Wisconsin-Platteville). In 1972 he left teaching, moved to Greeley, and established his business as a piano technician until he retired in 1999. Over the years, Earle also worked as a performing musician, having been principle cellist of the Nashville Orchestra (TN) and Sarnia, Canada. He soloed with a number of community orchestras, worked in the recording studios in Nashville and toured a short time through the South with Liberace. He was also a published composer and has been a member of the ASCAP since 1965. They reside in Greeley CO. Earle was the provider of much of the information on this ‘twig’ of the Tree - thanks Earle!
