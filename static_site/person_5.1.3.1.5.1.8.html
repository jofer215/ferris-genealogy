JAMES MONROE FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.1.5.1.8 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.5.1.8) JAMES MONROE FERRIS born February 23, 1832 Columbus NY and died December 29, 1923 Frederika IA; married June 7, 1856 at Earlville NY, Frances "Fannie" J. Dutcher born June 7, 1837 Ithaca NY and died July 5, 1917 Frederika IA. James enlisted, at Smyrna NY, January 11, 1864, as a Private in Company H., NY 5th Heavy Artillery for 3 years, at the age of 31. James received a bounty of $323.00 for his enlistment. He was involved in the following engagements: the Defense of Washington, Piedmont VA; Lexington VA; Buchanan; Liberty; Salem; Harpers Ferry; Lynchburg; Snickers Ferry; Martinsburg; Winchester VA; Charleston WVA; Halltown VA; Berryville; Opeguan Creek; Fishers Hill; and Cedar Creek. At the battle of Cedar Creek, on October 19, 1864, James became a POW; he was moved to Richmond and taken to Libby Prison. As fate would have it, James spent only 3 days at the infamous Libby Prison, before he was transferred to Pemberton Prison where he spent the next four months. He was paroled from Pemberton, February 15, 1865 and was mustered out of the Army, June 10, 1865 at Harpers Ferry WVA. James and Fannie left Chenango County NY after his Civil War service and settled in Douglas Township, Bremer County IA on March 18, 1867, where they lived until the fall of 1893, when they went to make their home in Frederika. Born to them were two sons and six daughters: (5.1.3.1.5.1.8.1) IDA MAY FERRIS born c1858 and died September 19, 1862 Smyrna NY; buried Smyrna East Cemetery, aka, Baptist Cemetery, Smyrna
