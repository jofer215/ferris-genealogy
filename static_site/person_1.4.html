JAMES FERRIS

ID: 1.4 | Source: John
(1.4) JAMES FERRIS (c1673-c1746/47) [will dated August 16, 1746, proved April 14, 1747] {born c1682 Throg’s Neck and died there before April 17, 1747} was proprietor of “The Homestead” at Throgg's Neck NY, March 9, 1695 [He took oath of allegiance to the King of England, January 31, 1692] [mentioned in his sfather’s will]; married c1715 Anna Sands (born c1695 Block Island, Washington Co, Rhode Island and died after August 17, 1746 Throg’s Neck age 51) of Sand's Point, Long Island; daughter of John and Catherine (Gutteridge) Sands, granddaughter of James, and had, according to one source ten children, another says nine, which Chaplain Ferris names. One source says Anna’s parents were Samuel & Mary (Ray) Sands, grand-daughter of James and Sarah (Walker) Sands. “Will: In the name of God, Amen, I, JAMES FERRIS, of Westchester, yeoman, I leave to my eldest son, James Ferris, all my lands, houses, messuages, and improvements, and my privilege of Commage, Provided he faithfully comply with the conditions hereafter mentioned. I leave to my wife Ann, a negro wench, and a good feather bed, with curtains and furniture, also the great looking glass and 6 silver spoons, and a silver tumbler, also my pacing bay mare and all linen and household goods. My wife and my 4 daughters, Martha, Mercy, Elizabeth, and Anne, shall have the use of the west half of my dwelling house and garden, and the privilege of my yard and well for raising poultry, so long as they remain unmarried; And they shall have out of my orchard as many apples for eating and for cider, as they need. I leave to my daughter Martha, oe100 and a bed. To my daughter Mercy, oe100 and a bed. To my son Benjamin, oe250, when of age. To my daughter Elizabeth, oe60, when 18. To my son John, oe250, when 21. To my daughter Anne, oe50. To my daughters Mercy and Martha, all they linnen they claim as theirs. I leave to my daughter Mary, wife of Caleb Pell, 5 shillings, as I have already provided for her. I leave to my son James all horses and cattle and farming utensils, and he is to provide for my wife 15 pounds of good wool, and 40 pounds of good swingled fax, yearly, and beef and pork, and her firewood, always ready at the door; Also the milk. I make Theodosius Bartow, John Bartow, and Basil Bartow, executors. Dated August 16, 1746, Witnesses, Barton Nathaniel.” Born to them were (not in birth sequence):
