EBENEZER LOCKWOOD

ID: 4.6.1.5 | Source: Mary
(4.6.1.5) EBENEZER LOCKWOOD (#239) (Major) born March 31, 1737 Stamford CT and died July 29, 1821 Pound Ridge NY; married (consort) 1st February 16, 1761 Hannah Smith [dau of Ezra Smith & Mary Weed] who died May 3, 1787 at age 45 years; married 2d Sarah Waring who died March 3, 1821 at age 67 years. Ebenezer’s early years were spent on the farm with his father, engaged in agricultural pursuits. After his father’s death, the estate of some 400-500 acres were divided among himself and his older brother. At the age of 25 he was engaged in merchandise, and was appointed one of his Majesty’s Justices of the Peace and one of the Quorum, which offices he held until the commencement of the Revolutionary War. On October 19, 1775, he was commissioned a Major in the 2d Regiment of Westchester County Militia, commanded by Colonel Thomas Thomas, and was in active service during most of the campaign of ‘76, when the enemy took possession of the lower part of Westchester County. In May, 1776, he attended the Provincial Congress then sitting in New York, having been elected to that office on the third Tuesday in April previous for one year, and was in continued served from that period until the close of the war, either as Major, Member of the Provincial Congress, Committee of Safety, and other posts of active duty in favor of American Independence. After the Declaration of Independence he was elected a member of the Provincial Convention for forming a constitution of Government for the State, and as such aided in the formation and adoption of the first Constitution and was returned as a member of the Legislature for several years after the close of the war. His unwearied zeal and activity in the American cause also was accompanied with much pecuniary loss and sacrifice of property. On July 3, 1779, a detachment of British troops under the command of Colonel Tarleton made a sudden descent upon the village of Pound Ridge and one of the objects of the attack was the capture of Major Lockwood, for whom a reward of forty guineas had been offered. His house was burnt with most of its contents, Hannah was cut and maltreated, and the livestock was driven off. After the close of the War, he and his family returned to his farm, converted a barn into a dwelling and was honored with the appointment of first Judge of County Court. Member 2d Board of Regents of the University of New York.
