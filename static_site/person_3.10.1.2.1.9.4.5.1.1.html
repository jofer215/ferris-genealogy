SARAH FRANCES DARBY

ID: 3.10.1.2.1.9.4.5.1.1 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.1.9.4.5.1.1) SARAH FRANCES DARBY born July 26, 1890 Clarksville, Iowa and died there 1940; married December 31, 1913 at Nashua IA, Charles Cross born May 19, 1884 Plainfield, Iowa and died November 1987, (103y?) Gwynn Oak, Maryland Raymond Darby writes: "We also heard about a "Marie" Cross, as children, probably Charlie's sister, but, she might have been Charlie's Dad Fred's sister. Charlie was born near Gramma Sutter's farm in Fremont Twp., just a quarter mile west and a half-mile south. When we were kids, there was a large piece of tin, probably metal roofing that had blown onto Gramma Sutter's farm near the back border. They always said that had blown there in the 1919 "Cyclone", from Marie Cross' farm buildings. There was a picture in Katie Darby's collection, probably taken at the Pleasant Valley Church where the Sutter's used to go, along with the Crosses, or possibly it was taken at the train station platform - hard to tell in the photo. The photo shows Fred Cross, Charlie's Dad, in the middle, with denim jacket & jeans. Next to him, Charlie's mother, & Charlie Cross. Rev. Van Loon also appears to be in the photo. They may have been here to pick him up Rev. Van Loon, or see him off on a train? When they were in Nashua we only knew Charlie's parents, farmers living close to Gramma Sutter's farm in Butler Co. Charlie & Fannie & Family, lived at the end of the street that went by the Nashua Hall, and on the other side lived Charlie's parents. Charlie & Fannie had an acreage and had a goat dairy, probably at least 20 goats in their herd.”

No children recorded
