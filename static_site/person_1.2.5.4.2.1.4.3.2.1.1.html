LEE CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.2.1.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.2.1.1) LEE CHADEAYNE (Photos) born December 22, 1933 White Plains NY; married June 23, 1962 at Carmel NY, Evelyn Anna Schumann [dau of Paul Armin & Elfrieda (Wehrkamp) Schumann] born September 8, 1937 New York City. Lee was raised by his father Leander and aunt Miriam at 19 Clinton Avenue, Ossining, NY from the early death of his mother until his father's second marriage to Augusta Kleindienst in 1943. The family then resided at 30 Sherwood Avenue in Ossining. Lee attended the Ossining Public schools, and later The Choate School and Hamilton College (1951-1953). From 1953 to 1956 he was a tour director for various European symphony orchestras on tour in the U.S. (Berlin Philharmonic, Amsterdam Concertgebouw, London Philharmoncia, others). He received his bachelors and masters degrees from Columbia University in 1959 and 1961 respectively, and completed additional work on the post-masters level at Syracuse University, Middlebury College, Ohio State University, and the Univ. des Saarlandes in Germany. From 1960 through 1988 he taught German and French at the high school level and German at the college level at Hobart and William Smith Colleges (1960-1963); Ohio State University (1963-1973); Boston University (1978-1981); and Northeastern University (1984-1987). From 1981 to 1984 he had a full-time position as a linguist for Logos Corporation of Waltham, Massachusetts developing German and English language translation software. In 1987 he started his own company, which did language translation work via e-mail. The firm continued to grow and became his full-time activity after 1989. The company went by the names Chadeayne Associates, Global Translations and later WORDNET, Inc. The firm employed five people in 1996. Evelyn taught in a number of schools in the West Suburban area of Boston and most recently (1986 - 2000) has been working at the Kennedy Middle School in Waltham, teaching as well as developing individual educational programs for Special Needs students.
