ROLLO STEWART BASSETT

ID: 5.1.3.8.2.2.3.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.8.2.2.3.1) ROLLO STEWART BASSETT born March 9, 1871/72 Cincinnati OH and died April 10, 1941 probably Champaign IL; married September 13, 1893 Ethel Sibley Benham [dau of Henry Laurens & Mary Huldah (Sibley) Benham] born October 5, 1873 Cincinnati and died 1953. Rollo attended local schools at Cincinnati, and at the age of sixteen began working with his father in the lumber business. Later he attended high school and also the Art and Mechanical Institute of Cincinnati, where he perfected himself in mechanical designing and illustrating. Mr. Bassett at the age of twentytwo went to Chicago and was connected with a wholesale milling concern in that city until 1908. In that year he came to Champaign as his headquarters, and as district manager for the Alexander Lumber Company his territory covered Rantoul, Villa Grove, Penfield, Royal, Mansfield, Sidell, Champaign and Decatur. Rollo was an accomplished musician, and while in Chicago he served as organist for the First Congregational Church. He was deacon in the First Congregational Church of Champaign.
