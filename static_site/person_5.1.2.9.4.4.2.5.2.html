ELIZABETH SMITH

ID: 5.1.2.9.4.4.2.5.2 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.4.4.2.5.2) ELIZABETH SMITH born May 13, 1887 Carmel NY and died April 21, 1945 Croton-on-Hudson NY; married Lewis G. Ferguson [son of Thomas & Kathryn] born January 26, 1886 and died April 11, 1957; both buried Kelley Cemetery, Carmel NY. Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, April 26, 1945: Mrs. Elizabeth Smith Ferguson, wife of Lewis Ferguson of White Plains, and formerly a resident of this village, died suddenly of a heart attack on Saturday evening, April 21, 1945, at the home of her brother-in-law, Mortimer Ferguson, in Croton-on-Hudson, where she had been spending two weeks owing to the illness of her husband. Mrs. Ferguson suffered a stroke about a year ago shortly after the death of a daughter and since then had failed gradually. However, on Saturday evening she was conversing with other members of the family after supper when she suddenly complained of feeling ill and died within a few minutes. Her age was 58. The deceased was one of a family of six children born to the late Alexander and Annie Rhodes Smith. Her birth occurred at their home west of this village on May 13, 1887. She attended the Carmel high school and was graduated in the class of 1906. After her graduation she taught school for a few years in the Field district in Patterson and for several years was employed in the general store of Charles P. Cole in this village. About 37 years ago she was united in marriage with Lewis Ferguson and twelve years later they moved to White Plains where they had since resided. She was a member of the Dutch Reformed church at Elmsford and superintendent of the Sunday school for several years. She was also a member of the Kings Daughters Circle there. Surviving besides her husband are one daughter, Mrs. Evelyn Villotoro of Astoria, L.I., a teacher of nursing in the hospital there; two sons, S2/c Lewis Ferguson, who had recently entered the service and was able to return home, and GM3/c Charles Ferguson, who was on a ship somewhere in the Pacific and could not be reached at the time of his mothers death. She also is survived by three sisters, Mrs. Nettie Baldwin, Mrs. Grace Adams and Mrs. Clara M. Mulfeldt, all of Danbury, and two brothers, Samuel Smith of Tarrytown, and James of Danbury Interment was made in the family plot in the Kelley Cemetery the brother-in-laws serving as pallbearers. Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Thursday, April 18, 1857: Lewis G. Ferguson, a resident of Carmel several years during his younger life, died of a heart attack last Thursday, April 11, 1957, at his home on the Felix M. Warburg estate near Harstdale. His age was 72. The deceased was a son of Thomas and Kathryn Ferguson and born in the town of Patterson, being one of eleven children. He came to Carmel at an early age and served as custodian of Carmel High School when the school was located on Brewster Avenue. In Carmel he was united in marriage with Miss Elizabeth Smith, whose death occurred 13 years ago. He left Carmel 28 years ago and had since been employed as a horticulturist on the Warburg estate. About ten years ago he married Mrs. Lillian Otley Venables. Mr. Ferguson was an elder of the Elmsford Reformed church and a member of the Get-Together club in Tarrytown. Surviving are his wife; two sons, Lewis G. Jr., of North Tarrytown and Charles Ferguson of Yonkers; a daughter, Mrs. Evelyn Merson of Astoria, L.I.; a brother, Robert of Elmsford; and three sisters, Mrs. Charles Luyster of Deer Park, L.I. and Mrs. Isaac Ballard and Mrs. Homer Sprague, both of Holmes. Interment was in the family plot in Kelley Cemetery in Carmel, which he was instrumental in incorporating some years ago. Born to them were: (5.1.2.9.4.4.2.5.2.1) KATHERINE FERGUSON born March 8, 1909 and died May 27, 1944; buried Kelley Cemetery, Carmel NY
