FRANCIS T NICHOLLS

ID: 1.4.8.4.2.1.8 | Source: John
(1.4.8.4.2.1.8) FRANCIS T NICHOLLS (Francis Redding Tillou Nicholls) (Photos) born August 20, 1834 Donaldsonville, Ascension Parish LA and died January 4, 1912; graduated from the US Military Academy, 1855, 12th in a class of 34; commissioned a Brevet 2d Lieutenant of the 2d Artillery Regiment and served briefly at Fort Myers FL and California; resigned his commission in 1856 due to health problems and studied law at the U of Louisiana (later Tulane U), but left before he gained a degree, to pass the bar and open a practice in Napoleonville; married April 26, 1860 at Ridgefield Plantation, Thibodaux LA, Caroline Zilpha Guion [dau of George Seth & Caroline Lucretia (Winder) Guion] born June 21, 1840 and died March 30, 1930 Ridgefield Plantation; both buried St John’s Episcopal Cemetery, Thibodaux LA; in 1861, he helped raise an infantry company and was elected Captain; later appointed Lieutenant Colonel of the 8th Louisiana Infantry Regiment and fought at the first Battle of Bull Run; May 25, 1862, lost his left arm at the Battle of Winchester; May 4, 1863, lost his left foot at the Battle of Chancellorsville where he commanded the 2d Louisiana Brigade as a Brigadier General; in 1864, he defended Lynchburg VA, and then controlled the Conscript Bureau of the Trans-Mississippi Department until the War ended; 1865, resumed his law practice in Assumption Parish; in 1865 he was nominated for Governor by the Democrats but was not elected; in 1876 he took the Governor’s seat of S B Packard, established a de facto government and was later recognized as Governor by the federal government as part of the Compromise of 1877 – see Louisiana history; 1892-1904 served as Chief Justice of the Louisiana Supreme Court; 1904-11 served as an Associate Justice of the LA Supreme Court; and born to them were:
