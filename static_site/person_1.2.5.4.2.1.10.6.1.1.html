MARY GENEVIEVE LEE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.10.6.1.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.10.6.1.1) MARY GENEVIEVE LEE born January 10, 1934 Peekskill NY; married November 14, 1959 at Lake Mohegan NY, Kenneth Michael Gellhaus [son of Herbert & Adele (Rohrer) Gellhaus] born October 22, 1927 Cincinnati OH. Ken graduated from Baltimore Polytechnical Institute in 1945; got his Bachelor of Electrical Engineering from Cornell U., 1949. He was drafted in 1946 and spent a year in the US Army Air Force. He received his Masters in Electrical Engineering, Drexel U., 1964. He worked as an editoral assistant and assistant editor of Electrical World at McGraw Hill in NYC; he was technical writer and, later, manager of the Editoral Branch of the Naval Ordnance Laboratory in Silver Springs MD, 1951-57. He joined UNIVAC in Philadelphia, 1957, as publications engineer. In 1963, he went to IBM where he worked as a software writer in their lab in Poughkeepsie and Kingston NY. He became publications manager, 1964. He took an assignment as publications manager for IBM, Hursley, Hampshire, England, December 1968 to July 1971. Several years after he and his family returned from England, he left the Publications Department to do computer programming at IBM Kingston. He retired from IBM in March 1990. Since 1995, he has been a volunteer tutor with SeniorNet, a computer instruction program for seniors. He is a member of St. John’s Episcopal Church, Kingston. Mary graduated from Keuka College, Penn Yan NY, 1954, BA. She worked in advertising in Rochester NY and Philadelphia PA for six years. She worked as a substitute in the Kingston Consolidated School District, 1985-91. She has been active in the Kingston branch of the American Association of University Women, serving as President, 1989-92. She was the newsletter editor of the Ulster Literacy Association, 1992-97; member of St. John’s Episcopal Church, Kingston and served on its vestry for 3 years; she has volunteer at the Huguenot Historical Society Library, New Paltz NY since 1995; member of the Huguenot Society, the Gerow Family Association, and the genealogical societies of Orange, Ulster, and Westchester Counties.
