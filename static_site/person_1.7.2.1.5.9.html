RICHARD LAWRENCE SCHIEFFELIN

ID: 1.7.2.1.5.9 | Source: John
(1.7.2.1.5.9) RICHARD LAWRENCE SCHIEFFELIN born November 9, 1801 and died November 21, 1889; at age 18 joined the militia and upon retirement held the rank of Brigadier General in command of the 82d, 197th, 236th, and 274th Regiments of the New York State Infantry; graduate 1819 Columbia College; studied law with his brother-in- law, Benjamin Ferris and was admitted to the Bar 1823; practice chancery law and retired in 1843; administered the estates of his father, of Benjamin Ferris, and his fatherin-law, George McKay; although he took little part in politics, in 1844 he was elected alderman for the 15th Ward and held the presidency of both the Board of Alderman and of the Common Council; a steadfast opponent of Tammany Hall, he published in the local press numerous articles against that organization; he was present as secretary at the meeting in 1823 that founded St. Mary’s Church and at the time of h is death was its senior warden; owner of numerous properties in New York City; married August 3, 1833 Helen Margaret McKay born August 2, 1813 and died December 13, 1892 [dau of Captain George Knox McKay]; and born to them were:

Children (3)

  • GEORGE RICHARD SCHIEFFELIN 1.7.2.1.5.9.3
