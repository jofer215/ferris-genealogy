BARTOW UNDERHILL

ID: 1.2.3.3 | Source: John
(1.2.3.3) BARTOW UNDERHILL born October 23, 1725 Westchester Co NY and died before January 5, 1787; married Sarah {mnu}; he was a farmer of Cortlandt Manor, Westchester, NY in the area known as Yorktown; at the time of the French-Indian Wars, he was an Ensign in a Militia Company in the North Battalion of Westchester under the Captain Jacob Teller 1760 and on the eve of the Revolution was a Captain of the same unit. In April 1775, he was among the Westchester Tories who met at White Plains to protest and pledged their loyalty to the King; went to Pictou, Prince Edward’s Island; and born to them were:
