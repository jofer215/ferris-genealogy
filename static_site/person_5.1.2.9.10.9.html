EBENEZER COLE TRAVIS

ID: 5.1.2.9.10.9 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.10.9) EBENEZER COLE TRAVIS born May 23, 1829 Putnam Valley NY and died August 12, 1904 Peekskill NY; married Phebe J. Shelley. Obituary, Highland Democrat, Peekskill, NY, Aug. 20, 1904: Ebenezer Cole Travis, a life long resident of this section, died at his home, No. 209 Nelson avenue, on Friday evening of last week in the 76th year of his age. The immediate cause of his death was paralysis. Mr. Travis born in Putnam County, May 23, 1829, and was one of a twelve children of William Travis and Ann Chapman both natives of Putnam County. From early youth he was a farmer and his entire life with the exception of the past two years has been spent on the farm; about fourteen years in Westchester County and the remainder in Putnam. In 1853 he was married to Miss Phebe J. Shelley. The deceased led a most exemplary Christian life; when a young man he united with the Putnam Valley M.E. Church. Later he transferred his membership to the Shrub Oak M.E. Church and was for many years active in the life of the church. He was a great Sunday school worker and had organized several Sunday schools, acting as their superintendents. He held the office of class leader and also steward for many years and his good advice and bright testimony has led many to lead better and purer lives. He was a Republican in politics but had never held an office. About two years ago Mr. Travis moved with his family to Peekskill to the house where he died. He united with the First M.E. Church here and has live a retired life on account of failing health. The deceased leaves a wife and one daughter and three sons to mourn the loss of a loving and kind husband and father. He is also survived by two sisters and one brother, besides a host of friends. The interment was at Adams Corners. (1900 Census-Had 6 children, with 4 living) Born to them were:
