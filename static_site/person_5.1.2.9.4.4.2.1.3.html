(5.1.2.9.4.4.2.1.3) IDA BELL SMITH Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, April 28, 1960: Mrs. Ida Bell Roscoe, widow of Sherman Roscoe, died Monday, April 25, 1960, at her home in Raymond Hill Park following an illness of 10 months after she suffered a stroke. Her age was 77. The deceased was a daughter of the late Orlando and Sarah Bennett Smith and she born in the town of Kent, March 24, 1883. In 1901 she was united in marriage with Sherman Roscoe and they resided in Kent until 29 years ago when they moved to a new home in Raymond Hill Park, Carmel. Mr. Roscoe died on Feb. 6, 1957. She was a member of the Mt. Carmel Baptist Church. Surviving are two daughters, Mrs. George Lockwood of Carmel and Mrs. Alonzo Williams of Somers. She also leaves two sisters, Mrs. William Harkins of Mahopac and Mrs. Jay Nelson of Carmel and three brothers, Chauncey Smith of Danbury, George Smith of Brewster and Charles Smith of Mahopac and three grandchildren. Interment was made in the family plot in Raymond Hill cemetery. Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Thursday, February 7, 1957: Sherman Roscoe, a lifelong resident of this vicinity, died on Wednesday morning, February 6, 1957, following an illness of five years, He was in his 75th year. The deceased was a son of Stephen R. and Mary Worden Roscoe and born in the town of Kent, May 5, 1882. He attended the district school near the homestead and at an early date began his life work as a painter. In 1901 he was united in marriage with Miss Ida Smith and 26 years ago they moved to a new home in Raymond Hill Park. Mr. Roscoe painted many of the residences in this village during the past 40 years, having a list of home owners to which he returned every few years when a home needed repainting and continued this work until illness compelled him to retire. Surviving are his wife, and two daughters, Mrs. George Lockwood, Sr., of Carmel and Mrs. Alonzo Williams of Somers; a brother, Herbert Roscoe of Yorktown Heights; and three grandchildren.
