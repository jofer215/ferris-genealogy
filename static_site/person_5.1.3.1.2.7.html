STEPHEN GANO FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.1.2.7 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.2.7) STEPHEN GANO FERRIS born August 11, 1788 Hillsdale, Columbia, NY and died November 29, 1876 Fountain Green (Carthage) IL; buried in the village graveyard; at the age of 4, he accompanied his family to their removal to North Norwich, Chenango Co NY, then a frontier county and quite sparsely settled. Here, he learned the tanner and currier’s trade and followed that business until his father moved to Yates Co NY, probably in 1810-12. About 1820, he moved to a farm near the town of Howard, Steuben Co NY, which was then a frontier county. Here he remained until the spring of 1832, when he departed for Illinois, where he was one of the founders of Fountain Green, Hancock, IL. The family, consisting of Stephen, his wife and six children, arrived in Illinois, in December, 1832 at Trader’s Point, which at that time comprised 3 or 4 log cabins; 3 or 4 traders and their families and 100 or so Indians and half-breeds. They arrived in Lick Grove (Fountain Green), December 13, 1832 where he farmed 110 acres of land and resided until 1846, when he sold his estate and made his home with his son, in that city. In early life, he became a member of the Baptist Church and maintained that membership until his death. He married August 11, 1814 in Chenango Co NY, Eunice Beebe born May 12, 1788 New London CT and died September 14, 1860. Born to them were: (5.1.3.1.2.7.1) JOHN MILTON FERRIS born 1816 Chenango Co NY; John went to Illinois with his family 1832 and resided at home until he was 21 years of age when he began the practice of law; was Justice of the Peace for many years and County Judge for 8 years.
