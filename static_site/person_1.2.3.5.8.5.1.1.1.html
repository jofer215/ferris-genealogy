WILLIAM RUDOLPH SLAYTER

ID: 1.2.3.5.8.5.1.1.1 | Source: John
(1.2.3.5.8.5.1.1.1) WILLIAM RUDOLPH SLAYTER (Sir) (1896-) joined Royal Navy 1909; 1913-16 HMS Queen Mary (battlecruiser sunk at Battle of Jutland 1916); 1916-18 Coastal Motor Boats (raid on Ostend 1918); 1923-1925 Gunnery Officer, HMS Constance (light cruiser); 1925-26 HMS Pembroke; 1926-27 XO & Gunnery Officer, HMS Broke; 1928-1930 Gunnery Officer, HMS Repulse; 1931-32 XO, Gunnery School, Chatham; 1934-36 XO HMS Devonshire; 1937 Staff College; 1938 HMS President; 1938-40 Assistant Director of Naval Ordnance Admiralty; 1940-41 Deputy Director of Naval Ordnance Admiralty; 1941 Director Naval Ordnance Admiralty; 1941-42 Commanding Officer, HMS Liverpool; 1942-43 Commanding Officer, HMS Newfoundland; 1943-44 Chief of Staff to Commander-in-Chief, Home Fleet; 1945-46 Commanding Officer, Gunnery School, Portsmouth; 1947 Chief of Staff, Staff of Naval Representative, UN; 1948 Washington, D.C. & Naval Representative of the UK on the UN Military Staff Committee; 1949-50 Flag Officer Commanding, 2d Cruiser Squadron (HMS Superb); 1950-52 Admiral Commanding Reserves; 1952-54 Commander-inChief, East Indies Station (HMS Highflyer); married 1925 Helen Justine Hale (-1969) [dau of Major Russell Hale] and they had a son

No children recorded
