CHARLES HENRY PALMER

ID: 3.1.3.10.5.1.4.1.2 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.3.10.5.1.4.1.2) CHARLES HENRY PALMER born November 2, 1920 St. Paul MN and died February 2, 1992 Kensett IA; married April 13, 1945 at Reno NV, Jane Ellen Randall born September 29, 1927 Omaha NE; Charles was drafted into the Army 1943 and served in the Philippines and Borneo and went into Japan with MacArthur; during this time he was with Army Ordnance and Army Intelligence, remaining in Japan until 1948 at which time he was a First Sergeant/ Sergeant Major; upon his return to the States he was assigned to the Presidio of San Francisco until 1951, when he went to Korea, where he remained until 1953. His next assignment was as an advisor the California National Guard in San Francisco and Los Angeles. In 1955, he was sent to Germany where he was stationed at Stuggartt and Hanau. In 1960, he returned to the States and was an advisor to the Nevada National Guard in Carson City NV. In 1961, he was again sent to Germany, being stationed at Gelnhausen, Hanau, and Nelligen. Charles was offered two field commissions but elected to stay with him men where he felt as a leader he could do more good. He retired from the U. S. Army after serving 23 years. [Jane E.
