GIDEON FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.8.6 | Source: James
(5.1.3.8.6) GIDEON FERRIS born January 13, 1795 Greenwich and killed by a train while walking on the tracks, near his home, in Stamford on October 15,1875; buried 1st Congregational Church Cemetery, Greenwich. Gideon was a lifelong agriculturist (farmer?), cultivating his large farm in Greenwich, which was situated between the Mianus River and Greenwich cove. He was a publicspirited citizen, and served his township faithfully as selectman and assessor. From 1857 he gave his political support to the Republican Party and he was an Episcopalian in religious faith. After his brother John, went to Ohio, Gideon made several trips there on horseback. Gideon married, February 14, 1825, Caroline Betts (*) born September 4, 1797 and died March 2, 1843 [daughter of Frederick and Hannah (Sackett) Betts]. In 1872, Gideon sold his farm at Riverside, Greenwich and moved to Stamford to live with his daughter, Caroline. He had acquired several farms in the town of DeWitt, Onondaga County NY and gave them to his sons. Early resident of DeWitt NY. Born to them were:
