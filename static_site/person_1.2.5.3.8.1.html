HANNAH QUIMBY

ID: 1.2.5.3.8.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.3.8.1) HANNAH QUIMBY born December 19, 1802 (1808) North Castle NY and died April 1, 1864; married August 19, 1829 at North Castle, Daniel Griffen Haviland [son of William & Anna (Griffen) Haviland] born October 16, 1799 Purchase NY and died July 30, 1864 Brooklyn NY; buried Friend’s Cemetery, Prospect Park. Daniel and his brother, David, formed the firm of D.G. & D. Haviland at 47 John Street, NYC, afterwards Haviland Brothers & Co., French China Importers. When another brother, Robert, was taken into the firm, David with his wife and one child went to France about 1840 and established the French House, which separated from the American house about the beginning of the Civil War. In 1864 the American House of Haviland Brothers and Co., failed, but the French house prospered becoming the largest manufacturer of French china in the world. On December 13, 1838, Hannah Haviland, wife of Daniel G., received a removal certificate from Chappaqua to New York City with four minor children, Caroline, Richard, George, and Albert; she had one for herself previously, dated June 8, 1837. They resided at one time at 68 Columbia Street (now Heights), Brooklyn NY. In 1848, Daniel & Hannah sell 20 acres in Harrison NY to William Haviland. In 1848 they sell 5 acres in North Castle to David Baker. In 1849 they sell 56 acres in North Castle to Jacob B. Carpenter. Born to them were:
