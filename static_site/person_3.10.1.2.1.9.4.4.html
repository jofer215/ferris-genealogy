NATHAN PECK DARBY

ID: 3.10.1.2.1.9.4.4 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.1.9.4.4) NATHAN PECK DARBY born 1838 Cortland NY and died March 22, 1916 Moravia, NY; married 1st before 1877 at McLean NY, Martha N. Mineah born May 13, 1841 McLean, NY and died there September 11, 1877; married 2d in 1878 in McLean, Mary Ruth Francis born September 23, 1840 Groton, Tomkins, NY and died October 01, 1903 Moravia, Tompkins, NY; married 3d {name unk); all buried McLean Old Cemetery. OBITUARY: Cortland Democrat, March 23, 1916 DEATH OF NATHAN P. DARBY "Word has been received here by relatives of Nathan P. Darby, announcing his death which occurred at his home in Moravia Wednesday morning, at the age of 79 years, death resulting from a shock of paralysis which he sustained on Thursday, the 16th. He is survived by his wife and a number of nieces and nephews and was a cousin of the late Mrs. Harriet Chollar of this village. Mr. Darby spent the greater part of his life near McLean, where he was a prosperous farmer. He had lived in Moravia for 29 years. Private funeral services will be held at the family home in Moravia Friday morning. The body will be brought here for internment in McLean Old Baptist Church Cemetery." From the "History of McLean Baptist Church": “It has been felt that this history should not be closed without a word of appreciation for those who helped to make the worship of the house of God a pleasant Service by the use of instrumental music. As far back as 1854 & 1855 Mr. & Mrs. James Mineah brought their small melodeon in a democrat wagon, if the day was fine, and it was carried upstairs to the singers seats, their daughter Susanna playing for the service. After the services of the day were over, it was taken home again at night. This continued for about two years, then Deacon Amos Hart, Susanna's grandfather, presented a $70.00 melodeon to the church. Miss Susanna Mineah played it until her marriage and was a very regular attendant. She was succeeded by her sister, Martha Mineah, who afterwards was Mrs. Nathan Darby. She played until the time of her death. She was greatly missed. The church felt as if she could not be spared. Sarah Ronk took her place as organist and played as long as her health permitted. These two were beautiful Christian characters, faithful unto death!”
