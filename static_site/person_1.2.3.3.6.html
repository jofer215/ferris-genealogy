TAMAR UNDERHILL

ID: 1.2.3.3.6 | Source: John
(1.2.3.3.6) TAMAR UNDERHILL born March 2, 1763 Westchester Co NY (Sing Sing (Ossining) NY) and died after 1853 (1852 Newburgh, Orange, NY); married April 24, 1786 (14y?) in Cortland Manor, Westchester, NY Peter Sypher (anglicized Cyphers) [son of Peter & Affie (Mebie) Sypher] born August 6, 1772 (April 6, 1762 Westchester Co NY; baptized April 27, 1762 at the Old Dutch Church of Sleepy Hollow – which makes more sense) Phillipse Manor NY and died April 3, 1819 Newburgh NYwithout a will; resided Sing Sing, Mt Pleasant, and Newburgh; Peter was a soldier in the Rev War, serving as a drummer initially and then as a Private with the “rebellious Americans”; he was with the Westchester County Militia commanded by Captain Lambert; he was taken prisoner by the British and held in the Sugar House in NYC, released in a prisoner exchange; and born to them were:

Children (8)
