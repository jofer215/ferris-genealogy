MARGARET SEDGWICK NORTON

ID: 5.1.3.5.1.5.2.2 | Source: James
(5.1.3.5.1.5.2.2) MARGARET SEDGWICK NORTON born July 15, 1895 North Salem NY; she graduated from Wellesley College 1915 at age 19; taught in high schools in New Hampshire and Chicago, then worked in the YWCA in New Jersey from 1919-20; from 1923-24 she taught at St. Mary’s Episcopal Mission School in Shanghai, China where she met Emory Wilson Lane [son of Warren Wilson & Katherine Elizabeth (Prass) Lane] on August 7, 1924 in Salisbury, Litchfield, CT; he was born February 21, 1891 Lafayette, Tippecanoe, IN and died August 10, 1963 Denver CO; he graduated from Purdue, the Cornell where he was awarded the Fuertes Medal, a gold medal awarded to the graduating student in Civil and Environmental Engineering who has maintained the highest degree of scholarship in his or her course of study during four consecutive years; a Civil Engineer specializing in hydraulics; he spent 1920-23 in China working on flood control for Morgan Engineering Co; later was professor of Hydraulics at State U of Iowa; worked on hydraulics projects in various cities included New Orleans, Detroit, Washington, D.C., Baltimore and Denver; from 1929-35, then from 1946 until retirement, he worked for the Bureau of Reclamation in Denver; helped design the Hoover and Grand Coulee Damns; worked for the TVA in Knoxville TN 1942-44; and born to them were:
