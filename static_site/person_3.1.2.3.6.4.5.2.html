CORA FERRIS

ID: 3.1.2.3.6.4.5.2 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.2.3.6.4.5.2) CORA FERRIS born July 1870 Eaton Co MI and died March 15, 1920 Battle Creek, Calhoun, MI; buried there Oak Hill Cemetery; married 1890 William N. Morse who died November 21, 1901 drowned in Muskoka Lake, Ontario, Canada. Excerpt from Cora’s obituary, Battle Creek Newspaper, March 16, 1920: PROMINENT NURSE DIES: Mrs. Cora F. Morse was Assistant Supervisor at Sanitarium Hospital. Mrs. Cora F. Morse, one of the best known of the sanitarium nurses, died last evening at 6 o'clock at the sanitarium hospital, after 6 weeks of great suffering. Death resulted indirectly from influenza, this being followed by a complication of diseases. Mrs. Morse came to the sanitarium first when a girl of 18. Had she lived until July she would have been 50 years old. The greater part of her life has been spent at the Sanitarium. Coming to the institution in the year, 1888, she was married 2 years later, then going with her husband to Muskoka, Canada, to reside on a farm. A few years later her husband was drowned in Muskoka Lake, leaving the wife with 3 small children. In 1905, Mrs. Morse returned to the Sanitarium and took the nurse's course and has been connected ever since with the institution. She has held many positions of responsibility in her work, having been head nurse at some time in nearly every department. Born to Cora and William were:
