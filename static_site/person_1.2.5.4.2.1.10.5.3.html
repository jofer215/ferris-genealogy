LUELLA EVA CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.10.5.3 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.10.5.3) LUELLA EVA CHADEAYNE (Photos) born August 19, 1892 and died December 27, 1967 Ossining NY; buried there Dale Cemetery; was a fashion artist; married September 6, 1933 at Hartford CT, Floyd A. Waterbury [son of Charles & Agnes (Shultis) Waterbury] who died September 25, 1959 North Tarrytown NY; buried Dale Cemetery, Ossining. He was an undertaker in Ossining. No offspring. Louella was a fashion artist and stylist for several New York firms, including Lane Bryant. She was also an officer in the Salvation Army, and an active church member. Louella was very active in the Ossining's Women's Club, and was one of the founders of the Ossining Historical Society. Obit: December 29, 1967 Services will be held tomorrow morning at the Miriam Osborn Memorial Home Chapel in Rye for Mrs. Luella Waterbury Chadeayne whose family resided in the Ossining area for more that 200 years. She died Wednesday at the home. Mrs. Waterbury born August 19, 1892, daughter of the late William F. Chadeayne and Lotta Plamer. The Widow of Floyd A. Waterbury, whom she assisted in his mortuary business here, she was a direct descendent of the French Huguenot Jean Chadeayne who fled religious persecution by escaping to the United States in 1686. In 1775, a son John and his son Daniel purchased a farm on the Pines Bridge Road, which was then a part of Van Cortlandt Manor. Among Mrs. Waterbury's ancestors were John Pine, artist, and the families of Coutant, Underhill, Vail, Secor, Van Cortlandt, Thorn, Rider, Conklin, Young and Palmer. Mrs. Waterbury was a graduate of the Traphagen School, Cooper Union, and served as chief artist for Lane Bryant for whom she designed catalogues and newspaper advertisements. She collaborated in the preparation of "The Chadeayne Family 1681-1964." Interested in community activities, Mrs. Waterbury was a trustee of the Ossining Historical Society and chairman of hostesses for the Society and the Museum. She was a volunteer worker specializing in flower arrangements at Phelps Memorial Hospital, where she was elected an honorary member of the Auxiliary last year. She was a member and served as secretary of the local unit of the Salvation Army. Mrs. Waterbury was hostess of the Ossining Women's Club and had served as chairman and judge of several Art Shows sponsored by the Recreation Commission and Historical Society. She was an active member of the Ossining Council of Social Agencies. She was a member of the Woman's Organization and served as chairman of the Altar Flower Committee of the First Presbyterian Church. Among Mrs. Waterbury's hobbies were ceramics, cooking, and jewelry making. She assisted in craft instructions in the Senior Citizens and worked at flower arrangements at the Osborn Home, where she had been a resident for two years. Floyd Waterbury was from a Westchester county revolutionary war-time family. He owned and operated a funeral home in Ossining, still known as Waterbury and Kelly Funeral Home. It is a beautiful Victorian home on Highland Avenue. Floyd was married to Louella Eva Chadeayne. He reportedly had a grown son from a previous marriage. Mr. Waterbury was an active Mason, and was a prominent businessman in Ossining, NY. Obit: September 28, 1959 Floyd A Waterbury died yesterday afternoon at Phelps Memorial Hospital after an illness of two months. Mr. Waterbury started the Waterbury Funeral Home in 1906, which he ran for 52 years. Last year the funeral firm at 45 S. Highland Ave. became the Waterbury and Kelly Funeral Home. Mr. Waterbury was a partner at the time of his death. Mr. Waterbury born in Woodstock, NY May 30, 1880, the son of Charles and Agnes Shultis Waterbury. He came to Ossining in 1902. Mr. Waterbury was the founder and first president of the Ossining Kiwanis Club. In 1949, he was Lt. Governor of Division 11 of New York District Kiwanis International. He was also a past grand representative of Westchester Commandery 42, Knights Templar, a member of Radium Lodge 844, a member of the Elks, Steamer Company, Yorktown Grange, and the Sons of the American Revolution. Mr. Waterbury belonged to the First Presbyterian Church of Ossining and the Men's Club at the church. Surviving are his wife, Mrs. Luella Chadeayne, a son by a former marriage, Floyd A. Waterbury, Jr. of Scotsville, NY, and one granddaughter, Carolyn Lowe Waterbury. Also surviving is a brother, Dr. Earl C. Waterbury of Newburgh.

No children recorded
