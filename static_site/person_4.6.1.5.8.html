HORATIO LOCKWOOD

ID: 4.6.1.5.8 | Source: Mary
(4.6.1.5.8) HORATIO LOCKWOOD (#695) born September 6, 1779 Ridgefield CT and died November 5, 1853 at age 74 years, 2 months, Pound Ridge NY; buried Pound Ridge Cemetery. Horatio born in Ridgefield, where he mother took refuge after their home was burned by the British. The family soon returned to the homestead, where Horatio living during the rest of his life. He was named after General Horatio Gates, but he never used the name Gates. When he became a man, be succeeded to a portion of the large estate owned by his father, and lived in the house, which was erected upon the site of the one, which was burned by the British. He followed the occupation of a farmer and a dealer in cattle. Horatio represented the town of Pound Ridge in the Board of Supervisors, of Westchester County, during the years, 1820-23 and 1825-39. He also represented Westchester County in the Lower House of the State Legislature during the years 1833-36, 1841-42. In politics he was a Democrat, but being very affable in his manner, and a lover of fun, he was popular with all parties. He acquired a considerable reputation as a practitioner in the courts of the Justices of Peace, a position in those days, which was frequently filled by laymen, and one, which required not such an intricate knowledge of legal lore as of good common sense and an ability to impress a jury. He was called General. On August 10, 1851, while at Ellicottville NY, where he had gone to purchase cattle and horses, he received a stroke of paralysis. He was brought home, but never afterwards engaged in active business. He married March 25, 1808 Bethia Close Lockwood (4.6.1.2.4.1) and born to them were: (4.6.1.5.8.1) MARY LOCKWOOD (#1417) born April 9, 1809 Pound Ridge NY (Ridgefield CT) and died January 13, 1885; married October 31, 1838 Horace Reynolds [son of David & Amy (Smith) Reynolds] born December 21, 1813, North Salem; he went to CA in 1850, never returned.
