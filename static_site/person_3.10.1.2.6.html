ABIJAH PECK

ID: 3.10.1.2.6 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.6) ABIJAH PECK (Photo) born April (3) 23, 1758 Greenwich and died November 12, 1848 Clifton Park, Saratoga, NY; buried there Clifton Park Baptist Church Cemetery; a soldier in the Revolutionary War entering the continental army January 1776; was under arms when the Declaration of Independence was read to the American army, and one of the sentinels on duty when it evacuated the city of New York; served in several campaigns, and was in the battle at White Plains. After the war he resided in North Salem NY, moved to Galway NY where he founded a Baptist Church, thence to Clifton Park in 1794 and established the Clifton Park Center Baptist Church; ordained a baptist minister March 12, 1801 and preached in the Baptist Church for 47 years. He had a fluent diction, a strong, well-balanced mind, and was a man of great influence and extensive usefulness both as a citizen and a Christian minister. He was a weaver by trade and had a weaving business in Galway, then Clifton Park; married 1st November 18, 1784 at North Salem, Mindwell Close [dau of Solomon, Jr and Abigail (Barnum) Close] born March 27, 1763 and died April 4, 1816; married 2d November 1821 widow Lydia Montgomery who died January 22, 1846 age 73y; buried with Abijah.
