CHARLES E. FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.2.4.3.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.2.4.3.1) CHARLES E. FERRIS born May 22, 1855 New Castle, Henry, IN; enjoyed superior educational advantages in youth, and under his father's instruction, acquired a comprehensive store of useful knowledge; 1873 he began work at the tinner's trade, and was thus engaged for two years. He was thus engaged, for two years, as clerk in a drug store at Winchester and 1877, was appointed Deputy Postmaster, under F. M. Way. In this capacity he proved efficient and obliging, winning the regard of the public, and unconsciously gaining popularity for himself. In November 1878, there were thirteen candidates for the position of Postmaster, Charles being the youngest among the number. Under the circumstances, the Representative in Congress, General Browne, felt reluctant to make a selection or recommendation, and it was accordingly decided to submit the matter to the people for adjustment. An election followed, at which Charles received a flattering majority of all the votes cast, receiving thus a well-merited testimonial to his ability and integrity. He was a member of the White River Encampment, #50 at Winchester; also member and officer of the Knights of Pythias, Winchester Lodge #91.

No children recorded
