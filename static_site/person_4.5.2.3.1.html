WILLIAM LOCKWOOD

ID: 4.5.2.3.1 | Source: Mary
(4.5.2.3.1) WILLIAM LOCKWOOD (#633) born August 15, 1753 Greenwich and died February 13, 1837; enlisted at Greenwich in Captain Webb’s Company of Colonel Sullivan’s Regiment in the New York Line; was in the engagement at Flatbush, L. I., and subsequent, in 1780, served in the whale and gunboat service, under Captain Jebez Fitch, of Horse Neck. He was a farmer and moved from CT to Sullivan County NY, shortly after the Rev. War; received a pension; married January 27, 1780 Jemima Lounsbury born June 12, 1760. They lived at Mamakating, Sullivan, NY. Born to them were:
