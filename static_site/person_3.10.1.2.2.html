HEATH PECK

ID: 3.10.1.2.2 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.2) HEATH PECK (1745-1780) married 1774 Rachel Roselle. He was a soldier in the Revolution, but retired from the service in 1780. He was a bold and out spoken Whig and led numerous scouting parties against the Tories after his return. The Tories buried his barn, stole his cattle and offered a reward for him. Nothing daunted, however, he continued to go out at the head of scouting parties and while out with one of them in October, 1780, was shot from the outside through a window and killed. His widow remarried to Ezra Weed and they had a son, Hiram. Born to them were:
