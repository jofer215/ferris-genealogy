CHESTER A. TOMPKINS

ID: 5.1.2.9.4.4.2.2.1.2 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.4.4.2.2.1.2) CHESTER A. TOMPKINS born April 16, 1899 Peekskill NY and died March 12, 1956; married Constance L. Millar. Obituary, Star, Peekskill, NY, Tuesday, March 12, 1956: Chester A. Tompkins, 56, of Cat Rock Road, Garrison, died suddenly of a heart attack about 5:55 yesterday afternoon at the Modern Home Center, Central Avenue where he was employed. When stricken, Mr. Tompkins who had been suffering from a heart ailment, attempted unsuccessfully to put a capsule in his mouth to relieve his condition. Police were notified that a man was unconscious at the Modern Home Center. They in turn notified the Peekskill Fire Patrol. Driver Frank McGinnis responded and used one tank of oxygen and the respirator. Police finally succeeded in locating a physician and Dr. William S. Martens responded. After one half hour Dr. Martens pronounced Mr. Tompkins dead. He born in Peekskill, April 16, 1899, the son of Tilden and Alice Hills Tompkins. He was a veteran of World War I. He married Constance Millar and was employed as a salesman by the Modern Home Center. Surviving are his wife, Constance L. Millar; and a son, Robert C., of Reseda, Calif., and one sister, Mrs. Joseph (Hilda) Sullivan of Garrison. Interment will be in St. Philips Cemetery. Born to them was:

Children (1)
