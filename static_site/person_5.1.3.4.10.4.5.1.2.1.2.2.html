RYAN BAUMAN

ID: 5.1.3.4.10.4.5.1.2.1.2.2 | Source: James
(5.1.3.4.10.4.5.1.2.1.2.2) RYAN BAUMAN born June 12, 1989 (5.1.3.4.10.4.5.1.2.2) RICHARD WILLIAM PEARSON born July 29, 1934 Sterling IL; married June 9, 1957 in Sterling IL, Frances H. Rhode born June 24, 1936 Sterling IL. In Richard’s words: “We lived south of Rock Falls, Illinois, then at the age of 10 we moved to Tampico, Illinois on the Pearson Farm, which was owned by our Grandparents Victor E. and Martha Pearson. Our grandparents moved to Shore Acres in Rock Falls, Illinois then to a new home in Prophetstown, Illinois. I went to school at Tampico, helped my father Kenneth farm. We farmed until I went into the Army in March 1957. I was at Fort Leonard Wood MO for basic training. Then I married Francis Rhode, then after basic training to Fort Lee, VA for 8 weeks, in which she went with me. After 8 weeks of training I received notice of being transferred to Verdun, France. I brought Francis home, then was shipped to Fort Dix, New Jersey for several days. Flew overseas to France, for 18 months. Did some traveling while there. My first son born at home on April12, 1958. I got out of service on March 1959. Came home to my family and farmed with my father again. We had a home built and lived next door to my folks. Our second son, Gary born November 5, 1960. We raised a lot of hogs, farrowed them to finish. After both of my parent’s death, I continued to raise hogs. In 1988, I got rid of all my hogs and sold the machinery and rented the land out to my neighbor. I worked at Alto Irrigation for 3 ½ years and then went to work in 1992 for Egan Brothers at Harmon, Illinois. Frances and I have 4 grandchildren and enjoy doing things with them.” (2001). Frances has always been a very hard worker. Before she was married, she worked in the offices of the Northwestern Steel and Wire Company in Sterling/Rock Falls IL. After her children grew up she worked at the restaurant in Tampico. Born to them were:
