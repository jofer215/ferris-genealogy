WILLIAM DARIUS BISHOP

ID: 5.1.3.4.3.1.2 | Source: James
(5.1.3.4.3.1.2) WILLIAM DARIUS BISHOP born September 14, 1827 Bloomfield, Essex, NJ and died February 4, 1904; interred Mountain Grove Cemetery (Photo), Bridgeport CT. After he graduated from Yale 1849, he soon became a director of the Naugatuck Railroad; he was the first superintendent of this road and then in 1855 was chosen to its presidency. He was a Democratic U. S. Representative from Connecticut, 1857-59, the 35th Congress; and when he failed to get reelected, was appointed United States commissioner of patents by President Buchanan. During his brief service as commissioner (1859-60), he is said to have done good service, especially in putting in order the Patent Office records and in systematizing the work of his subordinates. In May 1866, he left the Presidency of the Naugatuck to take up that of the New York and New Haven. Under his regime, the New York & New Haven acquired both the Hartford & Connecticut Valley and the Harlem & Port Chester, and leased the Shore Line. With this consolidation, the road became increasingly powerful. During William's administration, the New York & New Haven system gained an ascendancy it never lost. While he was executive head of this system, he was admitted to the Fairfield County Bar, 1870; represented Bridgeport in the General Assembly, 1872; state senator, 1866, 1877, 1878; and had a conspicuous part in general railway legislature. He made extensive and thoroughgoing improvements in local railroad conveniences at Bridgeport. He was a director of the Bridgeport Steamboat Company, operating between Bridgeport and New York; also of the Housatonic Railroad, extending from Bridgeport to Pittsfield MA. In March 1879 he retired from the presidency of the New York & New Haven, but until his death he remained on the board of directors. In 1883, he was once more called to be president of the Naugatuck and held this office until his retirement in October 1903. He married, in 1850, Julia A. Tomlinson of Bridgeport. Born to them was (and possibly more): (5.1.3.4.3.1.2.1) HENRY ALFRED BISHOP born December 4, 1860 Bridgeport CT and died October 22, 1934; entered Yale with the class of 1884, but did not graduate; was general ticket agent 1885-86; purchasing agent 1883-85; assistant superintendent 1885-86 of the Naugatuck railroad; served as superintendent, Housatonic Road and general superintendent same road and its branches; purchasing agent, NY/NH/N railroad 1887-1902; acting vice-president and vice-president, WV Central and Pittsburg and Western Maryland roads 1902-03 (resigned); vice-president, Clapp Fire Resistant Paint Company; on executive committee of 1st National Bank, Pacific Iron Works, Inc.; president board of Police Commissioners, Bridgeport 1888-90; democratic candidate for Secretary of State, Connecticut 1888 and for Lieutenant Governor 1904. Henry was president, Bridgeport Public Library, Bridgeport Boy's Club, director Bridgeport Hospital, St. Vincent's Hospital, Connecticut Humane Society; and trustee of Bridgeport Orphan Asylum. He was an Episcopalian and a Mason. He married February 6, 1883 Jessie Alvord Trubee [dau of William Edgar & Susan Curtiss (Alvord) Trubee] (1864-); and born to them were:

No children recorded
