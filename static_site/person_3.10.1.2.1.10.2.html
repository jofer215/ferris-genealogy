NATHAN NORTHRUP NASH

ID: 3.10.1.2.1.10.2 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.1.10.2) NATHAN NORTHRUP NASH born April 2, 1808 Sherburne, Chenango, NY and died July 11, 1854 Elizabeth, JoDaviess, IL; buried Plum River Cemetery, Stockton, JoDaviess, IL (Photo); married October 4, 1834 at Smyrna, Chenango, NY as her 1st husband, Betsey Hollenbeck Williams [dau of Seth & Mary (Stover) Williams Jr] born 1815 Granby, Oswego, NY and died 1890 JoDaviess Co IL; also buried Plum River Cemetery. Nathan injured his arm while helping to erect a cotton gin and it had to be amputated; Betsey held it while they sawed it off. In 1845, Nathan and his family, using the canal system and the Great Lakes, moved to northwestern Illinois. With one arm, he cleared his wooded land, consisting of 200 acres, and then farmed it. He built a frame house, a story and a half, 16x22 feet, which was quite a dwelling for a pioneer. He was a practical, progressive farmer, and in the years of hard labor that followed he placed much of his land, 80 acres of which were in timber, under excellent tillage. He was a man of sincere and honest convictions, and earnestly strove to lead a godly and upright life. In his earlier years he was a follower of the Whig Party, but on the formation of the Republican Party he joined its ranks and held allegiance to it until the day of his death. Nathan and Betsey were devoted members of the Methodist Episcopal Church and he was Class Leader and Steward. Born to them were:
