SAMUEL OSGOOD PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.1.5.1.6 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.1.5.1.6) SAMUEL OSGOOD PELL born July 3, 1875, Montclair NJ and died August 3, 1913 Long Beach, L.I; a real estate broker in NY and an enthusiastic hunter and autoist, and shortly before his death became much interested in aeronautics. He was well known in New York and Newport society, and was a member of many clubs. He met his death in a distressing grade crossing accident on Long Island. He and Mrs. Pell and friends were returning from a dinner party given at the Nassau Hotel, Long Beach, by William K. Vanderbilt, Jr.. The automobile in, which he was riding was struck by an electric train, and Samuel and his chauffeur were instantly killed. Mrs. Pell, who was with a party of friends in a car behind, escaped injury.
