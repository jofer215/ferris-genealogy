CHARLES GOADSBY FERRIS

ID: 1.4.1.8.1 | Source: John
(1.4.1.8.1) CHARLES GOADSBY FERRIS (HONORABLE) born at "The Homestead", Throgs Neck, Bronx, NY, c1796 and died June 4, 1848 NYC; graduated from Columbia College, B.A., 1811 (15 years old?), M.A., 1816; studied law and was admitted to the bar and practiced in New York City; married Catherine Youngs born 1794 and died August 4, 1848. He was the Alderman of the 4th Ward, New York City, 1832-3; member of the 23d and 27th Congresses of the United States; elected as a Jacksonian tothe 23d Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Dudley Selden and served from December 1, 1834 to March 3, 1835; elected as Democrat to the 27th Congress (March 4, 1841 - March 3, 1843). Charles introduced Napoleon III to President Tyler and the family story is family members frequently dined with Napoleon at Charles's house on Broadway, opposite West Washington Place, since pulled down. Morse, the inventor of telegraphy, stated at a banquet at Delmonico's that Charles enabled him to make telegraphy a success by getting an appropriation from Congress for the first line between Baltimore and Washington, and without his great aid and influence telegraphy might not have been in use to this day.
