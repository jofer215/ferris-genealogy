JOHN RICHARD MAGNER

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.10.5.1.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.10.5.1.1) JOHN RICHARD MAGNER (Photos) born September 4, 1919 Darien CT; married July 5, 1942 in Ossining, Margeret Esther Doyle [dau of Joseph T. & Ethel (Neville) Doyle] born March 31, 1920 Endicott NY and died November 29, 2000 in FL; buried Ossining NY. “Esther” was raised in Ossining; attended St. Ann Catholic Church and was on the yearbook staff at Ossining High School, graduating with the Class of 1938. She loved dancing as a child and was taught by an original Zeigfield Follies’ girl. Esther loved sports and was a wonderful homemaker and mother. She was a devout Catholic and active supporter of her faith. John was baptized at the First Baptist Church of Ossining; lived in Ossining most of his early life, for a brief time he lived in Portchester and White Plains, NY. He graduated Ossining HS in 1937. During high school, he ran track, was on the rifle team, glee club and drama club. John Richard was a member of " the Micks", an Irish-American social club. Richard, as he was commonly known, enlisted in the Army in April 1941. After becoming a staff sergeant, he attended Officer's Candidate School in Camp Lee, VA. Upon graduation of OCS, Richard was commissioned as a second lieutenant, was promoted to 1st lieutenant and served in England with the Quartermaster Corp. He received Battle stars for the battle of the Ardennes, Normandy beach and the final Germany campaign. After being awarded the Bronze Star, he was discharged in November 1945. "Dick" built a home for his family at 23 First Avenue, Ossining. He went to work for Borden's Inc., starting as a clerk and retiring in 1983 as a corporate vice president after 38 years with the company. Dick presently lives in Valrico, Florida.
