(3.1.6.2.7) KEZIAH SHERMAN was baptized January 22, 1769 at Christ Church, Salem NY and died January 1848 Seneca Co NY; buried Interlaken Cemetery; married Gideon Scott Be it remembered that heretofore, to with on the fourth day of April 1848, Abraham BLOOMER and Joseph DUNLAP the executors named in the Last Will and testament of Kezia SCOTT, late of Ovid in the County of Seneca deceased, appeared in open court before the Surrogate of the County of Seneca and made application to have the said Last Will and testament, which relates to personal estate proved; and on such application the Surrogate did ascertain by satisfactory evidence as far as the said executors could ascertain, who were the heirs and next of kin of the said testator and their residences. And said Surrogate did thereupon issue a citation in (___) form of law, directed to the said heirs and next of kin, by their respective names as fast as could be ascertained, stating their respective residences as far as the same could be ascertained requiring them to appear at the office of Said Surrogate in the Village of Waterloo in the county of Seneca on the 29th day of May then (next?), at ten O'clock in the forenoon of that day and alter it the probate of said Will and afterwards to wit, on the day & year & at the place last aforesaid, (satisfy?) (___) evidence by affidavits was produced and presented to said Surrogate of the Service and publication of Said citation in the moke prescribed by law and on that day, no one appearing to oppose the proof of said Will, such proceedings were thereupon had, that the said Surrogate took the proof of said Will herein after set forth on the day last aforesaid, and be adjudged the said will to be a valid will of personal Estate, and the proofs thereof to be sufficient; and that said last Will and Testament and the proofs and examinations taken in respect to the same be recorded which said Last will and testament and proofs are as follows that is to say; In the name of God Amen, I, Keziah SCOTT of the town of Ovid in the County of Seneca, being mindful of my mortality, do this twentyforth day of September in the year of our Lord 1840 make and publish this my Last Will and testament in manner following: First, I desire to be decently buried in the burying grounds by the side of my husband in the town of Ovid. Also, I give and bequeath unto Abraham BLOOMER and Joseph DUNLAP, Deacons of the Baptist Church in the town of Ovid the sum of five hundred dollars to be held by them, and to be by them (_?_) the interest to be paid annually to the Baptist minister that shall preach to said church for his support, or if they shall think propper (sic) to purchase a house and Lot for a parsonage for the use of said minister and if there should be no such minister or church, then I desire the yearly interest to be paid to the treasury of the American and foreign Bible Society. Also, I give and bequeath to my executors the sum of fifty dollars in trust to pay (over?) the sum in six months, after my decease to the person who, when the same shall be payable shall act (as?) measures of the American and foreign Bible Society formed in Philadelphia in the year Eighteen and thirty seven to be applied to the charitable uses and purposes of the said society and under its direction. Also, I give and bequeath to my Executors the sum of eighty dollars, five dollars in trust to pay (over?) the sum in six months after my decease to the Baptist Education Society of the State of New York. Also, I give and bequeath to my executors the sum of eighty-five dollars in trust to pay (over?) the sum in six months after my decease to the treasurer of the Baptist Missionary Union to be applyed (sic) to uses of said Society. Also, I give and bequeath to my Executor Executors the sum of fifty dollars in trust to pay (over) the sum in six months after my decease unto the person who may act as treasurer of Baptist trust and publication Society in Philadelphia. Also, I give and bequeath unto Leonard FERRIS, son of my sister, Phebe FERRIS, the sum of twenty five dollars to be paid as above. Also I give and bequeath unto Margaret SMITH daughter of Elihu GROVES and Nancy GROVES the sum of ten dollars to be paid as above. Also I give and bequeath Isaac CLARK son of Daniel CLARK the sum of one hundred dollars to be paid as above. Also, I give and bequeath unto Sarah SCOTT wife of Daniel SCOTT, and Betsey HODGE wife of Edward, all the rest and residue of my personal Estate whatsoever and wheresoever of what nature, kind and quality (___) the same may be, and not herein before given and disposed of, after payment of my debts, legacies, and funeral expenses. And I do hereby constitute and appoint Abraham BLOOMER and Joseph DUNLAP my executors of this, my last Will and testament. In witness whereof I have hereunto set my hand and seal, the day and year first above written. Kezia SCOTT {L.S.} The above instrument consisting of one sheet was now here subscribed by Keziah SCOTT the testator in the presence of each of us and was at the same time declared by her to be her last will and testament, and we at her request sign our names hereto as attesting witnesses Daniel SCOTT Ovid Seneca County D.D. SCOTT Ovid Seneca County N.Y. Seneca County for Daniel SCOTT being duly sworn & (exercised?) by (law?) for the Surrogate of Seneca County testifies as follows: I reside in Ovid, Seneca County. I was well acquainted with Kezia SCOTT now deceased, late of Ovid aforesaid. I was present as a witness and saw the said Kezia subscribe her name at the end of the instrument in writing now (Produced?) shown to me, bearing date the twenty fourth (24) day of September A.D. 1840 purporting to be the Last Will and T4estament of the said Kezia. The said Kezia SCOTT at the time of making the said subscription declared said instrument to be her Last Will & Testament and requested me to sign my name as witness thereto which I accordingly did as my name now appears, at the end of the memorandum which immediately follows said instrument at the time of the execution of said instrument by said Kezia as aforesaid. She was of the age of Seventy-eight years or thereabouts, of sound mind, memory - in all respects competent to dispose of real & personal property by will, and was under no restraint to my knowledge. She was then, and at the time of her death, which occurred on or about the 19th day of January 1848 an unmarried female. I saw David D. SCOTT the other attesting witness to said instrument sign his name as it now appears, at the end of said memorandum as such witness, in the presence of said Kezia at her request. The said Kezia SCITT resided with me at, and for several years previous to her death; no many days after her death I found the said instrument in a trunk in which the said Kezia kept her papers. I handed the same to Joseph DUNLAP, one of the executors named therein. No alteration was made in said instrument while the same remained in my possession. Sworn before me the 29th day of May 1848 Daniel SCOTT J.K. RICHARDSON, Surrogate Seneca County David D. SCOTT being duly sworn and examined by & before the Surrogate of Seneca County, testified as follows: I reside in Ovid, Seneca County, I was well acquainted with Kezia SCOTT, now deceased, late of Ovid aforesaid. I was present as a witness and saw the said Kezia SCOTT subscribe her name at the end of the instrument in writing now provided and shown to me, bearing date the twenty-fourth (24th) day of September, (1846) one thousand Eight hundred and forty six, purporting to the Last Will & Testament of the said Kezia SCOTT deceased. Said Kezia at the time of making said Subscription as aforesaid, declared said instrument to be her last Will and testament & requested me to sign my name as a witness thereto, which I accordingly did as my name now appears at the end of the memorandum which immediately succeeds said instrument. The said Kezia SCOTT, at the time she so executed said instrument, and at the time of her death which occurred on or about the 19th day of January 1848 was unmarried; at the time of such execution thereof, she was at the age of Seventy-Eight years or thereabouts; was of sound mind, memory, in all respects competent to devise real estate & to dispose of personal property by will, and she was under no restraint to my knowledge. I saw Daniel SCOTT the other attesting witness to said instrument, sign his name as a witness thereto as it now appears at the end of said memorandum, this was done by him in the presence of the said Kezia SCOTT, and at her request. Sworn before me the 29th day of May 1848 J.K. RICHARDSON, Surrogate} David D. SCOTT Seneca County by Joseph DUNLAP of Ovid in said county being duly sworn & examined by before the surrogate of Seneca County, testified as follows: I drew the instrument in writing now produced & shown to me, bearing date the 24th day of September, 1846 & purporting to be the Last Will & testament of Kezia SCOTT. I did so at her request. I read the same to her & she approved of it. I was present when the same was executed by her; remained in her possession. After her death, I received said instrument from the hands of Daniel SCOTT, with whom said Kezia resided at the time of her death. Said instrument is now in the same condition unaltered, as the same way in, which so executed. Sworn before me the 29th day of May 1848, J.K. RICHARDSON, Surrogate} Joseph DUNLAP Count of Seneca: Recorded the proceeding Last Will and testament of Kezia SCOTT deceased as a will of personal estate, together with the proof taken in the court of the Surrogate of the County of Seneca, relating to the said Last Will & testament which records hereby signed and certified by me, presented to the provisions of the Statutes, this twenty ninth day of May, one thousand Eight Hundred and forty-eight. J.K. RICHARDSON Surrogate
