LILLIE E. HAYMAKER

ID: 5.1.3.1.7.2.6.1.3 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.7.2.6.1.3) LILLIE E. HAYMAKER born September 25, 1866 Franklin, Portage, OH; like her sisters, she attended the school in Earlville; and then college at Akron, 3 years, with 1 year’s previous attendance at the high school of the city; married March 11, 1891 at her father's home in Franklin, Herbert A. Swan (Bert) born January 20, 1856 Kent OH (January 20, 1866 Everett, Summit, OH). Herbert's father was a farmer, and his son has always followed the same occupation. When about a year old his father moved onto a farm in the southwest part of Streetsboro, where he lived some 5 years; then moved into Kent, where he resided a few years; then moved out onto a farm he had purchased, about a mile from town, which was his home until his marriage. He was educated, principally, in the schools of Kent OH. After their marriage they moved onto a farm of 90 acres, one-half of which he eventually owned. He farmed the place with the intention of purchasing the other half.

No children recorded
