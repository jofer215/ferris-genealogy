ARCHIBALD TANNER BANNING, JR.,

ID: 4.5.3.1.2.3.6.10.3 | Source: Mary
(4.5.3.1.2.3.6.10.3) ARCHIBALD TANNER BANNING, JR., (Photos) born May 10, 1884 (at Mt. Vernon NY) New York City and died December 10, 1965 Mt Vernon NY; married in Duluth MN October 9, 1914, Margaret Frances Culkin [dau of William Edgar & Johanna Alice (Young) Culkin] born March 18, 1891 Buffalo MN and died January 4, 1982 Tryon NC - divorced in 1929 (1930) because of his alcoholism; she married second Leroy Salsich. He received his AB degree from Cornell U., in 1904 and his LLB from George Washington U., in 1908. Margaret was a best-selling author and early advocate of women’s rights. She spent one year at Sacred Heart Academy in Rochester NY; AB, Vassar College, 1912; Certificate, Chicago School of Civics and Philanthropy, 1913. Russell Sage Foundation fellow in research; she was active politics; delegate to one National Republican Convention and an alternative to another. Her first literary works were short stories and essays published in magazines. Her first novel was This Marrying, published in 1920’ her last was Such Interesting People, published in 1979. She authored 36 novels and more than 400 essays and short stories, usually concerned with problems of youth, women, and social change. She was a Vassar College trustee, 193644; Duluth Public Library trustee; first woman admitted to the Duluth Hall of Fame. She kept her Banning name on her writings. Following WWII, she worked in refugee and displaced persons camps in Austria and Germany. Born to them were:
