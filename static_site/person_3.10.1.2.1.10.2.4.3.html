WILLIAM AMBROSE BUSS

ID: 3.10.1.2.1.10.2.4.3 | Source: Joseph
(3.10.1.2.1.10.2.4.3) WILLIAM AMBROSE BUSS born February 3, 1873, Bear Grove, Guthrie County IA and died November 23, 1943, Monmouth OR. Bear Grove was a hamlet in Bear Grove Township and there was a Post Office there, 1854-1910. He married February 6, 1894 Pearl Emma Dow born September 25, 1875, Stockton IL and died November 28, 1956, Salem OR; buried Monmouth OR. Pearl was a member of the local Evangelical United Brethren Church and also a member of the Ladies Aid. She had a never-ending supply of sugar cookies shaped like rabbits, the best part of which was the red cinnamon candy eye. William was a farmer in the Stockton IL area and was the Superintendent of the Yankee Hollow Sunday School and the Treasurer was his bride-to-be. He moved his family to Morris MN, 1918, where he worked in a grocery store and was a farmer. He and Pearl moved to Monmouth OR, where their daughter Myrtle lived, around 1935, and ran a boarding house for 9-10 boys who attended college there. Born to them were:
