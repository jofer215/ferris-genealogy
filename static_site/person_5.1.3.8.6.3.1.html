JOSEPH SELLECK FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.8.6.3.1 | Source: James
(5.1.3.8.6.3.1) JOSEPH SELLECK FERRIS born March 29, 1853 Stamford CT and died February 24, 1947 Syracuse; buried DeWitt Cemetery; moved with his parents to Dewitt NY 1855; married March 16, 1881 at DeWitt, Charlotte Jane Miller [dau of Joseph Young & Mary Jane (Lain) Miller] born August 18, 1858 DeWitt and died January 3, 1943 Syracuse; buried with husband; 1889 Joseph bought a six-acre strip of land extending from E. Genesee Street to Salt Springs Road near the old Syracuse city line. In 1898, he had a road built, Ferris Avenue, to connect the two streets and cut the property into building lots. The 100+-year-old Cape Cod house on this land, one of the first frame houses built in Onondaga County, where all but the three oldest children were born, was torn down in 1902 after a new house had been built back of it. It was the family house until 1947. Brought up to be a farmer, in 1900 he took up salesmanship, selling school supplies until 1915 when he helped start the Farmers and Traders Life Insurance Company.
