THOMAS FERRIS COCK, M.D.,

ID: 1.4.7.8.4 | Source: John
(1.4.7.8.4) THOMAS FERRIS COCK, M.D., born July 1, 1819, NYC and died June 10/11, 1896 at Cold Spring Harbon, L. I., NY; married 1st May 17, 1842 at St. George’s Church, NYC, Ann Augusta Wood [dau of Isaac & Ann (Morrell) Wood] born September 28, 1820 NYC and died there April 7, 1863; and 2d widow Louisa (DeForrest) Woodruff. Thomas was a Friend in early life but after marriage became a member of the Protestant Episcopal Church. He was the first graduate of the Haverford School (after Haverford College) in 1836, and received the degree of L.L.D from that institution in 1886. He studied medicine in the offices of Dr. Alexander H. Stevens, a prominent NY surgeon, and Dr. George B. Wood of Philadelphia, and graduated from the University of Pennsylvania, 1839. M.D. He was a practicing physician, first with his father, consulting physician to New York Hospital, to the Women’s Hospital, the Women’s Infirmary and to the Lying-in Asylum, and a trustee of the NY Hospital. He was connected with the Lying-in Asylum on Marion Street, originated largely by his mother, for 55 years, and with the NY Hospital for over 50 years. Born to Thomas and Ann were:
