WILBUR CHRISTIAN

ID: 5.1.2.9.4.9.2.1.3 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.4.9.2.1.3) WILBUR CHRISTIAN born March 29, 1910 and died November 8, 1911 (5.1.2.9.4.9.3) MARGARET MELISSA CHRISTIAN born c1854 and survived her husband; married February 2, 1876 at the old Canopus M.E. Church, Lafayette Post [son of Levi & Sarah (Barrett)] born September 1854 and died November 7, 1936; buried at the family plot near Tompkins Corners. Although much of Lafayette’s life was given over farming, not all of it was spent at that occupation for he was employed in a clay pottery factory in Poughkeepsie for eight years and afterwards resided in Peekskill. They were of Oscawana Heights when he died. Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Thursday, Jan. 9, 1941: A number from this place attended the funeral-service at Peekskill on Monday, Dec. 30, for Mrs. Melissa Post, a former resident of this vicinity. She was the widow of the late Lafayette Post. Since his death a few years ago, she had lived with her daughter, Mrs. Edward Todd of Rochester. Mrs. Post had been in failing health for several months and she was in her 87th year. She was a highly respected citizen and friends and relatives in this place will miss her coming down to visit them. Survivors are daughters, Mrs. Edward Todd and Mrs. Eugene Dutcher, both of Rochester, and one son, Dudley W. Post, of Cranston, R.I. and six grandchildren and two great grandchildren, besides several nieces and nephews. Born to them were:
