CHARLES LUMAN KNAPP

ID: 2.6.7.4.7.1.1 | Source: Peter
(2.6.7.4.7.1.1) CHARLES LUMAN KNAPP (Hon.) born July 4, 1847 near Harrisburg, Lewis, NY and died January 3, 1929 Lowville, Lewis, NY; buried Rural Cemetery, Lowville NY; attended the rural schools, Lowville Academy, and Irving Institute, Tarrytown NY; graduated from Rutgers College, 1869; studied law and was admitted to the bar 1873 and commenced practice in Lowville; served in the State Senate 1886-87; appointed by President Harrison as General Consul at Montreal, Canada, 1889 and served until 1893, when he returned to Lowville and resumed the practice of law; also engaged in banking; elected as a Republican to the 57th Congress to fill the vacancy by the death of Albert D. Shaw; reelected to the 58th and to the 3 succeeding Congresses and served from November 5, 1901 to March 3, 1911; married June 26, 1887 Sarah Dorrance born in Florence, Oneida, NY and died c1909 Lowville NY

No children recorded
