SAMUEL FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3 | Source: James
(5.1.3) SAMUEL FERRIS (8RK9-G8) (Samuel Holmes Ferris) born September 21, 1705/6 Greenwich CT and died there April 25, 1786; age 81, buried Tomac Road Cemetery, Old Greenwich (tombstone photos); commissioned as an Ensign of the train band of Greenwich 1731; served as a Corporal in the French-Indian War in Captain White's Company of Stanwich, 9th Regiment; the company marched to the relief of Fort William Henry August 1757; he also served as a Sergeant in Captain George Peck's Company of Greenwich during the Rev. War. [It has been questioned that this Samuel was the Samuel who served in Captain Peck's company during the Rev. War, because of his age - -this is a valid point, considering that Samuel Ferris (5.1.3.2) is also listed as serving as a Sergeant in Captain Peck's Company, but... will leave here for now, pending further research.]; married Ann Lockwood (#150) [Robert, Gershom, Gershom, Jr.] born c1713 Greenwich and died there July 2, 1789 age 76y; and born to them were:
