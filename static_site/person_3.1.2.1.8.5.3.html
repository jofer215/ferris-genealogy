GILES HUBBARD

ID: 3.1.2.1.8.5.3 | Source: Joseph
(3.1.2.1.8.5.3) GILES HUBBARD born January 26, 1817 and died November 6, 1876 Mt. Clemens, Macomb, MI; he served in the cooper’s trade until 1829, when he visited his uncle, Daniel Davis. After passing one year there, he traveled through NY and OH, working at different points. In the summer of 1837, he made a journey south, and on account of the yellow fever epidemic, turned his steps northward, and arrived at Mount Clemens in the fall of that year. He read law in the office of Judge Thurston, with whom he entered in partnership for one year. He formed a partnership with R. P. Eldredge, which continued for 15y. Subsequently, he formed a partnership with Thomas M Crocker, which was observed for several years. He was appointed Postmaster at Mt Clemens under Harrison in 1840; elected prosecuting attorney; elected Senator and refused the appointment of Collector of Internal Revenue for the district; also nominated for Congress; married September 23, 1842 at Mt. Clemens, Euphemia Bingham [dau of Homer & Mary (Atwood) Bingham]; both buried Clinton Grove Cemetery, Clinton Township, Macomb Co MI (Photo); and born to them were:
