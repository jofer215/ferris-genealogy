LEANDER FISHER CHADEAYNE

ID: 1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.2.1 | Source: John
(1.2.5.4.2.1.4.3.2.1) LEANDER FISHER CHADEAYNE (Photos) born November 3, 1898 Ossining NY and died there March 31, 1988; buried there Dale Cemetery; married January 22, 1933 Marie Elizabeth Jessen [dau of Carl Ingward & Anna (Hurni) Jessen] born 1894 Elmira NY and died December 11, 1936 White Plains NY; buried Dale Cemetery. Marie was educated in the public schools of Elmira, NY and was graduated from Elmira College. She studied nursing at the Presbyterian Hospital, NYC from which she was graduated on March 1, 1918. In 1919 and 1920 she worked with the American Red Cross in Siberia, establishing and directing operating rooms. Upon her return to the United States she studied law and was admitted to the bar in Ohio as a practicing attorney. Returning to the medical field she specialized in anesthesia and became an anesthetist in the Cleveland Clinic under Dr. Crile, goiter specialist. Prior to her marriage she was chief anesthetist at the Henry Ford Hospital, Detroit, MI. where she anesthetized the original Henry Ford when he had surgery there. (Obituary from article in the New York Times, December 12, 1936): Mrs. L. F. Chadeayne Dies in Hospital, 44 Lawyer and Former Red Cross Worker Victim of Pneumonia in White Plains Server in Russia in 1919 Aided in Establishing Modern Operating Rooms - Once Aide in Henry Ford Hospital White Plains, NY Dec 12 Mrs. Marie F. Chadeayne, former Red Cross worker and attorney, died of pneumonia shortly before midnight last night at White Plains Hospital. She was 44 years old. She born in Elmira, attended Elmira College and became a trained nurse after studying at the Presbyterian Hospital, New York. In 1919 she was sent to Russia by the Red Cross and for two years assisted the Soviet Union in opening hospitals and establishing modern operating rooms with anesthesia units. Later she was head of the anesthesia department of the Henry Ford Hospital in Detroit. She studied law and on being admitted to the bar began practicing in Cleveland. Leander (Lee) attended public schools in Ossining, NY where he born. He entered Hamilton College in 1917 where joined Sigma Phi, was elected to DT and managed the varsity baseball team in his junior year. He also served in the Students Army Training Corps. Following his graduation in 1921, Leander traveled through Europe with college friends, an adventure fondly remembered all his life. Upon his return, he entered the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration and in 1923 acquired his MBA degree. His early employment was in advertising, initially with the radio Corporation of America in New York City. When RCA combined with Victor Phonograph and moved to Camden, NJ, he decided to remain in Manhattan and took a new job as advertising manager for Sonora Phonograph Co. The year was 1929, and by 1931 the Depression had forced the company into receivership. His next position was with an advertising firm in Detroit that failed when Michigan banks closed in 1933. It was then that Lee Chadeayne resolved to seek employment with a solid and well-established firm, Metropolitan Life. It marked the beginning of an association that continued for almost 30 years until his retirement. While in Detroit, Leander net Marie Jessen. They were married in New York City. Their son, Lee, was barely three years old when his mother contracted pneumonia and died. Leander remained a widower until 1943 when he married Augusta Kleindienst. She became the mother of his second son, Philip. Attached to Metropolitan Life's field organization in White Plains, NY until 1942, Leander was then transferred to the home office of the firm's ordinary insurance department in Manhattan. He retired in 1962 and thereafter gave more of his time to the Ossining community. Formerly chairman of the board of the First National bank of Ossining and a director of the First Westchester National bank in New Rochelle, NY, he served for more than 20 years as president of the Dale Cemetery Association and was a past vice president of the Ossining Historical Society and an elder of the First Presbyterian Church. He was also a devoted Hamiltonian and assisted the College as a class agent and as an editor of Class Notes for the magazine. Until their health began to fail, Leander and Augusta enjoyed traveling to the West Indies and spent numerous vacations on the islands dotting the Caribbean.
