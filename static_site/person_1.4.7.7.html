ANNA FERRIS

ID: 1.4.7.7 | Source: John
(1.4.7.7) ANNA FERRIS (Photo) born December 8, 1771 Westchester (Throggs Neck] and died November 5, 1854 Newburgh NY; married, May 7, 1794 at Flushing Meeting (Quaker ceremony), Valentine Seaman, M.D. [son of Willett & Mary (Searing) Seaman] [or 4th son of Samuel] born (March) April (March) 2, 1770 North Hempstead, L.I., NY and died June 27 (July 3), 1817 NY. Both Anna and Valentine were of the Society of Friends. Received his M.D. from University of Philadelphia, 1792; studied medicine with Dr. Romayne, and was a surgeon of the New York Hospital from 1796 to his death. They made their home on Beekman Street across from the Old Brick Church. It was a large home surrounded with gardens and vineyards. It was here they lost their first child Betsy to smallpox. This tragedy induced Valentine to visit Dr. Edward Jenner in England, the result of which brought Valentine home with the serum and a close friend for life. His son became the first white child vaccinated in this country. Controversey was raised over the use of the vaccine and almost cost him his medical career. After much debate and effort on the part of Valentine, he was finally given permission to vaccinate his own family and volunteers from the poor house. He was conspicuous in the introduction of vaccination in New York. He was a prominent physician and surgeon of NYC; President of the Medical Society which was the forerunner of the New York College of Physicians and Surgeons. Dr. Seaman was the first to have a class of women to instruct in the care of midwifery, writing a book for their instruction and guidance; this was at the Alms House in New York. He also published an account of yellow fever, which prevailed in 1791 and 1800; this may yet be found in the Medical Repository. Besides being the first analyst of the waters of Saratoga and Balston Springs, he wrote an exhaustive treatise on the suject. In 1810-11 he and others formed a medical institution associated with Queens College in New Brunswick NJ. He was an active member of the Manumission Society for the liberation of slaves and their protection. He published Inaugural Address on Opium, Philadelphia, 1792; Midwife’s Monitor, 1800; On Vaccination, 1816; and a pharmacopia. Valentine’s health began to decline about a year before his death from consumption in 1817. Dr. Seaman’s portrait hangs proudly in the boardroom of New York City Hospital where he was their first operating surgeon. Valentine’s made his will August 22, 1815 in New York City. “I, Valentine Seaman, of the city of New York, physician, do make and publish this, my last will and testament in manner and form following. That is to say: first I order and direct, that my funeral expenses and all my just debts be paid and my dues collected as soon after my death as can conveniently be done. Secondly, I authorize and empower my executor hereinafter named, and the survivors and survivors of them, at their discretion, to sell and dispose, in fee simple absolutely of all such of my real estate as I now hold in common with my brothers and sisters, John Seaman, Willett Seaman, Benjamin B. Seaman, Mary Thurston and Rachael Seaman, and with any or either of them; to give good and sufficient conveyances in the law for the same; to compromise and compound with any and all my debtors; and to refer to arbitration any and all disputes and controversies, that shall or may arise in the settlement of my estate. Thirdly, I order and direct that the proceeds of my personal estate together with the avails of such part of my real estate as may be sold by virtue of the authority above given, and the rents and profits of the residue thereof, as the same shall be received, be put out and kept at interest on good real security and that out of such interest and income my beloved wife Anna Seaman be supported; and my children, William F. Seaman, James V. Seaman, John F. Seaman, Valentine Seaman, Percival, Willett Seaman, Marianna Seaman, Anna Seaman, and Elizabeth Seaman, respectively, be supported and educated until they shall respectively at the age of twenty-one years. Fourthly, it is my will and I hereby direct that as my children above named shall respectively arrive at the age of twenty-one years that they each be paid out of my estate or from such income and interest as aforesaid, be from time to time put out and kept at interest such security as aforesaid, until the youngest of my surviving children shall arrive at the age of twenty-one years, and shall have received his or legacy as aforesaid. And that then what shall remain of all my estate real and personal be divided among my said wife, if she shall then be living, and such of my said children as shall then be living, and the lawful issue, if any there shall be, of such of my said children as shall then have deceased, [equally to be divided among said issue if these shall then be more than one] share and share alike; and when so divided to be and remain to them, their heir and assigns forever; hereby meaning and -----------, that the issue of any such of my said children, as shall have died previous to the time of such general distribution, shall receive the share or proportion thereof, that his, her or their parents would have received, if living, -----------; also, I declare my intention to be the before mentioned appropriations for the support of my said wife, shall be in heir and in bar of her dower, right of title of dower, and of all other claimed upon my estate. And I further declare my will and intention to be, that the above mentioned legacy of five thousand dollars to be paid to my children respectively, as they shall arrive at the age of twenty-one years, shall not be paid to the issue of anyone of my children who shall have died under the age of twenty-one years; but that only such of my said children shall attain to that age, shall respectively at such age be authorized out of my estate the said sum of five thousand dollars, previous to the general division and distribution of the above mentioned. And lastly, I hereby nominate ---- and appoint my eldest son, William F. Seaman, and my brother-in-law, William B. Thurston and my kinsman, Samuel Hicks of the city of New York, merchant Executors of this my last will and testament. In witness thereof I have there unto set my hand and seal this 22 day of the sixth month in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and fifteen. Valentine Seaman” Anna made her will February 9, 1850 in Newburg, Orange County, New York. “In the name of God, Amen: I, Anna Seaman, of the town of Newburg, Orange County, the state of New York (widow of the late Dr. Valentine Seaman of the City of New York) being of sound mind and disposing memory for which I am sincerely thankful to a merciful and almighty God, do make, publish, and declare this my last will and testament in a manner following that is to say – I direct that all must debts and funeral expenses be paid, among which debts is a bond and mortgage for two thousand eight hundred dollars dated on or about the first day of May 1847 and held by Stephen Bedford and Cyrus H. Browne, executors of the last will and testament of Peter Van Nute. Second, I give and bequeath to my daughter-in-law, Anna Amelia Seaman, wife of my son, Valentine Seaman, all the livestock, farming utensils, household and kitchen furniture, beds, bedding, wearing apparel, silver plate and all other articles of personal property that I may die possessed of belonging to the premises of which I now reside. And I do further give and bequeath to my said daughter-in-law, the sum of ten thousand dollars to be paid to her by my executors here in after named out of that first money they shall receive from the proceeds of my estate to be sold by them as here and after mentioned all of which bequeathed are for her sole use and benefit. Third, I hereby authorize and direct my executors to sell and dispose of all my real estate at public or private at such time or times and upon such terms and in such manner as to them shall seem meet and to give good deeds of conveyance therefore and further to collect all debts due and owing to me at the time of my decease – to see and transfer all shares of stock held by me in any company and companies and generally sell and dispose of all my property not here before specially bequeathed as soon after my death as the same can be done for the interest of the estate and divide the proceeds thereof after paying the ten thousand dollars herein before bequeathed and my just debts and funeral expenses, between my five sons James V., John F., Valentine, Percival, Willett Jun., and my two daughters Anna, wife of Charleton Ferris and Elizabeth S., wife of Augustus W. Leggett, share and share alike which said shares are hereby bequeathed to my said children as aforesaid and to their heir and assigns forever. Lastly, I hereby nominate and appoint Lindly M. Ferris of Montgomery, Orange County, New York and William F. Faring of Westchester County, New York Executors of this my last will and testament hereby revoking all former wills made by me. In witness whereof I have hereby set my hand and seal this ninth day of February one thousand eight hundred fifty.” Born to them were:
