ALEXANDER ARMSTRONG, JR.,

ID: 5.1.2.9.6.2.1 | Source: James
(5.1.2.9.6.2.1) ALEXANDER ARMSTRONG, JR., born June 2, 1833 Putnam Valley and died there July 13, 1856; buried there Adams Corners Cemetery (5.1.2.9.6.2.2) MARY ELIZABETH ARMSTRONG born October 8, 1836 Putnam Valley and died July 30, 1925 at the home of her daughter, Emma, at Canopus, near Oscawana Lake; died the same house and room where she born; married November 16, 1864 (1861) George Fox Barmore [son of Mead & Jane (Curry)] born August 7, 1841 and died January 14, 1917 at the home of his son, G. Frank, at Van Courtlandtville; both buried Hillside Cemetery, Peekskill. Mary was a member of the Peekskill Hollow Methodist Church and until failing health prevented was an active worker in the Ladies Aid. She was known far and wide for helpfulness and unfailing good nature. George’s parents were Quakers and he was named for a noted preacher of that faith. He attended the country school for a short time, a private school and the Peekskill Military Academy. At the age of 17, he began teaching school during the winter and working on the farm during the summer. After marriage he went West and followed agricultural pursuits, while in winter he taught school for a time in DeKalb County IL – 8 years later he returned East, purchased the Armstrong homestead and resided there until the March previous to his death. George was a charter member of the Putnam County Grange and was its master for several terms. He was a regular attendant at Quaker meetings in Yorktown. He was a successful farmer, honest to a degree, and was held in high esteem by all with whom he came in contact. George was a vocal minister and for a time conducted a class in singing. He was an independent in politics with Republican leanings.
