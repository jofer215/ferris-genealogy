(5.1.2.9.4.4.2.1.9) MARY SMITH Obituary, Putnam Courier, Sept. 7, 1983: Mrs. Mary Nelson, 84, of Belden Rd., Carmel, widow of Jay C. Nelson, died Sept. 1 in Putnam Hospital Center, Carmel, after a brief illness. She born Sept. 2, 1898, in Kent, daughter of the late Orlando and Sarah Bennett Smith. She had lived in Carmel 52 years and was a member of the Mt. Carmel Baptist Church in Carmel. She is survived by 2 sons: Earl C. Nelson and Harold Nelson both of Carmel; 5 grandchildren and 1 greatgrandchild. Burial was in Raymond Hill Cemetery. Obituary, Putnam Courier, Carmel, NY, Thursday, Nov. 19, 1953: Jay Charles Nelson, well known truckman and contractor of Carmel, died suddenly of a heart attack shortly after midnight, Wednesday, Nov. 18, 1953, at his home on Belden road. He had just returned from bowling with Skipper's team of which he was a member. His age was 62. Mr. Nelson was a son of Charles and Myra Pratt Nelson, and born in Hartford, NY on May 31, 1891. He came to Carmel about 25 years ago and had since been engaged in the trucking and contracting business. He has also for several years been employed by the town of Carmel highway department to clear roads of snow in the Carmel village area. Mr. Nelson was an enthusiastic bowler and had enjoyed that sport since the alleys at Mahopac were opened. He was a member of the Wildcats team for several years and more recently had been on Skipper's team. He was united in marriage 26 years ago with Mary Smith of Mahopac, who survives with two sons, Earl, who is in the armed services in Korea, and Harold, in the U.S. Navy at Little Creek, Va. He also leaves two sisters, Mrs. Erwin Wendell of Fishkill and Mrs. Frank Acome of Schenectady; and two brothers, William of Cross River, and Leon of Granville, NY. Interment will be made in the family plot in the Raymond Hill cemetery. Born to them were:
