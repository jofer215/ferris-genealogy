HENRY LOCKWOOD, the youngest child of Gideon Weed Lockwood,

ID: 5.1.4.1.5.1.12 | Source: James
(5.1.4.1.5.1.12) HENRY LOCKWOOD, the youngest child of Gideon Weed Lockwood, born in North Stamford, March 22, 1843 and died there December 6, 1925; buried Woodlawn Cemetery. He was educated in the district schools and spent his early years, up to eighteen, on a farm. He then went into a country store near his home as a clerk, remaining there two and one-half years. He then went to Stamford in February 1866, as a clerk in Seth W. Scofield's hardware business, which vocation he followed for fifteen years, when he purchased his employer's interest, continuing in the same store. He became one of the largest hardware dealers in that section of the state. In addition to the general line of hardware, he also carried carriages and wagons of all kinds, harness, and all kinds of agricultural implements. The firm was Lockwood & Palmer, the latter having been a clerk for a number of years for Mr. Lockwood and admitted to partnership in 1897. Henry was purely a self-made man, having started at the bottom of the ladder and rose to his prominent place by strict attention to business.
