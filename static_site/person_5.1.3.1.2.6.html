ABRAHAM FERRIS

ID: 5.1.3.1.2.6 | Source: James
(5.1.3.1.2.6) ABRAHAM FERRIS born June 15, 1786 (June 16, 1787) Hillsdale, Columbia, NY and died August 13/19, 1850 New London OH (Ruggles Township, Ashland Co OH); married Marinda (Merinda) Phillips born November 25, 1795 and died September 17, 1850, Ruggles Township, Ashland Co OH. He served a term in the War of 1812. Abraham, from Milo, Yates County NY, came to Ruggles (OH) in 1824. The journey was made as far as Buffalo by wagon, thence by water to Sandusky. They embarked on the schooner, Union, Captain Johnson, Commanding, and were driven by a storm to Detroit, returning to Sandusky after the storm had abated, where they arrived the last of October, after being on the water for three weeks, reaching New London the second on the second day of November, 1824, and Ruggles on the first day of December following; built a log house in Ruggles on Lot 17, Section, which he purchased from Messrs. Jessup and Wakeman. Abraham was one of a dozen residents who voted in 1826 to detach Ruggles from New London; he was appointed fence viewer; and then elected Treasurer. Born to them were:
