SARAH HYATT

ID: 1.2.3.7.2 | Source: John
(1.2.3.7.2) SARAH HYATT born December 30, 1741 White Plains NY and died March 10, 1835 Pleasant Valley, Dutchess, NY; buried Netherwood Baptist Churchyard, Salt Point NY; married David Frost born June 3, 1743 Oyster Bay, Long Island NY (?) and died May 5, 1826 Pleasant Valley, Dutchess, NY [son of John & Phebe (Le Telier) Frost]; buried with Sarah. He was a blacksmith who learned his trade at Cortland Manor from his stepfather Thomas Thorne. David lived in Carmel, Putnam, NY where he had a blacksmith shop on the Croton River and was a Private in the 7th Regiment Dutchess County NY (LtCol Reuben Ferriss) and in Mead’s Company during the Revolutionary War. David’s half-brothers were Tories and fled to western Long Island. He was listed on the tax lists of Fredericksburg NY, 1777. On June 20, 1783, he purchased a farm of 168 acres from the confiscated estate of Roger Morris. Born to them were:
