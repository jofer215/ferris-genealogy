RICHARD FERRIS

ID: 1.6.1.2 | Source: John
(1.6.1.2) RICHARD FERRIS born April 11, 1762 Scarsdale, Westchester, NY and lived there until 1810; and died October 19, 1846 Spencer NY where he was buried at the Garrit Cemetery. He lived in Spencer as of September 4, 1832; married [before January 1, 1784] December 15, 1783 Catherine Lent [dau of Hercules (Harculus) & Lavinia (Winchey( (Van Tastel)] born November/December 10, 1763 Courtlandt Manor, Westchester, NY and died April 11, 1854/5 Spencer NY; buried in Forest Home Cemetery, Waverly NY [her tombstone lists her age as 91y 4m 1d]. In the pension application of a Richard Ferris of the town of Spencer NY, he stated that he served in the Revolutionary War under his brother, Lieutenant David Ferris and that David was the eldest son of his father; that they lived in Scarsdale NY during the War where his father served on the Committee of Safety, Chairman for Scarsdale, and that his father's barn was burned by the British, that his livestock stolen and money and property taken by the British. He served from the fall of 1776 to November 1783 in short periods (patrolled every 4th night between 1778-83) equivalent to two years, with rank of Private; pension file is W25574. His Captain was Ebenezer Boyd, his Colonel was Samuel Drake, New York 3d Regiment of Westchester County Militia. Born to Richard and Catherine were:
