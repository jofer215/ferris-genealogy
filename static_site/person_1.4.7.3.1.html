WILLIAM FERRIS PELL

ID: 1.4.7.3.1 | Source: John
(1.4.7.3.1) WILLIAM FERRIS PELL (Photo) born September 1779 Westchester, at the Grove Farm, and died (October 28) November 28, 1840 NYC; the founder of the famous action/auction firm of Pell & Co., of New York City; was much impressed with the land around Lake Champlain and bought land near the Fort Ticonderoga remains in 1818; in 1825 his house was burned along with manuscripts from Pelham Manor; he then built “The Pavilion” close to the fort and the home served as a summer retreat for the Pell’s until the 1840s; and his descendants restored it (Fort Ticonderoga) and it was still in the family in 1930s; married November 17 (11), 1802 in Clinton Corners NY, Mary Shipley [dau of Morris & Ann] born December 22, 1783 London, England and died October 3, 1848.
